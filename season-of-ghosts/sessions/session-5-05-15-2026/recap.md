## Session Summary

Mo's first session with the party arrives in the wake of a violation that has no rules for it: Mingxi the Magus and Dot the Druid simply no longer existed. Not killed, not taken — *erased*. Willowshore moves through its days as if it has always been three party members short. Only Ming Bao, Jun-Yeong, and the animist Kageyuri still remember them, and even Kageyuri's adoptive mother Eliza is starting to forget. Ming Bao, who watched a red star detonate over the village on the first night of this whole disaster, spends the morning sliding down a slow existential ramp — *maybe none of us noticed, maybe I died that day, maybe this is just the trip* — while Jun-Yeong patches him together with Pharasma's sober rituals and offers to drink in honor of the lost. Mo, finally meeting the party he watched relight the Eternal Lantern from a Willowshore rooftop, slots in as the local-kid newcomer who proves the rule: he has never met Mingxi or Dot, and he knows every face in this town.

The party rides east into the Specterwood to investigate Fan Hongrui's three-year-old death site, hoping for a lead on the governor. They get something else. After a clean two-encounter fight against thatchlings — vegetation-trapped souls denied reincarnation — and a phantom wolf, they find the inn ruin and the singing within. Fan Hongrui's spirit is still here, still seventeen, still circling the dream of a crimson butterfly. Jun-Yeong takes point in Aklo, plays his daegeum, and the party buries the four travelers' bodies in a proper grave. Hongrui passes on, leaves them her **gratitude** in cloak form, and gives them the name that has been whispering in every spirit's mind since the first day of summer: **Kugaptee**. Before they can process it, two new undead — eyeless, broken-bladed, bleeding red butterflies from their ribcages — emerge from the tree line. The party stealths away.

They return to a Willowshore that has been hit again. Kum Sun-Chung's ranch is smoldering ash — magically set ablaze, with a crimson butterfly carved into the surviving fence. Eliza arrives in a panic: **Kageyuri is missing**. Witnesses describe a "half-Tengu, half-human man" leading Kageyuri west — a description that fits Mingxi exactly. Ming Bao tracks them on a nat 20 Survival check to the abandoned **Lumber Camp**, now occupied by a noppera-bo cult of Kugaptee with cells of captured Willowshore farmers. Mo's first real scout work pays out: he maps the camp room by room, identifies the two meditating occultists chanting "Kugaptee" telepathically over a thatchling-effigy, finds a secret door, spots a second pair of occultists lounging in an adjacent room, and ambushes the chanting pair with Ming Bao and Jun-Yeong. The fight is short and decisive — Mo's Crushing-rune crit batters the first occultist, Ming Bao's Crushing-rune crit batters the second, and **Jun-Yeong delivers both killing blows** (eunjangdo stab, then a needle darts cleanup). In the adjoining room they free **Kum Meng-Sung**, the stable-master's kidnapped son — the stable fire was a distraction. As they prepare to extract him, **Shinzo** appears in his usual on-cue, possibly-too-on-cue way, gifts them a noppera-bo hood for infiltration, takes Kum Meng-Sung home, and remarks in passing that Mingxi and Dot "are still of this world, even if their spirits may have been erased." Session ends on a second ambush — Mo stealth-stabs one of the second-pair occultists through the throat for an instant kill, the party scrums the survivor down to 1 HP, and Ming Bao Flurries the kill before catching the body mid-fall to keep the camp's stealth intact.

## Key Events

### Act I — The Erasure (Willowshore, morning)

- **Cold open**: Ming Bao opens the session with *"Haunted by ghosts of people who were never there."* Jun-Yeong follows with the framing — only he, Ming Bao, and Kageyuri remember Mingxi and Dot. Everyone else in Willowshore looks at them blankly.
- **Reintroductions at the Cerulean Tea House**: The DM resets the table for Mo's arrival. Jun-Yeong introduces himself as a Boneyard sorcerer two years gone from his own life. Ming Bao gives the deadpan monk pitch ("Mostly I punch people, get hit in the face, and now I can patch up some of your wounds"). Mo describes himself in third person as a Wayang street kid who survived the invasion by hiding.
- **Yami the cat**: A white cat with night-glowing eyes is in the tea house. Mo fails a Nature check trying to pet it (re-rolls with a hero point to a 10). The cat warms to Jun-Yeong, telepathically introduces itself as **Yami**, asks Jun-Yeong to keep the talking-cat thing secret, and gifts the party a **Lesser Talisman Cord** from a hidden crate.
- **Ming Bao's spiral**: As the morning conversation circles back to Mingxi and Dot, Ming Bao asks *"What if we just didn't notice? What if none of us noticed?"* Later, riding toward the Specterwood, he goes further: *"Maybe I died that day. Maybe... maybe this is just the trip. Maybe that's why nothing makes sense."* Jun-Yeong crits a Boneyard Lore check and gently rejects the hypothesis — *"I feel strongly that we are not dead. At least not presently."*
- **Jun-Yeong's offer**: *"After we return from the forest, if you would like to have a drink in the honor of Mingxi and Dot, I would be happy to join you in that. An important part of the cycle is mourning."*
- **Kageyuri at Eliza's**: The party meets Kageyuri (animist, pale skin, violet hair, fetchling-coded) before they leave town. She also remembers Mingxi and Dot. They part ways at Ugly Cute's shrine — Kageyuri tells them she's not coming.
- **Walking, not riding**: Granny Hu's five horses are now three. Ming Bao volunteers to walk; Mo walks with him to keep him company. *"There's a lot more mental focus on walking."*

### Act II — Hongrui's Requiem (the Canary Inn, Specterwood)

- **Fog approach**: The party enters the Specterwood under thickening fog. Mo scouts on Stealth, Ming Bao on Scout (+1 init to all), Jun-Yeong runs Detect Magic and his unjangdo glows — undead within 100 ft.
- **Jun-Yeong's rules briefing**: *"Don't call a ghost a ghost. Address them with friendly greetings if you must. Do not pat people on the head or shoulders. Avoid bodies of water when a ghost is nearby. Don't eat food that has two stick-like objects protruding from it."* — posted to chat for the record.
- **Combat 1**: Thatchlings (multiple) ambush from the underbrush; a Phantom Wolf joins from the tree line. See *Combat Encounters* below.
- **Loot pickup and Mo's intro**: After combat, Ming Bao tries to Treat Wounds — succeeds on Mo (11 HP), fails on himself. The party divides the loot from "the other characters" Mingxi and Dot left behind: Mo gets a **Crushing rune** for his rapier, the **Predictable Silver Piece**, and frost vials traded from Jun-Yeong. Ming Bao puts a Crushing rune on his Handwraps of Mighty Blows. Mingxi's cold-iron katana is kept untouched as a memorial.
- **Mo and Jun-Yeong's first real conversation**: Mo asks who Sashim was. Jun-Yeong explains — his Jeoseung Saja (Korean reaper-of-souls) guardian, gone since the same monastery incident that took two years from Jun-Yeong's life. They make plans for Jun-Yeong to teach Mo anatomy ("considering it would be helpful to know where to strike") in exchange for a Willowshore tour.
- **The cursed cloak**: At the inn ruins they find an old red cloak. Mo's Perception (with a Foundry low-reroll auto from 9 → 21) identifies the **curse trait** — meant to punish grave robbers. They don't put it on. Jun-Yeong takes it into his pack, intending to learn Remove Curse later and bury it properly in Willowshore as a memento.
- **The young woman singing**: Inside the inn, a teenage girl sings to four thatchlings about the crimson butterfly. Mo's quick Perception pegs her as undead. Jun-Yeong takes point. Diplomacy 24 (with Ming Bao Aid 16) — success. Her form shifts: she is a thatchling with red blood on its branches. She is **Fan Hongrui**.
- **Hongrui's story confirmed**: The governor's guards barred the inn doors. She set the first fire to flush them, but couldn't repeat the trick. She and her three companions burned. She names the three thatchlings around her: Chi Hong Shin, Na Shi Hong Ban, Misuki Ban Yong.
- **The requiem**: Jun-Yeong rolls Undead Lore (+1 from his Nosoi Skull) — they need to bury the bodies properly. Ming Bao and Mo dig four graves; Jun-Yeong plays his daegeum (Performance 17, success after Eliza-style "epic raves" stylistic choice). Hongrui's parting words confirm the **Kugaptee** thread:
  > *"In my dreams, every time I wake up in this place, I see that crimson butterfly... There is a voice that ruminates in every spirit's mind in this place. It started on the first day of summer. One calling itself Kugaptee."*
- **Hongrui's Gratitude**: Before fading, she manifests a cloak from her hand — *"a token of my gratitude, but be aware. There is a great evil lurking on the outskirts of this hell at this very moment."* Jun-Yeong wears the cloak (his outfit is now blue-black-red and "drippy").
- **The undead in the fog**: Rubble shifts. Two unknown undead emerge from the tree line — elongated bodies, oversized arms, no eyes, broken weapons held together by crimson energy, red butterflies pouring from their open ribcages. Jun-Yeong's Undead Lore + Religion checks both succeed but yield no recognition — this is a new type of undead.
- **Aklo eavesdrop**: Jun-Yeong remembers (worryingly) that he speaks Aklo. He hears the two creatures discuss the missing thatchlings — *"They should be there, ready to feast on any souls that come this way, but... I did sense living beings."* They split up to search. The party sneaks south and successfully evades.

### Act III — The Crisis at Home (Willowshore, evening)

- **Ugly Cute's news**: Returning to Willowshore in the late afternoon, Ugly Cute greets them and mentions a fire to the west.
- **Kum Sun-Chung's ranch**: The party arrives at smoldering ruins. The stable master is rounding up livestock with help from the guard. Jun-Yeong Detects Magic — an ignition spell. **Magic-induced fire.** The list of people in town who could do it is short (Eliza, Jubay, Granny Hu — none would). The second clue arrives when Kum Sun-Chung sighs at a carved fence panel: **a crimson butterfly**, carved into the wood. The motif is in town now.
- **Mo helps, sort of**: Ming Bao tries crafting repairs (success); Jun-Yeong holds the nails ("I am a seven-foot-tall bookworm. I don't know how to repair a fence"); Mo tries to round up scared livestock — his intimidating glare makes them flee deeper into the forest. Mo voices the working theory: *"If they can cast that kind of spell, surely a simple fire spell is not out of the possibility."* The noppera-bo cult is sabotaging Willowshore proper now.
- **Eliza arrives**: *"Have any of you seen Kageyuri?"* She hasn't returned since morning. Disturbingly, Eliza begins to falter mid-sentence about Kageyuri's friends — even *she* is starting to forget Mingxi. The erasure is spreading.
- **The search**: Diplomacy/Society checks in downtown find witnesses who saw Kageyuri leave with a "half-Tengu, half-human man" — Mingxi's description. Headed west.
- **Survival nat 20** (Ming Bao) tracks two pairs of footsteps to the abandoned **Lumber Camp**, which is no longer abandoned.
- **The scouting run**: Mo, with Jun-Yeong's Guidance, scouts the camp room by room over the next several minutes. He maps it: four noppera-bo grunts around a campfire (telepathic meditation), two occultists in a side longhouse chanting *"Kugaptee, Kugaptee"* over a misshapen wooden effigy of a four-armed twig-headed figure (almost certainly a thatchling totem), four guards holding ~5 farmer captives in a separate building. He finds a **secret door** in the fence for extraction. No sign of Kageyuri yet.
- **Combat 2**: The party ambushes the two occultists. See *Combat Encounters* below.
- **Kum Meng-Sung freed**: In an adjoining room, gagged on the floor, a 17-year-old boy in a rice-farmer's hat — **Kum Meng-Sung**, son of Kum Sun-Chung. The stable fire was bait to mask his kidnapping. Ming Bao asks him to bleed (verifying he's not a noppera-bo). He bleeds red.
- **Shinzo appears**: The mysterious merchant arrives at exactly the moment the party needs him, cart in tow, a crow on his shoulder. He gives them a **noppera-bo hood** "on the house" — a flawless disguise. He confirms he remembers Mingxi and Dot — *"Mingxi had quite the coin purse on him after all"* (laugh that doesn't reach the eyes) — and offers to escort Kum Meng-Sung home. His parting line:
  > *"Mingxi and Dot are still of this world, even if, well, their spirits may have been erased."*
- **Cliffhanger**: With the hood, a plan to take the second pair of occultists' clothes for disguise infiltration, and Kum Meng-Sung safely escorted out by Shinzo, the party doubles back to the second longhouse — the one Mo had peeked into earlier with the two *lounging* occultists. Mo Stealth-walks through the door undetected and Overwhelming-Combinations the first occultist with a rapier crit for **35 damage** *before* initiative — an instant kill on a target that never got to act. The second occultist gets a real round: Ming Bao charges, Jun-Yeong needle darts, Mo Demoralize 20 (Frightened 1) + a whiffed Overwhelming Combination → enemy at 1 HP and about to step outside to alert the camp. Ming Bao flurries the kill and *catches the body* before it hits the floor. Combat fades to black. Session ends.

Overwhelming Combination: rapier auto-hit on the off-guard target (9 piercing), fist hero-point-rerolled hit (7 bludgeoning — DM specifically clarified "not a crit, just a hit"). ## Combat Encounters

### Encounter 1 — Thatchlings + Phantom Wolf (Specterwood, ~49:20-73:00)

**Setup**: Mo's passive seek (Sneak exploration activity) hears the giggling first. He sees two visible thatchlings — woven sticks bound with Pharasma-blue thread — and bumps into an invisible spirit (the Phantom Wolf, hidden). A third thatchling joins later. Initiative.

**Init order**: Mo Stealth 26 (Surprise Attack — all enemies off-guard round 1) → Thatchling 1 → Jun-Yeong → Ming Bao (with Scout +1 init shared) → Phantom Wolf → Thatchling 2.

**Enemy roster**: 2 visible thatchlings + 1 hidden Phantom Wolf. A third thatchling re-engages later (the "last thatchling" that walks into Jun-Yeong's emanation in Round 3).

**Round 1**
- **Mo** (3 actions): Stride to flank → **Overwhelming Combination** on Thatchling 1: rapier auto-hit (off-guard from Surprise Attack) for **9 piercing**; fist Strike at MAP misses, **hero-point reroll → hit** (DM explicitly clarified *"not a crit, just a hit"*) for **7 bludgeoning**. Combined **16 raw**, halved to **8 damage** by T1's bludgeoning resistance (*"like punching solid wood"*). Third action: **Dance of the Mousedeer** on T1, Performance 30 crit success → **greater cover** (+4 AC, +4 Stealth) until Mo moves/attacks.
- **Thatchling 1**: Frond on Mo — **hit by 1 (rolled 18 on die) for 7 damage to Mo** (the +4 cover bonus from Dance does apply, but the hit was hot enough to land anyway). Second Frond on Mo — **crit miss**. Stride away from Mo.
- **Jun-Yeong**: Stride toward Ming Bao → **Recall Knowledge (Boneyard Lore) crit success** — learns weak to fire/slashing, lowest save Fortitude. Shares the lore dump in chat. Casts **Guidance** on Ming Bao for next turn.
- **Ming Bao**: Free action — switches to **Tiger Stance** (slashing, T1 is weak) → Stride to flank → **Flurry of Blows** on T1: crit + hit, **14 slashing combined** (resistance applies once). T1 bloodied. In-character roar (RP only, NOT a Demoralize action): *"I've already killed your kind, I know your weight and measure!"*
- **Phantom Wolf** reveals: Stride + Strike (jaws) on Ming Bao — first miss, second **hit ("gnaws ankle") for 8 damage to Ming Bao** (number not stated aloud, inferred from end-state HP).
- **Thatchling 2**: Focus-spell forces a Will save on Ming Bao — **Ming Bao saves** (only off-guard until end of T2's turn). T2's follow-up Strike on Ming Bao misses.

**Round 2**
- **Phantom Wolf**: Charges from the tree line. First attack miss, second attack — **claw scratch on Jun-Yeong for 3 damage** (back-applied at the table when the DM realized Jun-Yeong was still at max HP).
- **Mo**: Stride to flank T1 → **Demoralize T1 — fails (12)**. *"His usual intimidation doesn't work very well — I guess it's because these are creatures that are kind of... not very emotional."* Rapier Strike on T1: **CRITICAL HIT (rolled 30)** for **25 damage** with Sneak Attack 1d6 + Deadly d8 — *"most of this damage just immediately pierces through... it doesn't matter as it immediately falls."* T1 destroyed. **Unbalancing Blow** self-procced for the next turn anyway.
- **Thatchling 2**: Two Strikes on Jun-Yeong — **crit miss + miss**, both dodged.
- **Jun-Yeong**: Casts **Sepulchral Mask** focus spell (10-ft emanation, DC 19 Will). Phantom Wolf fails save → **4 mental** (1d4 + Sorcerous Potency +2). Thatchling 2 succeeds → **2 mental** (half). **Psychopomp blood magic** adds **+2 vitality** to each undead creature in the area (manually applied at the table).
- **Ming Bao** flanks the Wolf with Mo → **Flurry of Blows** (Tiger Claw): crit + hit, plus **1d4 persistent bleed** from Tiger Stance. Wolf is bloodied to near-zero (not dead yet — damage number not stated). Final action: Raise a Shield.

**Round 3**
- **Phantom Wolf**: Casts a **Bay/Fear-type focus effect on Ming Bao** (DC 14 Will). Ming Bao saves on RP grounds (*"man with a mission"*).
- **Mo**: Stride to flank Wolf → Demoralize — **fails (13)** → **Overwhelming Combination** on Wolf (flanked, off-guard): rapier hit (17) for 10 dmg + fist hit at MAP (14) for 11 dmg = **21 raw → 17 damage dealt** (Phantom Wolf has some physical resistance, applied once). **Wolf killed by Mo.**
- **Thatchling 2** (no self-preservation): walks back into Jun-Yeong's Sepulchral Mask emanation, triggers an immediate Will save → **fails** → **22 mental damage** from the sustain (likely heightened or with the vitality weakness multiplier applied) — destroyed. Jun-Yeong sustained the spell.

**Damage taken**: Mo 7 (Frond hit R1). Ming Bao 8 (Wolf jaws R1). Jun-Yeong 3 (Wolf claw R2).
**Killing blows**: Thatchling 1 = Mo (R2 rapier crit). Phantom Wolf = Mo (R3 dual-Strike for 21). Thatchling 2 = Jun-Yeong (R3 Sepulchral Mask sustain for 22).
**Treat Wounds after**: Ming Bao heals Mo +11 (full); Ming Bao fails on himself (DC roll 10); later, **Ming Bao successfully Treats Jun-Yeong for +8** (Jun-Yeong has no Medicine training and plans Battle Medicine at L4).

### Encounter 2 — The Two Chanting Occultists (Lumber Camp, ~194:30-205:30)

**Setup**: Mo and Ming Bao open the door of the candle-lit longhouse. Two noppera-bo occultists are meditating cross-legged around a misshapen wooden effigy of Kugaptee, telepathically chanting its name. Noppera-bo have no vision but precise hearing — they're off-guard to silent entry. Mo's stealth roll holds; the occultists are off-guard for round 1 only.

**Init order** (effective): Ming Bao drops into init via reaction stance → Jun-Yeong → Occultist B → Mo → Occultist A. (Round 2: Ming Bao → Jun-Yeong → combat ends before Mo's slot.)
