Session Summary

Mo's first session with the party arrives in the wake of a violation that has no rules for it: Mingxi the Magus and Dot the Druid simply no longer existed. Not killed, not taken — erased. Willowshore moves through its days as if it has always been three party members short. Only Ming Bao, Jun-Yeong, and the animist Kageyuri still remember them, and even Kageyuri's adoptive mother Eliza is starting to forget. Ming Bao, who watched a red star detonate over the village on the first night of this whole disaster, spends the morning sliding down a slow existential ramp — maybe none of us noticed, maybe I died that day, maybe this is just the trip — while Jun-Yeong patches him together with Pharasma's sober rituals and offers to drink in honor of the lost. Mo, finally meeting the party he watched relight the Eternal Lantern from a Willowshore rooftop, slots in as the local-kid newcomer who proves the rule: he has never met Mingxi or Dot, and he knows every face in this town.

The party rides east into the Specterwood to investigate Fan Hongrui's three-year-old death site, hoping for a lead on the governor. They get something else. After a clean two-encounter fight against thatchlings — vegetation-trapped souls denied reincarnation — and a phantom wolf, they find the inn ruin and the singing within. Hongrui's spirit is still here, still seventeen, still circling the dream of a crimson butterfly. Jun-Yeong takes point in Aklo, plays his daegeum, and the party buries the four travelers' bodies in a proper grave. Hongrui passes on, leaves them her gratitude in cloak form, and gives them the name that has been whispering in every spirit's mind since the first day of summer: Kugaptee. Before they can process it, two new undead — eyeless, broken-bladed, bleeding red butterflies from their ribcages — emerge from the tree line. The party stealths away.

They return to a Willowshore that has been hit again. Kum Sun-Chung's ranch is smoldering ash — magically set ablaze, with a crimson butterfly carved into the surviving fence. Eliza arrives in a panic: Kageyuri is missing, last seen leaving with a "half-Tengu, half-human man" who fits Mingxi exactly. Ming Bao tracks them on a nat 20 Survival check to the abandoned Lumber Camp, now occupied by a noppera-bo cult of Kugaptee with cells of captured Willowshore farmers. Mo's first real scout work pays out: he maps the camp room by room, identifies two meditating occultists chanting "Kugaptee" telepathically over a thatchling-effigy, spots a second pair of occultists lounging in an adjacent room, finds a secret door. The party ambushes the chanting pair — Mo's Crushing-rune crit batters the first, Ming Bao's Crushing-rune crit batters the second, and Jun-Yeong delivers both killing blows (eunjangdo to the chest, then a needle darts cleanup). In the next room they free Kum Meng-Sung, the stable master's kidnapped son — the fire was a distraction. As they prep to extract him, Shinzo appears in his on-cue, possibly-too-on-cue way, gifts them a noppera-bo hood, takes the boy home, and remarks in passing that Mingxi and Dot "are still of this world, even if their spirits may have been erased." Session ends on a second ambush: Mo stealth-stabs one of the lounging occultists clean through the throat for an instant kill, the party scrums the survivor down to 1 HP, and Ming Bao Flurries the kill before catching the body mid-fall to keep the camp's stealth intact.

Timeline

Act I · Roleplay · The Erasure

Haunted by Ghosts of People Who Were Never There

  • Cold open: Ming Bao — "Haunted by ghosts of people who were never there." Jun-Yeong sets the frame: only he, Ming Bao, and the animist Kageyuri remember Mingxi and Dot.
  • Reintroductions at the Cerulean Tea House: Jun-Yeong introduces himself as a Boneyard sorcerer two years gone from his own life. Ming Bao gives the deadpan monk pitch: "Mostly I punch people, get hit in the face, and now I can patch up some of your wounds." Mo describes himself in third person — Wayang street kid raised by Willowshore.
  • Yami the tea-house cat: White cat with night-glowing eyes. Mo's Nature check to pet her: failure (with a hero-point reroll to 10). She telepathically chooses Jun-Yeong, asks him to keep her secret, gifts the party a Lesser Talisman Cord from a hidden crate. Eyes are glowing in a well-lit room; Jun-Yeong's anti-undead unjangdo also lights up nearby. Definitely not just a cat.
  • Ming Bao's spiral begins: As the morning conversation circles back to Mingxi and Dot, Ming Bao asks "Wait, are there other people who are missing? What if none of us noticed?" — Kageyuri goes wide-eyed in real time.
  • Mo confirms the erasure: "I swear, I never met them before, and usually I'm pretty good at keeping track of new people coming into that town." The newcomer's "I don't know them" is the proof.
  • Jun-Yeong's offer: "After we return from the forest, if you would like to have a drink in the honor of Mingxi and Dot, I would be happy to join you in that. An important part of the cycle is mourning."
  • The dream exchange: "I usually dream of dying." [pause] "Oh no, they're not nightmares. I simply dream of dying. It's part of the cycle."
  • Visit to Kageyuri at Eliza's — pale skin, violet hair, fetchling-coded animist. She also remembers Mingxi and Dot. Parts ways at Ugly Cute's shrine.
  • Walking instead of riding: Granny Hu's five horses are now three. Ming Bao volunteers to walk; Mo walks with him to keep him company. "There's a lot more mental focus on walking."
Act II · Exploration · Into the Specterwood

Rules of Engagement, Then Fog

  • Party enters the Specterwood under thickening fog. Mo on Stealth (Avoid Notice), Ming Bao on Scout (+1 init to all), Jun-Yeong running Detect Magic.
  • Jun-Yeong's unjangdo glows — undead within 100 ft.
  • Jun-Yeong's rules briefing, posted to chat: "Don't call a ghost a ghost. Address them with friendly greetings if you must. Do not pat people on the head or shoulders. Avoid bodies of water when a ghost is nearby. Don't eat food that has two stick-like objects protruding from it."
  • Ming Bao starts hearing childlike giggling in his mind. Mo passive-Seeks and finds the source: two visible thatchlings woven from sticks and Pharasma-blue thread, plus an invisible spirit (the Phantom Wolf). Initiative.

"Slashing and Burning Them Should Work"

Act V · Mystery · Hongrui's Requiem

The Canary Inn, the Cursed Cloak, the Crimson Butterfly

  • At the inn ruins, an old red cloak in the grass. Mo's Perception (Foundry low-reroll auto from 9 → 21) identifies the curse trait — a duplicate meant for grave robbers. Jun-Yeong takes it for Remove Curse later.
  • Inside: a teenage girl sings to four thatchlings about the crimson butterfly. Mo Perception pegs her as undead. Jun-Yeong opens with Aklo. Diplomacy 24 + Ming Bao Aid 16 → success. Her form ripples: she's a thatchling with blood on its branches. She is Fan Hongrui.
  • Hongrui's story confirmed: governor's guards barred the inn doors. First fire flushed them out. Second time they barred her in. She and her three companions (Chi Hong Shin, Na Shi Hong Ban, Misuki Ban Yong) burned together.
  • Jun-Yeong Undead Lore (+1 Nosoi Skull): bury the bodies properly to release them.
  • Mo and Ming Bao dig four graves. Jun-Yeong plays daegeum (Performance 17 success, channeling Eliza's "epic raves" style). Hongrui's voice softens.
  • Hongrui's revelation: "There is a voice that ruminates in every spirit's mind in this place. It started on the first day of summer. One calling itself Kugaptee."
  • Before fading, Hongrui manifests "Hongrui's Gratitude" — a magical cloak Jun-Yeong wears immediately. His outfit is now blue-black-red and "drippy." Her warning: "There is a great evil lurking on the outskirts of this hell at this very moment."
  • Two new undead emerge from the tree line: elongated bodies, oversized arms, no eyes, broken weapons held together by crimson energy, red butterflies pouring from open ribcages. Jun-Yeong's Undead Lore + Religion both succeed but yield no recognition — new undead type.
  • Jun-Yeong realizes (worryingly) he speaks Aklo. Eavesdrops: "They should be there, ready to feast on any souls that come this way, but... I did sense living beings." The two split up to search.
  • Party stealths south. Successfully evades. Heads back to Willowshore.
Act VI · Mystery · Crisis at Home

The Stable Fire and the Carving

  • Late afternoon arrival back in Willowshore. Ugly Cute greets the party: "I did see a fire near out the west in the town."
  • Kum Sun-Chung's ranch is smoldering ruins. The stable master is rounding up livestock with help from the guard.
  • Jun-Yeong Detect Magic: an ignition spell — magic-induced fire. Short list of suspects in town: Eliza, Jubay, Granny Hu — none would.
  • Ming Bao crafting check: success. Jun-Yeong holds the nails. Mo's intimidating glare scatters the livestock deeper into the forest (DM joke).
  • Discovery: a crimson butterfly carved into the surviving fence. The Crimson Stain motif is now inside Willowshore.
  • Theory crystallizing: noppera-bo cult sabotage. Ming Bao recalls: "A number of those faceless people we ran into were capable of magic. They mostly were casting spells to fill us with fear..." Mo extends it: "If they can cast that kind of spell, surely a simple fire spell is not out of the possibility."
Act VII · Mystery · Kageyuri Vanishes

The Mingxi-Impostor and the Lumber Camp

  • Eliza arrives in a panic: "Have any of you seen Kageyuri?" She hasn't returned since morning. Mid-conversation, Eliza falters about Kageyuri's friends — even she is starting to forget Mingxi. The erasure is spreading.
  • Diplomacy/Society in downtown Willowshore: witnesses describe Kageyuri leaving with a "half-Tengu, half-human man" — Mingxi's exact description. Headed west.
  • Ming Bao's Survival nat 20: tracks two pairs of footprints to the abandoned Lumber Camp.
  • Mo's working theory, voiced to the party: "The one that made the governor disappear and the one that made your friends disappear might be two different forces." Ming Bao: "Maybe. But then we have two overwhelmingly powerful things going on."
Act VIII · Exploration · The Scouting Run

Mo Maps the Camp

  • Night. Crescent moon. Ming Bao drinks a Lesser Darkvision Elixir.
  • Mo with Jun-Yeong's Guidance — Stealth blind, succeeds. Mo finds a secret door in the fence (extraction route).
  • Layout he sketches via the message cantrip: four noppera-bo grunts around a campfire in telepathic meditation; two occultists in a longhouse chanting "Kugaptee, Kugaptee" over a misshapen wooden effigy of a four-armed twig-headed figure (almost certainly a thatchling totem); five captive farmers held in a separate building under four noppera-bo guards; a second longhouse with two more occultists lounging. No sign of Kageyuri yet.
  • Mo and Jun-Yeong recognize the cult is praying to Kugaptee — the same name Hongrui revealed at the inn ruin. The party connects: the noppera-bo rebel's "they worship her" was leadership change to Kugaptee.
