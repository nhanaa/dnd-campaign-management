## Class Features

- **Sneak Attack 1d6** — extra precision damage on off-guard targets with agile/finesse melee, ranged, or finesse unarmed Strikes (scales: 2d6 at L5, 3d6 at L11)
- **Surprise Attack** — round 1, if init via Stealth or Deception, enemies who haven't acted are off-guard to you
- **Deny Advantage (L3)** — can't be off-guard to hidden/undetected/flanking enemies of your level or lower
- **Thief racket** — DEX modifier to damage on finesse melee Strikes (replaces STR); trained in Thievery

## Feats Taken

### Ancestry
- **L1** — *Refined Motion in Darkness*: Sneak +5 ft (up to Speed). Stay unobserved when Sneaking in dim light/darkness with cover or concealed.
- **L1 Paragon** — *Aegis of the Dissolution*: +1 circumstance to saves vs. darkness/shadow effects.
- **L3 Paragon** — *Dance of the Mousedeer*: 1-action Performance vs. Perception of one enemy in 30 ft. Success → standard cover (+2 AC, +2 Stealth). Crit → greater cover (+4 AC, +4 Stealth). Lasts until next turn or until you move/attack/are unconscious. Targets scale with proficiency (2 at expert, 4 at master). **Performance trait, occult/illusion/shadow/visual.**

### Class
- **L1** — *Nimble Dodge*: Reaction. When hit by an attack you can see, +2 circumstance AC against that attack. Requires not encumbered.
- **L2** — *Unbalancing Blow* (Thief-only): When your Strike is a critical hit and deals damage, target is **off-guard against your attacks** until end of your next turn. Self-perpetuating sneak-attack chain.

### Skill
- **L1** — *Intimidating Glare*: Demoralize without speech. Removes -4 language penalty.
- **L2** — *Terrain Stalker (Underbrush)*: While undetected in underbrush, Sneak without Stealth check (5 ft, no within-10-ft of enemy).
- **L3** — *Slink Away* (Feats+): 1-action — choose an observer, Hide + Sneak away from them with cover bonus, immune for 1 minute.

### General
- **L3** — *Incredible Initiative*: +2 circumstance to initiative rolls.

### Free Archetype
- **L2** — *Spirit Warrior Dedication* (Tian Xia, uncommon, requires Tian Xia origin):
  - Fist damage die → 1d6 (from 1d4)
  - Fist gains the **parry** trait

### Granted Action

## Combat Profile

**Initiative**: Stealth +11 + **Incredible Initiative +2 circumstance** = **+13 average**, pushing Mo to act before most enemies.

**Surprise Attack** triggers when Stealth-init places Mo above the target's initiative — round 1, that target is off-guard, enabling Sneak Attack.

**Round-1 burst**:
- Overwhelming Combination (1A): rapier Strike (1d6+4 + 1d6 sneak vs off-guard) + fist Strike (1d6 bludgeoning + 1d6 sneak vs off-guard, +1 striking via Handwraps when invested → 2d6+0 + 1d6 sneak)
- Strike again or Dance of the Mousedeer to set up next turn
- Average round-1 damage vs off-guard: ~21 damage across two strikes

**Sustained**: critical hits proc Unbalancing Blow → target off-guard until end of next turn → consecutive Sneak Attack rounds without re-positioning.

**Defensive reactions**: Nimble Dodge (+2 AC vs one attack); Slink Away to escape; Dance of the Mousedeer for greater cover (+4 AC vs one enemy).

## Backstory Summary

Mo was found at the edge of Willowshore as a small Wayang child, with no memory of a homeland and no record of his people in town. The town took him in. He grew up on the streets — never sleeping in the same place twice, picking pockets at festivals, learning to disappear when adults raised their voices. Yong Wu-Xiu, the kitsune smith at Eternal Blaze Ironworks, started letting him watch the forge when he was small; she has never asked where he came from. Zheng Peng, the constable, caught him stealing a few times in his early teens and quietly let him off because he understood Mo's situation. Sometimes there is bread on Zheng Peng's windowsill that Mo "happens" to find. They have never spoken about it.

Mo missed the Reenactment Festival's abductee selection by chance — he was hiding through the invasion of Willowshore as the jinkin and noppera-bo poured through the streets, watching from a rooftop while the rest of the town screamed. The party that broke the curse and relit the Eternal Lantern inspired him. He's been trying to find a way to fall in with them since.

The town's unconditional welcome is the foundation of his character. He is grateful, light-fingered, weird-looking, quiet, and quick to laugh. He bows to Ugly Cute every time he sees the stone guardian. He drops copper coins in the Eternal Lantern when he can spare them — and sometimes when he can't.
