## Class Features

- **Sneak Attack 1d6** — extra precision damage on off-guard targets with agile/finesse melee, ranged, or finesse unarmed Strikes (scales: 2d6 at L5, 3d6 at L11)
- **Surprise Attack** — round 1, if init via Stealth or Deception, enemies who haven't acted are off-guard to you
- **Deny Advantage (L3)** — can't be off-guard to hidden/undetected/flanking enemies of your level or lower
- **Thief racket** — DEX modifier to damage on finesse melee Strikes (replaces STR); trained in Thievery

## Heritage-Granted Action (Shadow of the Smith)

- **Inscribe Shadow Pamor** (1 action, occult/shadow, frequency 1/minute) — Plunge fist or weapon into your shadow. If your next action is to Strike, on a hit the target is **Dazzled** until the start of your next turn. On a critical hit, the dazzled condition can't be torn away. *Not a feat slot — automatically granted by the Shadow of the Smith heritage.*

## Combat Profile

**Initiative**: Stealth +11 (no Incredible Initiative — L3 General is Fleet). Still very high init for round-1 Surprise Attack.

**Surprise Attack** triggers when Stealth-init places Mo above the target's initiative — round 1, that target is off-guard, enabling Sneak Attack.

**Round-1 burst (vs corporeal enemies)**:
- Overwhelming Combination (1A): rapier Strike (1d6+4 + 1d6 sneak vs off-guard) + fist Strike (1d6 bludgeoning + 1d6 sneak vs off-guard, +1 striking via Handwraps when invested → 2d6+0 + 1d6 sneak)
- Strike again or Dance of the Mousedeer to set up next turn
- Average round-1 damage vs off-guard: ~21 damage across two strikes

**Sustained**: critical hits proc Unbalancing Blow → target off-guard until end of next turn → consecutive Sneak Attack rounds without re-positioning.

**Off-guard generation toolkit** (5 ways to generate Sneak Attack conditions):
1. **Surprise Attack** — round 1 from Stealth init, target off-guard automatically
2. **Flank with Mars (incoming Guardian)** — reliable melee flank partner, off-guard while standing opposite
3. **Dance of the Mousedeer** → Hide → Strike from Hidden — 3-action self-Hide setup chain
4. **Smokescreens** → smoke ball → throw at feet, Hidden from outside-smoke creatures, Sneak/Strike
5. **Inscribe Shadow Pamor** → Strike → Dazzled rider on target (concealment-vs-target for 1 round)

**Mode A combat (vs corporeal enemies — humanoids, noppera-bo, cultists)**:
- Sneak Attack works normally. OC + Unbalancing Blow crit-chain is full power.
- L6 Cutting Heaven, Crushing Earth (Spirit Warrior FA) will create auto-off-guard chains.

**Mode B combat (vs precision-immune enemies — phantoms, thatchlings, swarms, elementals)**:
- Sneak Attack precision damage doesn't apply (unless Orion's table house-rules around it — confirm).
- Damage comes from raw weapon + DEX +4 + alchemical riders.
- **Ghost Touch property rune (L4, 75 gp)** is the answer to incorporeal resistance — bypasses res, triggers weakness on most phantoms. **Top equipment priority once funds allow.**
- Spirit of the Blade (Spirit Warrior, L6, takes L8 slot) is the alternative L8 pick if Mo wants a non-precision +1d6 damage rider for these turns.

**Defensive reactions**: Nimble Dodge (+2 AC vs one attack); Slink Away to escape; Dance of the Mousedeer for greater cover (+4 AC vs one enemy); Smokescreens for area concealment of self or ally.

**Ally-protection plays (non-obvious)**:
- **Inscribe Shadow Pamor + Strike on a target engaged with Mars** → Mars effectively gains ~25% miss chance on incoming attacks from that target for 1 round (Dazzled = treat creatures as concealed, flat DC 5 attack check).
- **Smoke ball thrown at engaged Mars's space** → same flat DC 5 concealment vs all attacks against him.

## Backstory Summary

Mo arrived in Willowshore years ago as a small Wayang child with his mother. His father had not been able to cross from the Netherworld with them, and after a few months settling in she told Mo she was going back to find him. She left him with a small copper coin, the name of a ratfolk fisher she had come to trust, and a promise she would return as soon as she could. He is still waiting.

He grew up between the riverbank and the back-alleys after that. **Zataku**, the observant old ratfolk fisher whose name his mother had pressed into his hand on the way out, started feeding him scraps from the fishery ponds without ever asking why his mother had sent him. The unspoken thing between them was that being small and strange in this town was something two outsiders could carry together. (Zataku always seems to know when the rain is coming. Mo never asks how.) For a few wild years he was all pranks and light fingers, lifting purses at festivals, swapping the chalk signs on the shopfronts of Mercantile Street, vanishing under a market stall when a constable turned the wrong way. Constable **Zheng Peng** caught him stealing apples a few times in his early teens and always seemed to forget to file the report. Bread still appears on the constable's windowsill that Mo "happens" to find, and the two of them have never spoken about it.

Somewhere along the way the pranks got smaller and Mo got quieter. He started noticing the kindnesses the town had given him without anyone naming them, and he started paying back the only way he knew how. Copper coins dropped in the Eternal Lantern when he can spare them, errands no one asked him to run, a bow to Ugly Cute every time he passes the gate. He hid through the noppera-bo invasion on a rooftop above the Eternal Lantern, watching the party break the curse and relight it. That was when he decided to approach them.

**Open mysteries** (RP hooks for later):
- What happened to his mother and father in the Netherworld — dead, captured, lost in the crossing, or simply unable to come back?
- What does Zataku actually know about his mother that Mo doesn't?
- Why does his shadow carry the Shadow of the Smith heritage pattern (forge-quenching imagery) when he's never apprenticed at a forge?
