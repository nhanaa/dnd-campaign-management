# Season of Ghosts — Campaign State

**System**: Pathfinder 2e Remastered
**Adventure Path**: Season of Ghosts (Paizo, levels 1-12)
**Setting**: Willowshore, Shenmen, Tian Xia
**Pax's character**: **Mo** — Wayang Rogue (Scoundrel racket, Shadow of the Smith heritage), age 17, joining mid-campaign at level 3
**Source**: Pre-session prep meeting on 2026-05-08 (DM verbal recap; no live play). Session 4.5 by the DM's count.

---

## The Setting

The campaign is set in **Willowshore**, a small village in the haunted forest country of **Shenmen** in **Tian Xia** (Pathfinder's east-continent analog). Roughly three years before the AP began, the **Imperial Lung Wa** dynasty that once governed the region collapsed under a schism — its fall is recent enough that the village still carries the political residue.

In-game date: the AP opened on **the last day of spring, 7108 IC** (Imperial / Tian Xia calendar). The party is currently in the early summer that follows.

The village sits in a forest infested with spirits, haunts, and incorporeal threats. Every spring, Willowshore performs the **Reenactment Festival** — a ritual play where six volunteers are "abducted" by costumed ghost-actors so the real spirits outside town will see Willowshore as a poor target and look elsewhere. The governor traditionally plays the role of **Zhi Hui**, the legendary keeper of an ancient monastery to the northwest (likely **Tan Sugi Monastery** in the AP canon) who founded the tradition. Willowshore historically thrived because monks from the monastery passed through and spent coin in town.

## Session 1 — The Festival, the Red Star, and the Invasion

**The festival**: At the latest Reenactment Festival, the PCs noticed (where the rest of the village did not) that **Governor Heh Shan-Bao** was visibly exhausted under his stage makeup as he played Zhi Hui. He gave a speech about the town surviving "no matter how many cycles they go through, how many repeats of this tragedy" — flagged as ominous in retrospect. Then the six original PCs were "abducted" by the ghost-actors and taken to a prepared camp outside town for the night.

**The red firework**: Around the campfire the PCs spent ~90 minutes getting to know each other (real RP, not a glossed prelude). **Ming Bao** (the party *Monk*, distinct from Mingxi the Magus) took first watch. From the direction of Willowshore he saw what the recap describes as a firework: a red star rising into the sky and detonating in a pulsating wave that hit him directly. His eyes rolled back and he was knocked unconscious. (Confirmed by rolls log: "Ming Bao will volunteer for first watch" and "Bao stayed up later than everyone and was knocked unconscious by the crimson beacon thing.")

**The next "morning"**: The party slept more than 20 hours. They woke to a thick white fog, distant chittering, and a sun position that said it was already evening. No villager came to retrieve them as tradition required.

**First combat**: Centipedes emerged from the forest, speaking **in Common** — not telepathically, just chittering English: *"flesh, bone, skin, hair, eyes."* The party killed them quickly, realized something was deeply wrong, and ran for town.

**Willowshore invaded**: They returned to find Willowshore overrun by **jinkin** (small evil fey, weak to cold iron — DM has noted this repeatedly). Civilians being dragged from homes and killed. Corpses in the streets.

**Old Matsuki and the Eternal Lantern**: The PCs found one of the village elders, **Old Matsuki** — 101 years old, present at Willowshore's founding, superstitious-but-knowledgeable, leans toward tradition and agriculture. He told them the only way to break the curse was to relight the **Eternal Lantern** at the bridge in the town center — old folklore claims it wards spirits when lit. The party found the lantern extinguished, and *something* guarding it: a 10-foot one-eyed creature (not an oni) with a kukri, sitting on a throne of rubble and corpses, surrounded by jinkin. Too strong to engage head-on.

**Granny Hu (north side)**: They took a boat across the river to the north side and met the second village leader, **Granny Hu** (real name unknown, "Granny" is honorific). Pragmatic foil to Matsuki — focuses on militia, trade, and supply rather than tradition. The two elders constantly clash ideologically but agreed the invasion was the priority. She gave the party supplies and potions and pointed them at downtown to free the imprisoned guards.

**The governor's manor — gone**: While clearing downtown the party reached the governor's house and found *nothing*. Not destroyed, not burned — *missing*. Empty lot, no foundation, no debris. The only trace: two stone lion-dog statues that had stood at the front entrance. One of the campaign's central mysteries.

**Closing the lantern**: They cleared downtown, freed the guards, killed a long-necked monster called **Moldeku** (DM noted "an unfortunate coincidence with your name" — directed at Pax, hinting his character's name **Mo** is similar). Mingxi crit-killed a key enemy ("the preacher") with a Spellstrike. They killed the one-eyed oni-like creature at the bridge. Earned the blessing of six gods and relit the Eternal Lantern. Fog lifted. Town began to rebuild.

**Casualties**: Willowshore lost ~75 people — about a third of its population. Down to ~150 survivors. Cannot afford another hit like that.

## Sessions 2-4 — Aftermath, RP, and the Sealed Border

The last two-to-three sessions before Pax's arrival were heavy on downtime and RP, not combat.

### Funeral and the eastern road

The town held a mass funeral pyre — most bodies were too damaged for open casket and were cremated and sent to **Pharasma**.

The two elders set their differences aside long enough to send the party east to **Karahai** (a fortress town on the nearby ocean) for help: supplies, food, manpower. The party rode out, but at the eastern boundary of the region they hit **a fog wall**.

Every attempt to cross failed. Marking the ground, tying ropes to a person standing outside the wall, walking in a single straight line — every method ended the same way: the party would, without realizing it, end up back inside the wall facing Willowshore. They burned most of a day on this before accepting the conclusion: **the curse traps them. They cannot get help. They must survive on their own.** They returned to break the news to the elders, who are currently keeping it quiet to avoid a panic.

### Willowshore side projects

- **Tea house**: Party is opening one. Strange given the circumstances, but the DM says it's a deliberate morale play — somewhere people can decompress.
- **Inn restoration**: Discussed as a potential home for the party's newest member (**Ramon**, joined recently).

### Ugly Cute and the red butterflies

**Ugly Cute** — Willowshore's stone guardian, a kind of animated foo-dog-style statue from the gates leading into town proper — disappeared from its pedestal during the invasion. (The party banter joke is that Ugly Cute is "always at Death's Door" but functions fine; it stuck.) Old Matsuki insisted Ugly Cute had a reason for leaving and wanted them to look for it.

The party found Ugly Cute at the **Gorge of Fangs and Teeth** (the same stone the statue was carved from — its "home"). Ugly Cute described visions: **Willowshore in ruins, covered in red butterflies.**

This is the campaign's central recurring motif — **red**:
- The red firework that started everything
- The red butterflies in Ugly Cute's vision
- The same imagery appeared in the **Investigator PC's mind** (Atlacoil — name uncertain, possibly Atlakoya)
- Several of the invading creatures worship a deity called **the Crimson Stain** (also rendered "the Red Stain"). They are reportedly trying to sacrifice the townsfolk to it.

Identity of the Crimson Stain is still unknown to the party — god, devil, or something else.

### The Noppera-bo and the rebel

The party encountered **noppera-bo** (transcribed as "no-better-bowl" — these are canonical Tian-Min faceless yokai). They can perfectly impersonate townsfolk; in their natural state they have no faces and speak telepathically.

One noppera-bo introduced himself as a **rebel**: half of his people are enthralled to the Crimson Stain, the other half are coerced into servitude under threat of execution for "treason against the Crimson Stain." He offered the party a deal: when the cult's main group returns from a monastery, he'll signal the party so they can either uproot or kill the cult's leader (referred to as "her"). **Party accepted the deal.**

## The Three-Year-Ago Incident — Why the Manor Disappeared (Lead)

Roughly three years ago, while Lung Wa was already disintegrating, a famed rebel/fugitive named **Fan Hongrui** arrived in Willowshore. The Imperial family's standing order was for the governor to seize her on sight.

Governor **Heh Shan-Bao** — depending on your character's politics, this was either heroic or treasonous — *did not arrest her*. He pretended he hadn't noticed she was in town. ("Fugitive? In my town? Whatever do you mean.") However, some imperialist-leaning members of the militia, loyal to Lung Wa, took it on themselves to attempt the arrest.

Fan injured the guards with magic and fled. They tracked her to a roadside inn in the **northeast** — right at the edge of where the fog wall now stands.

Accounts of what happened next conflict:
- **Official story**: Fan and her two travelling companions started the fire that burned the inn down.
- **Persistent rumor**: The guards started the fire to flush her out. She refused to leave. She died in the blaze (smoke or flame).

What is *not* disputed: her dying words, which witnesses reported in some form, were that **the governor was the one who had ordered her hunted** (which made political sense — he was the executive authority over Willowshore). And she cursed him: *"All those who serve Lung Wa will soon know how it feels to lose everything."*

A few months later, Lung Wa fell.

The party suspects this curse — and Fan Hongrui's death — may be why the governor's manor vanished. Their next planned destination is the inn at the fog wall to investigate the site of her death.

## Standing Mysteries / Open Threads

1. **What happened to Governor Heh Shan-Bao?** Manor and man both vanished without trace.
2. **What is the Crimson Stain?** A deity, devil, or something else? Cult is actively sacrificing townsfolk to it.
3. **Why the recurring red imagery?** Firework, butterflies, visions, the cult's name.
4. **The fog wall.** What's enforcing it, and how do they break out — assuming they can?
5. **Fan Hongrui's curse.** Connection to current events all but confirmed; the inn is the next investigation site.
6. **The reincarnation hint.** Governor's speech: "no matter how many cycles, how many repeats of this tragedy" — strongly implies the village has been through this before.
7. **The rebel deal.** When the cult returns from "the monastery" (almost certainly Tan Sugi), the noppera-bo signals.
8. **The missing-monk monastery.** Zhi Hui (the figure the governor played) ran the monastery to the northwest. It hasn't yet appeared on screen but is referenced in the festival's framing — the monastery is almost certainly Tan Sugi (canon Book 2 location).

## DM Hints for Pax's Build

The DM was vague but explicit on two points:
- **Holy damage / holy tag** is strong this campaign. Worth investing in.
- **Fire damage** is strong. ("I'll be vague on that. Fire damage is really good.")
- **There are surprisingly few incorporeal enemies** in an AP literally called Season of Ghosts. Most "ghost" content manifests as **haunts**, which are resolved with rituals and skill checks rather than combat. *Ghost Hunter dedication* (Pax considered it) — DM said it's playable but not the slam dunk it sounds like.
- **Cold iron** matters — jinkin (the invasion fey) are weak to it. Ming Shi already swapped his katana to cold iron.
- The campaign is **gated by season**. Currently summer (Book 1 closing). Book 2 = autumn, Book 3 = winter, Book 4 = spring. There will be a ~1.5 month time skip after Book 1 ends.
- DM expects level 4 in 2-3 sessions; final boss at level 12.

## Geography Quick Reference
