## Combat Flowchart

### Turn 1 Priority (Level 2)

**Big fight (boss or tough enemy):**
1. BA: Wrathful Smite (adds 1d6 psychic + Frightened on failed WIS save)
2. Action: Attack with Greatsword (+5, 2d6+3 + 1d6 psychic)

**Multi-enemy fight:**
1. BA: — (save it)
2. Action: Bless on 3 allies (Avarath + Thors + Rathis/Rune)

**Emergency:**
1. BA: Lay On Hands (heal self or adjacent ally)
2. Action: Attack or Dodge

### Turn 1 Priority (Level 3+ with Vengeance)

**Boss / Priority Target:**
1. BA: **Vow of Enmity** (advantage on all attacks vs this target, 1 min, no concentration!)
2. Action: Attack with advantage (Greatsword)
3. Future turns: Full advantage attacks, smite on crits

**Multiple dangerous enemies:**
1. BA: Hunter's Mark on the one you'll focus
2. Action: Attack (+1d6 per hit from HM)

**Key distinction:** Vow of Enmity has **no concentration**. Hunter's Mark does. Vow is better for bosses; Hunter's Mark is better for clearing mobs (you can move the mark as a BA when a target dies).

## Smite Decision Tree

```
Did you hit? 
├── YES → Is the target high-priority (boss, caster, nearly dead)?
│   ├── YES → Do you have your free 1/LR smite?
│   │   ├── YES → SMITE (free, no slot cost)
│   │   └── NO → Is this a crit?
│   │       ├── YES → SMITE (crit doubles the smite dice!)
│   │       └── NO → How many slots remaining?
│   │           ├── 2 slots → Smite if it will down/kill the target
│   │           ├── 1 slot → HOLD unless it's a life-or-death moment
│   │           └── 0 slots → Can't smite
│   └── NO → Don't smite, save resources
└── NO → Graze deals 3 slashing (STR mod) anyway
```

**Golden rule:** Always use the free 1/LR smite. Save spell slot smites for crits or kill shots.

## Weapon Selection Guide

| Situation | Weapon | Why |
|-----------|--------|-----|
| **Default / sustained damage** | Greatsword | Graze = damage on miss (3 slashing). Best DPR over time. |
| **Need to control a target** | Maul | Topple = CON DC 13 or Prone. Gives advantage to all melee allies. |
| **Setting up Rathis (Rogue)** | Maul | Prone = advantage for Rathis = guaranteed Sneak Attack. |
| **Ranged / can't reach** | Javelin | +5, 1d6+3, 30/120 ft. It's not great — get a longbow. |
| **Fighting undead/fiends** | Either + Smite | +1d8 extra radiant damage from Divine Smite vs these types. |

**Weapon swapping is free** in 2024 rules (once per turn as part of an attack). Carry both.

## Fey Step Tactics (Winter)

**2 uses/Long Rest.** Don't waste them, but don't hoard them either.

| Situation | How to use |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Surrounded** | Fey Step out — one enemy within 5 ft. must WIS save DC 13 or be Frightened |
| **Reach the backline** | 30 ft. teleport to protect Virel or Kane |
| **Escape grapple** | Teleportation ignores grapple — you're just gone |
| **Chase a fleeing enemy** | 30 ft. instant reposition, no opportunity attacks |
| **Combo with Vow** | Teleport next to your Vow target across the battlefield |

**Note:** At level 3, Fey Step's Winter fear and Vow of Enmity both don't use concentration. You can have a Frightened target AND Vow active simultaneously.

## Concentration Management

You only have one concentration slot. Choose wisely.

| Spell | When to concentrate on it |
|-------|--------------------------|
| **Bless** | Multi-enemy fights, long combats, supporting the party |
| **Wrathful Smite** | Single tough enemy with low WIS (Frightened is debilitating) |
| **Hunter's Mark** (Lvl 3) | Mob clearing — move the mark when targets die |

**Vow of Enmity is NOT concentration.** This is why it's so good — you can Vow + concentrate on something else.

---

## Spell Slot Budget (2 slots at Level 2-4)

You have 2 first-level slots per long rest. That's it. Be stingy.

**Priority spending:**
1. **Divine Smite (free 1/LR)** — use this every adventuring day, it's free
2. **Bless** — if you expect 3+ rounds of combat, the party-wide +1d4 outvalues a single smite
3. **Smite on a crit** — doubled dice makes this worth a slot
4. **Cure Wounds** — only to pick up a downed ally (unconscious → 0 HP → any healing = conscious)
5. **Wrathful Smite** — only against scary single targets you need to lock down

**Don't burn both slots on smites** unless it's the final fight of the day.

## Party Synergy Plays

### Avarath + Rathis (Rogue)
- **Maul Topple → Sneak Attack:** Knock the target Prone with Maul, Rathis gets advantage = guaranteed Sneak Attack
- **Bless Rathis:** +1d4 to their one big attack per round matters a lot
- Stay within 5 ft. of Rathis's target so they can Sneak Attack even without advantage (ally within 5 ft. rule)

### Avarath + Thors (Fighter)
- **Frontline brothers:** Two melee heavies means enemies can't focus one of you
- **Maul Topple + Thors's attacks:** Prone gives advantage to all melee — Thors benefits most with multiple attacks
- **Bless both:** +1d4 on both your attacks and Thors's = lots of extra hits

### Avarath + Kane (Druid)
- **Two healers:** Coordinate — don't both heal the same person. Avarath handles emergency BA heals, Kane handles sustained healing
- **Entangle + melee:** If Kane restrains enemies, Avarath has advantage on them

### Avarath + Virel (Wizard)
- **Protect the Wizard:** Avarath's job is to keep enemies off Virel. Stand between threats and the backline.
- **Aura of Protection (Level 6):** +3 to Virel's saves = huge for concentration checks

## Icewind Dale Survival

### Cold & Darkness
- **Cold weather clothing** is essential — prevents CON saves against extreme cold
- **Lamp + oil** — the Everlasting Rime means near-perpetual darkness. Track oil supply.
- **Darkvision 60 ft.** — you have this from Eladrin, but your Human and Tiefling allies may need light

### Resource Tracking
- **Rations:** 7 days. Icewind Dale has limited food. Buy more when in towns.
- **Lay On Hands pool:** 10 HP. Track this separately from spell slots.
- **Fey Step:** 2/Long Rest. Mark when used.
- **Vow of Enmity (Lvl 3):** 1/Short Rest. Less precious — more recharges.
- **Spell slots:** 2 first-level. The most precious resource.
- **Divine Smite (free):** 1/Long Rest. Use it or lose it.

### Travel
- 30 ft. speed in Chain Mail is fine, but snow/difficult terrain halves it
- **Find Steed at level 5** will be huge for overland travel across the tundra
- Fey Step ignores difficult terrain (it's a teleport)
