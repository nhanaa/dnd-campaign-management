## Session Summary

The party climbed back up out of the Lost Spire and walked into six bugbears taking shelter from a blizzard on the top floor. Jack went up the rope first, offered the leader — Brack — four rations, rolled an 18 Persuasion, and bought ten minutes of peace. Brack pivoted to demanding 15 gold to let the party stay. Rune cast Booming Voice via Thaumaturgy and tried to intimidate the bugbears into walking away; the bugbears laughed. *"Dice tell a story."* Initiative was rolled.

The fight ran one full round plus the top of round two before Jack closed it with Persuasion. Jack opened with Hold Person on Brack — wisdom save failed — paralyzing the leader before he ever got to swing. Thors used a previously-unseen ability to grow from medium to large, taking up four squares, and tried to grapple a second bugbear (lost the contested check, then missed his Action Surge punch). Virel cast Magic Missile at 1st level on a bottom-right bugbear for 10 force damage. Avarath Fey Stepped (Winter) into the middle of the room — frightened one bugbear, then crit the paralyzed Brack for 17 with the greatsword (no Smite, bonus action gone to Fey Step). Jack walked up and put the Knight of Artorias greatsword through Brack for 23 magical radiant — kill confirmed. Then Jack rolled a 26 Persuasion on the remaining three bugbears: *"You stay down there until we leave. You get to live. Or you can die up here and then we can go and kill your friends. Choose."* They hopped down the hole, met two more bugbears coming up with the basilisk meat, and the whole group fled together. The party took zero damage. The DM, deflated: *"That's how poorly I was rolling."*

After a short rest waiting out the blizzard, the party climbed out of the burrow tunnel (Rune crampons up the slope on a 12 Acrobatics, throws a rope down) and started the eight-to-ten-hour walk along the lakeshore back to Easthaven. The encounter check at the midpoint rolled a polar bear breaking through the ice — but it stood up on two legs, waved, and shifted into a werebear's hybrid form. A man named **Timon** (DM's stock-name joke from Monty Python's "There are some who call me… Tim"), one-eyed with an eyepatch that looks like sunglasses, dreaded beard, polar-bear-shaped. He'd built a fire, caught a haul of fish, and offered to share. Rune's Insight 20 confirmed he was genuine. Timon revealed he is hunting for **Oya Minartok**, a famous local werebear legend who he believes may be his mother. He asked the party about "strange black crystal rocks" — chardalyn — and described a cave somewhere east where men corrupted by the stones had turned violent, broken-common, and wielded the rocks as weapons. He suggested putting them out of their misery.

Then the fire scene tipped over into the heart of the session. Virel asked Timon about Drizzt. *"He knew my father. Who died."* It was the first time Virel had volunteered anything about her family in front of the whole party. Avarath, dry: *"I think it's the first time anybody's asked. […] It's true. It's a failure on all of you."* What followed was a thirty-minute round-robin lore drop. Avarath's father died about two years ago, cause unknown — the search is what brought him to Icewind Dale. Thors never knew his father, raised by his mother and grandmother alone. Rune's whole village in the Spine of the World vanished while he was on a one-month trading trip — no bodies, no messages, just gone, and he's been wandering the Ten-Towns ever since. Jack short-circuited into AOL dial-up sounds. Timon, casual: *"My dad was just a tiny little man. Very small. I could put my dad on my shoulders."* The DM broke character to tell the table this was his favorite scene of the campaign so far.

Thors then revealed — pulling his cloak in, showing the gray cloak underneath — that he is not from the Dales. He's from the Sword Coast. *"Where I'm from, you never really see a winter that often."* The "never winter" pun was unmistakable. He's in hiding from something. The DM canonized it.

Virel then pivoted from one mask to another and went after Jack. She physically walked up and tapped his face: cold metal. Then his shoulder: hollow. *"For someone as charismatic as you, you are not very convincing."* She demanded one no-omission question. Jack accepted. She asked: *"Are you human?"* Jack: *"Evidently not so. […] I would not identify as what you would call a human."* Avarath cut through Jack's defense that he was technically only omitting, not lying: *"Lying by omission is still lying."* Jack closed the scene by admitting he belongs to an organization that requires him to control what he shares. Avarath, out of character: *"Oh my god, Jack's from the CIA. He's here for regime change."*

The party slept by Timon's fire — he turned full polar bear for warmth, and Rune used him as a pillow. *"Hey, you lie down with bears, you stay warm."* Avarath: *"And that's how I met your father."* In the morning Timon was already fishing. They walked into Easthaven by mid-morning.

In Easthaven, Jack sent a letter to Bryn Shander through a "pre-arranged account through the organization" — bill goes to them. Rune sold Brack's morningstar to the dwarven blacksmith (9 gp after persuasion). Virel asked Avarath and Thors about Sephek's "Frostmaiden" reference from Session 8 — a religion check (22) confirmed Auril is a lesser god embodying winter's cruelty who hoards beauty by freezing it, and is said to live somewhere in Icewind Dale. Virel pressed Rune for the coldest part of the Dale; he made her come with him to an apothecary as the price of admission. The apothecary lowballed the basilisk eye at 15 gp; Virel computed (history with advantage) that the potions made from it sell for 50 gp; her intimidation rolled poorly; Rune threatened to take it to Bryn Shander, and the apothecary settled at 20 gp. On the way out Rune told Virel about the **Sea of Moving Ice** — the far north of Icewind Dale — and credited her: *"I saved you from being swindled."*

Avarath spent the whole shopping run drinking off-screen. The DM, narrating: *"That conversation about your parents really hit Avarath hard. He needed to get a drink and think about life. His loss is way fresher than the rest of yours."* Avarath: *"You guys open the wound. That is healing."*

Thors bought a halberd (15 gp, Cleave mastery, reach) — he'd been on short sword + spear and wants something two-handed to use in his enlarged form. Avarath bought a lance (Topple mastery — haggled down to 13 gp). The session ended at the smithy, with the smith referring to Rune and Virel as a married couple based on their ongoing arguing. *"They've been in here a few times. I think they're like a married couple or something. They're always arguing."*
