Timeline

Roleplay
Evening at the White Lady Inn

Bartaban, the elderly inn owner, gave the party free rooms for saving his bar from the poltergeist. The party split up for the evening — each taking rooms on different floors of the converted mansion.

  • Virel studied the murder evidence late into the night and suffered a mysterious magical effect — she failed a DC 10 Charisma save. The details were sent privately by the DM.
  • Avarath talked with a local guard about Auril's endless winter. The guard mentioned she lives far north across the Sea of Moving Ice and suggested finding a Tempest Cleric for answers.
  • Rune pressed the same guard about the murdered trapper. Nat 20 Insight revealed the guard was hiding something about Torg's Caravan. The guard deflected and left — Rune cast Message threatening him. Response: "Be careful who you threaten."
  • Avarath and Rune bonded over drinks, discussing the winter. Rune rolled nat 20 Religion, recalling the name "Island of Solstice" in connection to Auril.
  • Kane talked to Selune (the moon) in his room, excited about Wild Shaping for the first time.
  • Virel asked Bartaban about Drizzt — he once stayed at the inn, wrote extensively, asked about the White Lady, then vanished one night.
Exploration
Setting Sail from Easthaven

The party took a longboat northeast along the coast, searching for the missing Bunch o' Knuckle fishing crew. Kane fished off the back and caught a boot (nat 1 Survival).

After an hour, they spotted the crew's rowboat near 80-foot cliffs with four cave mouths. Thors rolled a nat 20 Survival: four clear humanoid footprints leading into the water-level cave, no signs of struggle — the crew entered willingly.

Combat
Water Weird at the Frozen Waterfall

Inside the cave, the party reached a 10-foot frozen waterfall. Kane rolled a nat 1 climbing — his foot broke through thin ice and a serpentine creature grabbed his leg, dealing 8 cold damage.

  • Rathis (nat 20 Strength) pulled Kane free; water poured through the hole
  • A Water Weird emerged — invisible in water, serpent-like with a draconic head
  • Virel used Shocking Grasp with advantage (water conductor) for 14 lightning damage
  • Kane wild shaped into a Deinonychus and tried speaking to it in Druidic
  • Thors' Sap weapon mastery gave the creature disadvantage, saving Virel from an attack
  • Party escaped by climbing above the waterfall where it couldn't follow

Lost: 1 javelin (Avarath), 1 spear (Thors) — dropped in the water

Lore
Frozen Giants and the Drowning Ritual

Exploring the lower level, Rune discovered an open cavern — a former hot spring now frozen solid, with Auril's Rime visible through the open ceiling above. Beneath the ice lay the silhouettes of four frozen frost giants.

Virel identified them: elderly frost giants perform a drowning death ritual rather than die of old age or sickness. These giants drowned themselves in the hot spring, and the Rime froze the water over them.

Roleplay
The Old Woman and Her Cauldron

Following the smell of cooked food, the party found an elderly woman stirring a cauldron of delicious-looking beef stew. Kane (as a tiny raptor) and Rune accepted bowls — it was genuinely good.

A faded blood trail led from behind the cauldron deeper into the caves. When Thors knocked his bowl away with a Monty Python quip, the woman threatened to call her "friend" — then transformed. Her jaw opened impossibly wide, her face peeled away, and a sea hag emerged: grayish-purple skin, bulging eyes, greasy black knotted hair.

Combat
Sea Hag + Frost Giant Skeleton

The hag's scream summoned her companion from the deeper caves.

Phase 1: The Sea Hag

  • Frightening Presence (DC 11 WIS): Rune failed; Thors, Kane, Avarath passed
  • Thors: Action Surge for two hits (16 piercing) + Sap/Vex weapon masteries
  • Kane (Deinonychus): Pounce — bite + two claws for 17 damage
  • Rune: Longbow + Hunter's Mark for 7 damage
Killing Blow

Avarath swung his maul into the hag's ribs and channeled Divine Smite through the strike — 15 radiant damage. She crumpled to the ground, dead in a single round.

Phase 2: Frost Giant Skeleton (CR 6)

A massive skeleton with a huge axe emerged from deeper in the caves, bones clicking and cracking. It wailed with rage upon seeing the hag's body.

  • Giant hit Avarath for 12 damage (3d12) and Thors for 23 damage (out of 26 max HP)
  • DM intentionally skipped multi-attack — would have killed whoever was targeted
  • Rune tried speaking Giant: "She's dead. You're free!" — it roared in response
  • Party chipped away: Rune (14), Kane (15), Rathis (15), Virel (12), Thors (8)
  • Emergency healing: Rune → Healing Word on Thors (7 HP), Avarath → Lay on Hands (10 HP), Kane → Healing Word on himself (9 HP)
Critical Hit — Killing Blow

Avarath rolled a natural 20 with his maul. The skeleton was vulnerable to bludgeoning — 19 damage doubled to 38. He channeled Divine Smite with an extra d8 against undead for 19 radiant on top — 57 total damage. The maul shattered its knee; the giant toppled and its ancient bones crumbled to dust.

The DM confirmed: the party was not expected to survive this encounter.

Pax's Notes — Avarath's Journal

Private notes, observations, and things to think about. Pax's notes only — not shared with the rest of the party.

What Avarath did this session

  • Worked the room at the White Lady Inn under the cover of "buddy-buddy" talk with a local guard. Played a slow, reserved hand — asking about the execution, about magic, about the Frostmaiden — never pressing when the guard went quiet. The guard read him as "reserved, wants to keep to himself, doesn't trust you yet." That's the exact read Avarath wants people to have.
  • Asked directly how to get an audience with Auril. Out loud. To a stranger. First time he has spoken the real goal to anyone in this world — buried inside a casual conversation about the weather. The guard's answer ("find a Tempest Cleric") is his first concrete lead.
  • Hesitated on the word "magic" when talking to Rune later. The DM called the hesitation out. Avarath is trying to understand the Rime without admitting why it matters to him.
  • Sat in the corner of the inn "thinking about something deeply." That is House Solvane's cold halls, replayed in the firelight.
  • Healed Rune with Cure Wounds on the way upstairs. Played it grudging: "sigh, and actually go cast a Cure Wound on him — just for fun. Well, not for fun, but yeah." Avarath wants to like Rune and is not letting himself admit it.
  • Water Weird fight: threw a javelin blind into the pool and wasted it. Lesson: ranged weapons are worthless against invisible targets in water.
  • Stared down the sea hag in her disguise, said nothing. Wanted to throw a javelin the moment she started monologuing: "I was so tempted to just throw a javelin at her when she started talking, I should have done that."
  • Killing blow #1 — the hag. Walked up, swung the maul, activated Divine Smite as a bonus action. In-character: "let the divine smite force through her body. He doesn't like a creature this ugly."
  • The pivot moment. The frost giant skeleton stepped out of the deeper cave and Avarath locked on it: "I shall take down this creature next." Said out loud, in front of the party, before he knew it was CR 6. That wasn't bravado — that was a vow.
  • Took 12 damage from the giant's axe. The "tactical retreat" was a fake-out to heal with Lay on Hands (10 HP) and come back.
  • The line: "I'll never run from this guy. I shall take it down."
  • Killing blow #2 — the frost giant skeleton. Nat 20 crit with the maul. 19 bludgeoning doubled to 38 via vulnerability, plus Divine Smite (19 radiant with +1d8 vs undead). 57 damage. Hit it in the knee, toppled it. Rune had predicted the knee hit. Avarath, smiling slightly: "Basically, it's like, it's destiny, you know."

Things Avarath knows that the party does not

  • Auril is the reason he is here. Not "the reason we're here" — the reason he is here. Icewind Dale is the closest lead Avarath has found to whatever destroyed House Solvane. The Rime covering the sky looks and feels like what he watched freeze his home from the inside out.
  • The guilt. Avarath believes he may have drawn Auril's attention as a child through the forbidden moonlit pond. That is why asking for an audience with her is both his mission and his penance.
  • The tactical retreat was a lie. Pax played it as cowardice to needle Kane, but Avarath was buying time to heal, reset position, and deliver the killing blow himself. Do not share with Kane.
  • Spell slot accounting. Only 2 first-level slots. Burned one on Cure Wounds for Rune in the inn (a "luxury" cast) and one on Divine Smite for the hag. The free Paladin's Smite (1/LR) was not used this session — still on the clock.
  • The maul is the signature weapon now, not the greatsword. Topple + vulnerability against skeletal undead was decisive. The greatsword is still the last proof House Solvane existed — he can't stop carrying it, even if the maul is doing the work in practice.

What Avarath is thinking about

  • Find a Tempest Cleric. Single most actionable lead from the session. Easthaven doesn't seem to have one. Next town along the route is the priority.
  • The Island of Solstice. Rune's nat 20 Religion recall. If it's tied to Auril, it is where Avarath is eventually going. File the name.
  • The audience question. A paladin asking a god for a face-to-face meeting is not the same as a cleric praying for advice. If it works, it works on her terms — and her terms will involve ordeal, loss, or a bargain. Be ready.
  • The Sea of Moving Ice. The physical obstacle between Avarath and the answer. Solving coastal caves is peanuts compared to crossing the Sea. Start thinking about boats, supplies, and who in the party actually wants to come.
  • The mood rule. Avarath leaked more this session than he meant to — the hesitation, the corner-sitting, the audience question. Tighten up. Rune has noticed something, and Rune is smart.
  • Rune specifically. Drinks went well. Good land knowledge, good Religion, good Insight, pressed the guard with the right instincts. If Avarath ever trusts one party member with the real reason he's in Icewind Dale, it will be Rune first. Not yet.
  • Kane's needling ("the paladin is running away from the undead creature?") is going to be a running bit. Lean into it. Play it grumpy. It lets the rest of the party see Avarath as a rigid paladin stereotype instead of someone carrying a secret.
  • Spell prep for next session. Bless is always worth the slot. Consider swapping Wrathful Smite for something broader — Wrathful Smite is weaker than just Divine-Smiting the target. Maybe Shield of Faith if the Oath allows it.
