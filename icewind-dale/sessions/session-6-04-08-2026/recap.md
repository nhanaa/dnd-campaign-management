## Session Summary

The session began at the White Lady Inn in Easthaven, where the party spent the evening gathering information after the séance incident. Avarath interrogated a local guard about Auril's endless winter and learned about the Frostmaiden's domain across the Sea of Moving Ice, while Rune pressed the same guard about the murdered trapper — discovering the man was hiding something about Torg's Caravan's involvement. Virel studied the murder evidence late into the night and suffered a mysterious magical effect (failed a Charisma save), then asked the innkeeper Bartaban about Drizzt, who had apparently stayed at the inn years ago.

The next morning, the party set out by longboat to search for the missing Bunch o' Knuckle fishing crew along the coast northeast of Easthaven. They found the crew's rowboat near a cliff face with four cave openings and tracked four sets of humanoid footprints into the water-level cave. Inside, they fought a Water Weird at a frozen waterfall — an invisible water elemental that grabbed Kane's leg through the ice. After escaping to higher ground, they discovered frozen frost giants beneath the ice of a former hot spring (Virel identified them as participants in a frost giant drowning death ritual).

Following the smell of cooked food deeper into the caves, the party encountered an old woman stirring a cauldron of stew. When Thors refused her hospitality and knocked the bowl away, she revealed herself as a sea hag and summoned her companion — a frost giant skeleton. Avarath delivered both killing blows: a Divine Smite crushed the hag, and a critical hit with his maul (exploiting the skeleton's bludgeoning vulnerability) shattered the frost giant skeleton's knee and toppled it. The DM revealed the skeleton was CR 6 — the party was not expected to defeat it.

## Key Events

### Act 1 — Evening at the White Lady Inn

- **Bartaban**, the elderly inn owner, gave the party free rooms for saving his bar from the poltergeist
- **Virel** studied the murder evidence and something happened to her overnight — she rolled a d6 (got 1) and failed a DC 10 Charisma save. The DM sent her the result privately
- **Avarath** spoke with a guard at the bar:
  - Auril caused the endless winter two years ago — one day the sun simply didn't rise
  - An aurora-like barrier (Auril's Rime) covers the sky, blocking sunlight
  - Auril lives far north across the Sea of Moving Ice in some kind of fortress or temple
  - The guard suggested finding a Tempest Cleric for more information
- **Rune** pressed the guard about the murdered trapper:
  - Found dead in an alley with a stab wound, couple inches in diameter, bled out, no weapon found
  - When Rune mentioned Torg's Caravan, the guard glared and deflected
  - Rune's nat 20 Insight: the guard is hiding something
  - The guard tried to blame the wizard (Dzaan) and left
  - Rune cast **Message** threatening him; guard replied: "Be careful who you threaten"
  - Guard description: tall, clean-shaven, short hair, expensive pocket watch — not a regular at the inn
- **Avarath and Rune** shared drinks and talked about the endless winter:
  - Rune confirmed seasons existed before — summer was short but real; trade caravans came through
  - Rune rolled nat 20 Religion: recalled the name **Island of Solstice** but doesn't know what it means
  - Avarath asked how to get an audience with Auril — Rune suggested prayer or finding a temple/Tempest Cleric
  - Avarath cast **Cure Wounds** on the injured Rune and helped him upstairs
- **Virel** stayed up late reading evidence, then asked Bartaban about **Drizzt**:
  - Bartaban met Drizzt years ago — he stayed at the inn, wrote extensively in a book, asked about the White Lady
  - Drizzt disappeared one night and was never seen again
- **Kane** was in his room talking to Selune (the moon), excited about casting Wild Shape for the first time

### Act 2 — The Coastal Caves

- Party took a longboat from Easthaven docks northeast along the coast
  - Noticed the **Easthaven Ferry** — a large boat frozen into the water near the White Lady Inn
  - Kane fished off the back and caught a boot (nat 1 Survival)
- After about an hour, found the **Bunch o' Knuckle** rowboat near 80-foot cliffs with four cave mouths
  - Boat contained four oars, four sets of fishing tackle, and empty bottles
  - Thors rolled nat 20 Survival: four clear humanoid footprints leading into the water-level cave, no signs of struggle — they entered willingly
- Entered the cave — frozen river path, constant moaning wind that blows out torches
  - Virel used **Dancing Lights** for illumination
  - Reached a 10-foot frozen waterfall

### Act 3 — Water Weird Encounter

- **Kane rolled nat 1** on climbing the waterfall — his foot broke through thin ice and something grabbed his leg
  - Took **8 cold damage** (3d6) from a serpentine creature on the other side
  - Rathis (nat 20 with advantage from Kane's help) pulled him free
  - Water poured through the hole; a **Water Weird** emerged — invisible in water, serpent-like with a draconic head
- Kane identified it as a Water Weird (nature 15): elemental creature, invisible in water, cannot survive outside it, often found in the Underdark
- **Virel** used **Shocking Grasp** with advantage (creature in water) for 14 lightning damage — the water became electrified
- Kane wild shaped into a **Deinonychus** (raptor) and tried speaking to it in Druidic — it responded in an unknown language (possibly Primordial)
- Party fought defensively: Thors' **Sap** mastery gave the creature disadvantage, saving Virel from an attack
- Eventually all party members climbed above the waterfall where the Water Weird couldn't reach
- **Casualties**: Avarath lost a javelin, Thors lost a spear (both in the water)

### Act 4 — Frozen Giants and the Walking Path

- Kane (as raptor) smelled cooked food from a higher ledge
- Rune scouted the lower level: found an open area (former hot spring) with **four frozen frost giants** beneath the ice
  - Open to the sky — Auril's Rime visible overhead
- **Virel** (history check) identified them: frost giant elderly perform a **drowning death ritual** rather than die of old age — they drowned themselves here, then the Rime froze the water
- Rune found a walking path up (easier than climbing), messaged the party via **Message** spell

### Act 5 — The Sea Hag's Lair

- Followed the food smell to find an **elderly human woman** (80s-90s) calmly stirring a cauldron of stew
- She offered food — Kane (as raptor) ate it: delicious beef stew with vegetables, herbs, and spices. Genuinely filling and good
- **Blood trail** visible behind her leading to the next room (old, absorbed into ice, ~10 days old)
- Thors quoted Monty Python: *"Strange women distributing bowls of soup in ice caves is no basis for a system of meeting people"* — then knocked the bowl away with his sword hilt
- She threatened to call her "friend," then **transformed**:
  - Jaw opened impossibly wide, old woman's face cracked and peeled away
  - Revealed a **sea hag**: grayish-purple skin, bulging eyes, greasy black knotted hair
  - Her scream echoed through the caves

### Act 6 — Boss Fight: Sea Hag + Frost Giant Skeleton

**Sea Hag Phase:**
- Frightening Presence (DC 11 WIS): Rune failed (frightened), Thors/Kane/Avarath passed
- Thors: Action Surge — two hits for 16 piercing, applied Sap mastery (disadvantage on her next attack) and Vex (advantage on his next attack)
- Rune: 7 damage with longbow + Hunter's Mark
- Kane (Deinonychus): Pounce attack — bite + two claws for 17 damage total
- **Avarath: Killing blow** — Maul hit + **Divine Smite** for 15 damage, crushing her

**Frost Giant Skeleton Phase:**
- Massive skeleton with a huge axe emerged from the deeper cave, wailing in rage
- CR 6 vs. a level 2 party — DM confirmed they were not expected to win
- DM mercy: intentionally did not use multi-attack (would have killed whoever was hit)
- Giant hit Avarath for 12 damage (3d12, rolled low) and Thors for 23 damage (nearly killed him)
- Rune tried speaking Giant: "She's dead. You're free!" — it roared in response
- Party chipped away: Rune (14 damage, Hunter's Mark), Kane (15 damage, raptor attacks), Rathis (15 damage, sneak attack), Virel (12 damage, Magic Missile)
- Rune healed Thors with **Healing Word** (7 HP), Avarath used **Lay on Hands** (10 HP on himself), Kane healed himself with Healing Word (9 HP)
- **Avarath: Killing blow #2** — **Critical hit** with maul: 19 bludgeoning **doubled to 38** (vulnerability) + **Divine Smite** (19 radiant, with bonus d8 vs. undead) = **57 total damage**
  - Shattered its knee, toppled the giant, bones crumbled
