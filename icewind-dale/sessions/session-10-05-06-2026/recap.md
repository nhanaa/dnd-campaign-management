## Session Summary

The session opened on Floor 2 of the Lost Spire, where the party found a sealed tube containing a Scroll of Invisibility, a small brass key, and the magical signatures of necromancy and illusion bleeding from a side room. Through that door walked a tall, withered undead warrior and a man the rest of the party recognized immediately — Dzaan, the wizard publicly burned at the stake in Easthaven a month ago. He explained, calmly: he was a *simulacrum*, made of snow and shadow, and his time was running out without a "life spark" from a living creature. The lower floors of this tower held a Netherese chamber that could turn an illusion real. He needed one of them to volunteer. They refused. He ordered the wight, Krintas, to kill them.

The fight ran two rounds. Rune opened with a longbow shot for 25 damage, Avarath Fey Stepped in and Toppled Krintas prone, and Thors's Action-Surged spear strikes set off the fiery chains on his armor — restraining the wight while he burned. Krintas's life-drain touch knocked Virel's max HP down to 21. Jack's Hellish Rebuke punished a hit; Jack's Hold Person aimed at the simulacrum upstairs failed (DM ruled it a construct, not humanoid) before he switched to a dagger stab on Krintas. The simulacrum Misty Stepped to the level above on a held action when Krintas was bloodied, then cast Invisibility from upstairs the next round. Krintas burned to death restrained in Thors's chains at the start of his round 2 turn; the simulacrum is still loose somewhere in the tower.

After a short rest, the party split. Virel and Jack searched the wizard's office and recovered four books, including *From Shadow to Substance* (a Netherese treatise on illusion-to-reality magic) and *Ventitas* (history of a sister flying-city that disintegrated over Cormanthor before this one — Uthred — fell). Virel attuned to a silver-framed wooden amulet bearing a triple-crescent symbol; she immediately sensed an inactive Shield Guardian, far southwest. Rune wandered into a small altar room, stepped within five feet of an upside-down stone altar, and watched its eight-pointed star transform into seven stars in a circle — Mystra's symbol. A flask of luminous blue liquid dropped from above into his hand. Avarath spent the rest hunting the upper level for the simulacrum and found nothing.

Floor 3 was a basilisk. Thors hit the floor first, Jack second, and the basilisk came out from behind an overturned dining table before Avarath was even down the rope. Rune jumped, Hunter's Marked it, and dealt 22 damage in one turn. Jack pulled a greatsword that hadn't been there before — a prayer to his patron, a flash of purple energy, "Knight of Artorias" — and a tinkling laugh sounded only inside his head. The basilisk's petrification cone caught Rune in the restraint stage; he shook it off the next round and put an arrow through its eye. The DM, post-combat: "That fight was supposed to be a lot harder."

The party long-rested in the basilisk's den. The next morning, Thors opened the north door into the rune-inscribed chamber the simulacrum had warned about — a five-foot golden disk on the ceiling, illusory wizard repeating words in Loross. Jack opened the south door into a bedroom where a will-o'-wisp rose out of a long-dead wizard's nightgowned skeleton, tried to electrocute Rune, missed, and faded. Behind the bed, Rune found an invisible chest. Jack's brass key from the previous floor unlocked it; inside was an ancient spellbook bearing the same rune as Virel's Shield Guardian — disintegrating on contact with air. The chest itself stays.

The session ended in the chamber, with the disk. A voice in Jack's head: *"This magic could bring me forth… and together we can rule this realm, Jack."* No one else heard it. Rune wanted to break the disk. Virel wanted to keep it — *"You think an old dying woman might want to provide for her family and give life to someone else?"* No decision yet.

### Act II — Snow and Shadow (~12:00–26:00)

- Two figures enter. The first is **Krintas** — a tall, withered humanoid undead, wight, identified by Rune (Nature). Wights are former great warriors, can speak Common
- The second is **Dzaan** — recognized on a History check by everyone except Jack, who wasn't there for the execution. Looks identical to the wizard the party watched burn at the stake
- Dzaan introduces himself: *"I suppose you could also call me Dzaan, but I'm not technically Dzaan. I am a simulacrum of the wizard Dzaan. Have you seen him? We've been waiting for him to return for about a tenday now."*
- **Rune (flat):** *"He's dead."* / **Avarath:** *"He burned alive."*
- The simulacrum's pitch:
  - This tower is a fragment of a Netherese flying city that crashed under the glacier 2,000 years ago. The rest is buried somewhere else under the ice
  - The lowest level contains a chamber that turns illusions into reality, but it requires a "life spark" from a living creature to activate
  - Without it, the simulacrum dissolves into a puddle in a couple of days
  - **Dzaan's actual research goal**: the simulacrum says he wants to *find* **Ythryn** — a separate buried city — *"if I find Ythryn and find its magic within"*
  - Krintas killed the original guides on Dzaan's order to keep the place a secret
- Avarath asks if his character can tell whether the simulacrum is lying (Insight check; Avarath: *"I should have invested in wisdom"*). DM ruling: no signs of deception. The simulacrum is *desperate*
- **Virel interrogates:** *"Tell me, what is your association with the organization known as the Arcane Brotherhood?"* — confirms Dzaan was a member; **two other Brotherhood wizards are out there doing similar research**, plus a fourth tagalong who has died
- **Rune refuses:** *"Yeah, I don't see any benefit to letting you be a real boy."*
- **Virel** continues the Pinocchio bit: *"Well, you see, we get a grasshopper..."* (Jiminy Cricket — *if you become a real boy, we get a conscience figure*)
- **Thors** lands the closer: *"I was literally looking at something like that. Nothing is free in this world."*
- The simulacrum: *"If you're not going to help us… princess and I are going to have to kill all five of you."*
- **Avarath:** *"I don't like your attitude. It's an abomination. Get him."*
