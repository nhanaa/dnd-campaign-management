Session Summary

The session opened on Floor 2 of the Lost Spire, where the party found a sealed tube containing a Scroll of Invisibility, a small brass key, and the magical signatures of necromancy and illusion bleeding from a side room. Through that door walked a tall, withered undead warrior and a man the rest of the party recognized immediately — Dzaan, the wizard publicly burned at the stake in Easthaven a month ago. He explained, calmly: he was a simulacrum, made of snow and shadow, and his time was running out without a "life spark" from a living creature. The lower floors of this tower held a Netherese chamber that could turn an illusion real. He needed one of them to volunteer. They refused. He ordered the wight, Krintas, to kill them.

The fight ran two rounds. Rune opened with a longbow shot for 25 damage, Avarath Fey Stepped in and Toppled Krintas prone, and Thors's Action-Surged spear strikes set off the fiery chains on his armor — restraining the wight while he burned. Krintas's life-drain touch knocked Virel's max HP down to 21. Jack's Hellish Rebuke punished a hit; Jack's Hold Person aimed at the simulacrum upstairs failed (DM ruled it a construct, not humanoid) before he switched to a dagger stab on Krintas. The simulacrum Misty Stepped to the level above on a held action when Krintas was bloodied, then cast Invisibility from upstairs the next round. Krintas burned to death restrained in Thors's chains; the simulacrum is still loose somewhere in the tower.

After a short rest, the party split. Virel and Jack searched the wizard's office and recovered four books, including From Shadow to Substance (a Netherese treatise on illusion-to-reality magic) and Ventitas (history of a sister flying-city that disintegrated over Cormanthor before this one — Uthred — fell). Virel attuned to a silver-framed wooden amulet bearing a triple-crescent symbol; she immediately sensed an inactive Shield Guardian, far southwest. Rune wandered into a small altar room, stepped within five feet of an upside-down stone altar, and watched its eight-pointed star transform into seven stars in a circle — Mystra's symbol. A flask of luminous blue liquid dropped from above into his hand.

Floor 3 was a basilisk. Thors hit the floor first, Jack second, and the basilisk came out from behind an overturned dining table before Avarath was even down the rope. Rune jumped, Hunter's Marked it, and dealt 22 damage in one turn. Jack pulled a greatsword that hadn't been there before — a prayer to his patron, a flash of purple energy, "Knight of Artorias" — and a tinkling laugh sounded only inside his head. The basilisk's petrification cone caught Rune in the restraint stage; he shook it off the next round and put an arrow through its eye.

The party long-rested in the basilisk's den. The next morning, Thors opened the north door into the rune-inscribed chamber the simulacrum had warned about. Jack opened the south door into a bedroom where a will-o'-wisp rose out of a long-dead wizard's nightgowned skeleton, tried to electrocute Rune, and faded. Behind the bed, Rune found an invisible chest. Jack's brass key from the previous floor unlocked it; inside was an ancient spellbook bearing the same rune as Virel's Shield Guardian — disintegrating on contact with air. The session ended in the chamber, with the disk. A voice in Jack's head: "This magic could bring me forth… and together we can rule this realm, Jack." No one else heard it.

Roleplay
Act II — Snow and Shadow

Two figures walked through the door. The party recognized one of them immediately — the dead wizard from Easthaven. The negotiation was civilized for fourteen minutes, then it wasn't.

  • Krintas — a tall, withered humanoid undead. Wight, identified by Rune (Nature). Wights are former great warriors; they speak Common
  • Dzaan — recognized by everyone except Jack on a History check. Looks identical to the wizard burned at the stake in Easthaven a month ago
  • Dzaan introduces himself: "I suppose you could also call me Dzaan, but I'm not technically Dzaan. I am a simulacrum of the wizard Dzaan. Have you seen him? We've been waiting for him to return for about a tenday now."
  • Rune (flat): "He's dead." / Avarath: "He burned alive."
  • The simulacrum's pitch:
    • This tower is a fragment of a Netherese flying city that crashed under the glacier 2,000 years ago. The rest is buried elsewhere under the ice
    • The lowest level contains a chamber that turns illusions into reality, but it requires a "life spark" from a living creature
    • Without it, the simulacrum dissolves in a couple of days
    • Dzaan's actual research goal: find Ythryn — a separate buried city — "if I find Ythryn and find its magic within"
    • Krintas killed the original guides on Dzaan's order to keep the place a secret
  • Avarath asks if he's lying. No signs of deception — the simulacrum is desperate
  • Virel interrogates: "Tell me, what is your association with the organization known as the Arcane Brotherhood?" Confirms Dzaan was a member; two other Brotherhood wizards are out there doing similar research, plus a fourth tagalong (feminine pronoun) who has died
  • Rune refuses: "Yeah, I don't see any benefit to letting you be a real boy."
  • Virel continues the Pinocchio bit: "Well, you see, we get a grasshopper..." (Jiminy Cricket — if you become a real boy, we get a conscience figure). Thors cuts in: "I was literally looking at something like that. Nothing is free in this world."
  • The simulacrum: "If you're not going to help us — princess and I are going to have to kill all five of you."
  • Avarath: "I don't like your attitude. It's an abomination. Get him."

Mystery
Act VI — Long Rest, the Wisp, and the Voice

The party long-rested in the basilisk's den. The next morning, two doors opened simultaneously. The session ended on a voice.

  • Vote on rest: 3-2 for long rest. Rune sets Alarm. Jack, after the vote: "I have forgotten that I require sleep as well. Let's go to sleep, fellow humans."
  • Uneventful night. Long rest restored HP, slots, and Virel's max HP back to 23
  • Morning: Thors opens the north door → The Chamber. Walls inscribed with cracked, broken interconnected runes. Ceiling bears swirling runes converging on a golden crystal disk, 5 feet in diameter
  • Same moment: Jack opens the south door → The Bedroom:
    • Bed upside-down, rotting blankets, tall cracked mirror on the north wall
    • Skeleton in a tattered nightgown under two dirt-clogged windows — rime-covered, long dead. Probably the wizard who lived here when the city fell
    • An illusory upside-down figure — the long-faced wizard from the entrance statues — stands on the ceiling, repeating phrases in Loross (ancient wizard language). Nobody at the table speaks it
    • Rune searches the body: chest glows. A will-o'-wisp rises out of the skeleton. Jack identifies it on Nature 19. It tries to electrocute Rune (16 vs AC, miss — he feels the static raise his arm hairs), then fades when Virel calls others over
    • Virel: "Rune, this is known as a will-o'-wisp. We have to keep this from Thors. Thors will be very upset."
  • Avarath finds an invisible chest behind the bed — stubs his toe. Jack's brass key unlocks it; the chest becomes visible on unlock
  • The chest is airtight, watertight, room-temperature inside, with permanent invisibility magic. ~25 lbs. "A really nice personal safe." Inside: an ancient leather-bound spellbook with the same triple-crescent rune as Virel's Shield Guardian on its spine — disintegrates on contact with air
  • The chest itself, they keep. Thors carries it
  • Back in the chamber. The Voice. When Jack walks in, in his head only:
    • "I've seen this. I know. This magic could bring me forth. … I feel it needs repairs. This one is not good. The walls are great. But if we can find one from the city, I think that you could bring me forth and together we can rule this realm, Jack."
  • Virel's Arcana check (DC 15, nailed): the chamber works as advertised — place an illusory object or creature under the disk, it becomes corporeal and real. But the cracked runes mean side effects are likely
  • Vote on the chamber: Virel + Avarath want to keep it. Rune wants to destroy it. Thors votes evil disc. Jack stands silent in the middle of the room
  • Virel, utilitarian: "You think an old dying woman might want to provide for her family and give life to someone else?"
  • Rune: "Sounds like it's pretty powerful if it's worth killing people over."
  • Session ends mid-debate. The chamber is intact. The simulacrum is still loose. Jack's patron has spoken twice now
