Session Summary

The session opened with the party still standing over Durth's body in the wine cellar. Kane pushed for an immediate intervention on Virel's deteriorating behavior, and the party confronted her directly. Virel denied any change, but Avarath rolled a natural 20 on a Medicine check and identified the necklace as a curse requiring Greater Restoration — a 5th-level spell far beyond the party's reach. Virel reluctantly agreed to seek a healer in Easthaven.

At the town hall, the party found the door ajar and the building silent. Inside, they discovered a soaking-wet woman lashed to a demon-shaped ship figurehead — the White Lady ghost from the séance, bound to a statue made of the same dark evocation stone as the soothsayer's necklace. Avarath attacked first and asked questions never, triggering a two-round combat where the ghost resisted nearly everything — only Divine Smite dealt full damage. Kane summoned an octopus named Reginald as a distraction; the ghost possessed it and tried to flee, but Avarath and Rune killed the possessed octopus and the ghost in quick succession. Below the town hall, they found Speaker Whalen and the guards locked in cells — Prudence had betrayed them, hiring men to steal the Cauldron of Plenty and flee toward Targos.

That night, Rune stole the cursed necklace from Virel's bag while she tranced. He passed his Charisma save against its corruption, but barely. Hours later, Sephek Caltro — the serial killer the party had been hunting since session 1 — stabbed Kane through the stomach with an ice sword while he slept. Kane's Relentless Endurance and Wild Shape into Dinosuchus bought the party time, but Sephek Misty Stepped past Thors and struck Kane down permanently with a max-damage roll. The remaining party surrounded Sephek; Virel put him to sleep with a clutch Sleep spell. In the justice debate that followed, Thors wanted to cut off Sephek's hands, but the party realized Misty Step is verbal — hands don't matter. They gagged him, hooded him with a pillowcase to block line of sight, and turned him over to the Easthaven guards. Thors: "He's still turning him in. He's just turning less of him in." Kane is dead. His player is rolling a Warlock.

Act VI — Justice for Sephek

With Sephek unconscious and Kane dead, the party had to decide what justice looks like in Icewind Dale.

  • Sephek woke up tied in a stress position (Thors, follower of Helm, "doing the correct thing")

  • Sephek's confession: He has the "blessing of the Frostmaiden." He kills people who paid off town officials to avoid the sacrifice lottery — "They deserved to die. I did what needed to be done."
  • Virel cast Detect Magic: Sephek's entire body emanated necromancy magic — he's undead. His ice sword is separate, painfully cold to bare hands
  • Detect Magic also revealed the necklace glowing on Rune's belt — Virel discovered the theft: "You thief! You half-baked potato!"

  • Compromise: gag him (Rune's idea) + pillowcase over his head (blocks line of sight for Misty Step). Party walked Sephek to the town hall. Guards applied a caster's muzzle. Thors carried Kane's Dinosuchus body on his back.

Round 2

  • Virel: Shocking Grasp (melee spell attack, 11 hits AC 11), 2 lightning → halved to 1. Moves away without provoking OA (Shocking Grasp prevents reactions).
  • Thors: Spear attack hits (25), 13 piercing (Dueling) → halved to 6.
  • Avarath: Maul hits (23), 6 bludgeoning → halved to 3. Saves spell slot this time — no Divine Smite.
  • Kane: Casts Find Familiar — summons Reginald the Octopus (initiative 18, goes on Kane's turn). Tentacle hits (22), 1 bludgeoning → halved to 0. Grapple attempt fails (ghost immune). Kane orders Reginald to sing "Under the Sea."
  • Rune: Longbow hits (22), 8 piercing → halved to 4. Ghost "still alive, but barely."
  • Ghost: Possesses Reginald (failed CHA save). Tries to flee as a haunted octopus.
  • Avarath opportunity attack: Hits for 9 bludgeoning — kills the octopus. Ghost emerges at prior HP.

Aftermath

Figurehead identified as Errtu (Balor demon, terrorized Icewind Dale 100+ years ago). Same evocation stone as the soothsayer's necklace. Ghost was the White Lady from the séance — bound to the figurehead, visible only to the party because of the séance ritual.

Sephek Caltro's Ambush — White Lady Inn (~96:00–141:00)

Enemy: Sephek Caltro — humanoid (undead/necromancy), ice sword (d10+3 piercing), multi-attack, Misty Step (3/day), passive healing. DM later admitted he forgot to roll extra ice damage on every attack — Kane likely would have died even sooner.
