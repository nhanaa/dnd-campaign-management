## Session Summary

The session opened with the party still standing over Durth's body in the wine cellar. Kane pushed for an immediate intervention on Virel's deteriorating behavior, and the party confronted her directly. Virel denied any change, but Avarath rolled a natural 20 on a Medicine check and identified the necklace as a curse requiring Greater Restoration — a 5th-level spell far beyond the party's reach. Virel reluctantly agreed to seek a healer in Easthaven.

At the town hall, the party found the door ajar and the building silent. Inside, they discovered a soaking-wet woman lashed to a demon-shaped ship figurehead — the White Lady ghost from the séance, bound to a statue made of the same dark evocation stone as the soothsayer's necklace. Avarath attacked first and asked questions never, triggering a two-round combat where the ghost resisted nearly everything — only Divine Smite dealt full damage. Kane summoned an octopus named Reginald as a distraction; the ghost possessed it and tried to flee, but Avarath and Rune killed the possessed octopus and the ghost in quick succession. Below the town hall, they found Speaker Whalen and the guards locked in cells — Prudence had betrayed them, hiring men to steal the Cauldron of Plenty and flee toward Targos.

That night, Rune stole the cursed necklace from Virel's bag while she tranced. He passed his Charisma save against its corruption, but barely. Hours later, Sephek Caltro — the serial killer the party had been hunting since session 1 — stabbed Kane through the stomach with an ice sword while he slept. Kane's Relentless Endurance and Wild Shape into Dinosuchus bought the party time, but Sephek Misty Stepped past Thors and struck Kane down permanently with a max-damage roll. The remaining party surrounded Sephek; Virel put him to sleep with a clutch Sleep spell. In the justice debate that followed, Thors wanted to cut off Sephek's hands, but the party realized Misty Step is verbal — hands don't matter. They gagged him, hooded him with a pillowcase to block line of sight, and turned him over to the Easthaven guards. Thors: "He's still turning him in. He's just turning less of him in." Kane is dead. His player is rolling a Warlock.

## Key Events

### Act I — The Virel Intervention (~0:00–13:50)
- Kane opens with: "Do we have an intervention for Virel now, or are we going to put this off for another torture or two?"
- Rune positions himself between the party and the door, bow nocked but not aimed — a subtle statement
- Virel insists she feels no change: "If anything, I have a clearer mind, a much more passionate mind"
- Party debates who can help — Virel is the most magically inclined but also the compromised one
- **Avarath rolls a natural 20 on Medicine** — identifies the necklace as a curse, recalls Greater Restoration from his family's library
- DM confirms: Greater Restoration is a 5th-level spell, far beyond the party's capabilities
- Kane to Virel: "You have a painkiller. We're opting to actually heal the wound."
- Virel agrees to see a healer: "Fine. I'll agree to it. And I'll comply."

### Act II — The Town Hall Ghost (~14:00–73:00)
- Party arrives at the town hall to ask Speaker Whalen about healers — front door is ajar, building is silent
- Rune takes point, investigating room by room; Avarath checks Prudence's office ("Looks like just a normal office to me. Totally clueless.")
- Virel finds two or three drops of dried blood near a back door (Investigation check)
- Rune hears banging and yelling from somewhere in the building (Perception nat 20)
- Behind the back door: a room with stairs up and down, and a tall black ship figurehead in the center — carved into a winged demon, made of the same dark stone as the soothsayer's necklace
- A scrawny woman in a white dress is lashed to the figurehead, soaking wet but dripping no water on the floor
- Rune casts Detect Magic — the figurehead radiates evocation magic (same school as the necklace); the woman does not
- Avarath approaches the sobbing figure. The head rises — a bear skull with a rictus grin, sobbing turning to cackling
- **Avarath attacks immediately** ("I had enough of these ugly, creepy creatures in this goddamn place") — 14 bludgeoning with maul, halved to 7 (resistance)
- DM reveals above-table: "This has nothing to do with anything that you're in here for. This is totally unrelated."
- **Combat: The White Lady Ghost (2 rounds)**
  - Ghost is resistant to bludgeoning, piercing, fire, acid, and lightning — only radiant (Divine Smite) does full damage
  - Virel's Fire Bolt: 1 fire damage, halved to 0. "I did damage, guys." "I think that's zero."
  - Kane's Primal Savagery: 2 acid damage, halved to 1. "One is infinitely more than zero, which is what I expected to do."
  - Avarath's Round 1: Maul + **Divine Smite** = 12 damage (5 bludgeoning halved + 6 radiant full + 1 bonus undead) — the only full-damage hit of the fight
  - Round 2: Kane summons **Reginald the Octopus** via Find Familiar. 1 bludgeoning damage (halved to 0), grapple attempt fails (ghost is immune). "The octopus is doing his absolute best."
  - Ghost attempts to possess Avarath — CHA save 18, resisted
  - Ghost possesses Reginald instead (failed CHA save) and tries to flee
  - **Avarath's opportunity attack kills Reginald** (ghost emerges), **Rune's opportunity attack kills the ghost** with a longbow shot
- Post-combat: Rune identifies the figurehead as depicting **Errtu**, a Balor demon that terrorized Icewind Dale over a century ago (History check with advantage for Icewind Dale natives)

### Act III — The Dungeon and the Betrayal (~73:00–88:00)
- Party hears banging and yelling from downstairs — Speaker Whalen, Captain Arlath, and guards are locked in the dungeon cells
- Whalen explains: **Prudence** betrayed him. She called in men who held a knife to his throat; guards surrendered to save his life; Prudence and her accomplices stole the Cauldron of Plenty
- Rune picks the locks (DC 15 Dexterity, passed exactly on the first, Captain Arlath finds keys in a snowbank for the second)
- Whalen can't pay the remaining 9 gemstones without the cauldron — "I can't afford to just give my family's entire savings away with nothing to show for it"
- Kane argues the contract was fulfilled on their end — "We have a contract. The cauldron was delivered."
- DM reveals: a clause Avarath missed on his investigation check requires the cauldron to still be present in 48 hours for full payment
- A guard overheard the thieves mention **Targos** — conveniently the same town where the party needs to return for the murder bounty
- Party learns there is a healer/temple on the south end of Easthaven, but it's closed for the night

### Act IV — Night Events (~88:00–99:00)
- **Virel's research**: Discovers that the murder victims were people who paid off town officials to avoid the sacrifice lottery — connecting the killings to Auril's worship
- **Virel's investigation on her bag**: The necklace is gone. It's not in her bag anymore.
- **Rune stole the necklace** — snuck into Virel's room while she tranced, rolled successfully
- **Rune's Charisma save (DC 10)**: Rolled 13, passed. Feels the necklace's evil trying to creep in even wrapped in cloth, but pushes it away
- DM: "You now understand that wrapping it up didn't help Virel, and it did not help you either."
- **Kane fails a Perception check** (nat 1 with disadvantage, sleeping) — doesn't hear someone entering the inn

### Act V — Sephek Caltro's Ambush (~95:00–141:00)
- Kane wakes up to a cold, piercing pain — a man he doesn't recognize has stabbed him through the stomach with an ice sword
- **Sephek's surprise round**: First strike — 11 piercing, Kane drops to 0 HP, **Relentless Endurance** triggers (back to 1 HP). Second strike — 5 piercing, Kane drops to -4 HP (unconscious). Sephek: "I heard you've been asking about me."
- Kane bonus-action Wild Shapes into **Dinosuchus** (10 HP pool) and lets out a raptor call
- Thors hears it immediately (same floor, Perception 15); second-floor party members wake up; Rune (third floor) sleeps through initially
- **Combat: Sephek Caltro (3 rounds + surprise)**
  - **Round 1**: Avarath dashes downstairs; Thors runs in, bonus-action Second Wind, unarmed Tavern Brawler strike (Heroic Inspiration reroll to hit) — 6 bludgeoning, pushes Sephek 5 ft away from Kane, interposes himself; Virel tries to burn a hole in the floor with Fire Bolt to drop down faster (DM gives DC 15, she misses — "You failed to save Raptor Jesus"); Sephek **Misty Steps** past Thors to Kane and attacks twice — 9 damage takes Dinosuchus down, then **max damage roll (10+3=13)** on the killing blow. Kane is **dead dead** — past negative max HP while at 0
  - **Round 2**: Rune wakes (Perception check from Virel's Fire Bolt noise), climbs out a third-floor window (Acrobatics 18), Dashes down, bonus-action Hunter's Mark; Avarath **Fey Steps** into the room, maul attack (23 to hit, 7 damage), **Topple knocks Sephek prone** (failed CON save DC 13); Thors attacks with advantage — short sword for 9 piercing; Virel Dashes to first floor (no action remaining). Sephek stands from prone, swings at Rune (12, miss) and Thors (14, miss): "It doesn't have to be this way." Rune: ***"I will never stop hunting you now."***
  - **Round 3**: Rune dual-wields short swords for 16 damage (8 + 4 piercing + 4 Hunter's Mark); Avarath casts **Bless** (self, Rune, Thors) + **Divine Favor** — no attack, pure buff turn; Thors lands 12 piercing (max damage) with Bless; Virel casts **Sleep** (DC 15 Wisdom save — Sephek rolls 10, fails) — Sephek collapses unconscious
- Kane is dead. Died in Wild Shape form (Dinosuchus). Player confirms 2024 rules don't force un-shaping on death. "Raptor Jesus is always with us in spirit."

### Act VI — Justice for Sephek (~141:00–173:00)
- Sephek wakes up tied in a stress position (Thors, follower of Helm, "doing the correct thing")
- Avarath interrogates with Intimidation (advantage from full party): fails twice, but Sephek talks anyway
- **Sephek's confession**: He has the "blessing of the Frostmaiden." He kills people who paid off town officials to avoid the sacrifice lottery — "They deserved to die. I did what needed to be done."
- He's never seen Auril but claims to "know her intimately" — she is inside him
- Virel casts Detect Magic: Sephek's entire body emanates **necromancy** magic — he's undead. His ice sword is separate, painfully cold to bare hands
- Detect Magic also reveals the **necklace glowing on Rune's belt** — Virel discovers the theft: "You thief! You half-baked potato!"
- Sephek has no possessions, no armor, not dressed for cold weather — "I don't live anywhere. I travel."
- **The justice debate**:
  - Virel wants him dead: "If he's alive, he's still a problem"
  - Thors wants justice through the system (Helm's influence) but also wants revenge for Kane
  - Rune backs Thors: "If you want to hold him responsible for crimes, he needs to be able to fucking talk"
  - Thors initially wanted to cut off Sephek's hands — "He's still turning him in. He's just turning less of him in."
  - DM reveals: Misty Step requires verbal components, not somatic — hands are irrelevant
  - Compromise: gag him (Rune's idea) + pillowcase over his head (can't see to target Misty Step)
  - Virel argues for cutting out his tongue; Rune talks her down — "he needs to be able to talk for his trial"
  - Rune: "Virel, go the fuck to sleep." / "Have a Snickers. I don't know what the fuck you need, Virel."
- Party walks Sephek to the town hall. Guards take him, apply a caster's muzzle. Thors carries Kane's Dinosuchus body on his back.

**Enemies**: 1 Ghost (the White Lady, bound to the Errtu figurehead)
**Resistances**: Bludgeoning, piercing, fire, acid, lightning (half damage). Immune to grapple. Only radiant damage dealt full.

- **Virel**: Shocking Grasp (melee spell attack, 11 hits AC 11), 2 lightning → halved to 1. Moves away without provoking OA (Shocking Grasp prevents reactions).
- **Thors**: Spear attack hits (25), 13 piercing (Dueling) → halved to 6.

- **Rune**: Longbow hits (22), 8 piercing → halved to 4. Ghost "still alive, but barely."
- **Ghost**: Possesses **Reginald** (failed CHA save). Tries to flee as a haunted octopus.
- **Avarath opportunity attack**: Hits for 9 bludgeoning — kills the octopus. Ghost emerges at prior HP.
- **Rune opportunity attack**: 12 piercing. Ghost lets out a high-pitched scream and fades. **Ghost destroyed.**

**Aftermath**: Figurehead identified as Errtu (Balor demon, terrorized Icewind Dale 100+ years ago). Same evocation stone as the soothsayer's necklace. Ghost was the White Lady from the séance — bound to the figurehead, visible only to the party because of the séance ritual.
