Exploration
The Cave Cleanup

With the sea hag and frost giant skeleton already dead from last session, the party searched the rest of the coastal cave.

  • Rune decapitated the hag for a trophy; hung the head from his belt
  • Kane (still a raptor) devoured the leftover stew with a 19 Investigation — found a human finger in it. "Good thing I'm a raptor, or this might be cannibalism."
  • Found a "Bunch o' Knuckleheads" jacket patch among stripped bones — the crew's fate confirmed
  • Rune's Sleight of Hand 18 opened a waterlogged chest: 600 gp
  • Rune rolled 26 Investigation on the hag's body — no items, but spotted the cauldron as magical
  • Virel cast Identify: rare wondrous item, stew feeds 4/gallon, 30 gallons, 3x/day — can feed the whole town
  • Water Weird was likely frozen in place once the water source froze; no combat
  • Short rest. Thors volunteered to drag the 50-lb cauldron out.
Roleplay
Speaker Whalen and the Contract

Back at the Easthaven town hall, the party met Speaker Dennis Whalen — a man in his 30s who looks 50, dark circles under his eyes, scraggly beard, heavy fur coat. Captain Arlath was already waiting.

  • Whalen pleaded to buy the cauldron to feed the town. Offered 10 family gemstones at 500 gp each = 5,000 gp. One now, nine in two days.
  • Thors (Insight): Whalen's intent was genuine. But Thors's caravan-guard instincts pushed for a written contract.
  • Whalen called in Prudence, a mousy young assistant, to witness. Avarath read it over ("copacetic") and all signed.
  • Virel briefly asked for 12 gemstones ("two each") before remembering the party had dropped to five.
  • Arlath delivered Dzaan's bag: spell components, a Scroll of Fireball, and a gray Bag of Tricks (1/day, d8 creature). Rune took the bag.
  • 600 gp split — 120 gp each.
Roleplay
Ice Fishing With Kane

Kane and Rune sat on the frozen lake with fishing rods — the first genuine one-on-one RP of the campaign.

  • Kane revealed he belongs to the Order of the Fang, a druid circle of Selune; his druid chief Arendelle sent him north alone to investigate the cult
  • Rune confessed years of isolation; visibly envious of Kane's lasting religious connection
  • Kane caught three large trout and donated them to Bartaban for the inn's dinner
"You're never alone when you have religion, Rune."
— Kane
Exploration
Avarath Finds The Ferry

Avarath walked the docks alone, looking for leads. Connected the séance clue "ferry" to Easthaven Ferry — a completely frozen boat at the docks right in front of the White Lady Inn.

Failed Perception. Failed Investigation. Got up close to the boat and found nothing visible. Returned to the inn empty-handed but with the lead. "This is not my job. I'll just let the curious people handle this."

Lore
The Plague Doctor Reveal

Over dinner, Virel asked about local surgeons. When the party pressed her, she revealed:

"She wants to be a plague doctor. It stems from studying the evidence — she's interested in forensics. How people were killed, the style in which they were cut. Like a whole field of study."
— Virel, in-character

The party filed this under "things about Virel we didn't know." Minutes later they would file it under "things that now feel sinister."

Roleplay
The Alignment Confrontation

Rune opened with the 5,000 gp gem deal: "Deeply uncomfortable taking family gemstones off the speaker." Tried bribing Virel with the Fireball scroll. She refused.

  • Rune Insight 13, Kane Insight 15 (advantage): Virel has changed since they left The Caer
  • DM confirmed: Virel failed a Wisdom save studying the evidence overnight; her alignment has shifted
  • The soothsayer's evocation necklace is still on her
  • Rune cast Detect Magic on Virel — she clocked it with Perception 19
  • Lord of the Rings derail: "Take the ring from her and throw it in Mount Doom"
  • Kane signaled Thors he'd grapple Virel on cue. Avarath was lost; Rune filled him in. No one acted.
"How long do you think you'll survive here on your own?"
— Rune, to Virel
Exploration
Boarding the Frozen Ferry

Morning of the next day, Virel convinced Rune to return to the ferry with the full party's help.

  • Kane wild-shaped into a Giant Spider and climbed the 20-ft mast for overwatch
  • Virel cast Detect Magic (nothing within 30 ft)
  • Brute (Rune's bear companion) carried the blindsight; Thors swung Rune and Virel around like a metal detector
  • Kane pre-webbed the floor in front of the cabin door
  • Rune opened the cabin: bedrolls, a large map of Icewind Dale, with a marking for Sun Blight Fortress — Xardorok's base
  • As Rune reached for the map, Brute's blindsight pinged an invisible duergar right beside him. "Don't you dare." — and initiative.
Combat
The Invisible Duergar

Initiative: Rune 23 → Thors 17 → Virel 17 → Kane 14 → Avarath 5 → Duergar nat 1.

Round 1

  • Rune: Pulls the map off the wall, flees the cabin. Duergar's OA (15) misses.
  • Thors / Virel / Kane / Avarath: Hold actions. Virel's trigger is "Fireball" (she doesn't have it at level 2 — held attack resolves as Fire Bolt cantrip).
  • Duergar: Uses Reduce (bonus action) to shrink to Tiny, then Dashes. Passes DC 13 Dex save on Kane's web (total 18 with +8 Tiny bonus).
Held Actions Resolve

Virel's Fire Bolt — nat 20 with disadvantage (crit negated by disadvantage rule). 10 fire damage (max d10).

Kane's Giant Spider bite — hits on 15. 8 piercing + 7 poison = 15 damage.

Total: 25 damage to the duergar. Still alive. Finishes movement past Avarath.

  • Avarath: Maul OA with disadvantage — rolls 11, miss.

Round 2

  • Rune: Longbow at Tiny target (disadvantage) — 25/24, hits on 24. 7 piercing. Bonus: Ensnaring Strike. Duergar rolls nat 1 on Strength save (with disadvantage). Restrained. Additional 3 piercing. Turn total: 10 damage.
  • Avarath: "I don't think we should kill him. Keep him alive."
  • Thors: Sling shot, non-lethal. Restrained (advantage) + Tiny (disadvantage) cancel to normal. Hits on 15 for 5 bludgeoning. Duergar drops from 4 HP to 0 — unconscious, alive.

~50 seconds later the duergar returns to normal size, still unconscious. Kane wraps him in webs. Party hauls him to the White Lady Inn. Bartaban: "I'll just be upstairs then."

Combat
The Wine Cellar Interrogation

The party dragged their prisoner into the White Lady Inn's wine cellar. Cement floor, drain. Virel took charge.

  • Virel cast Create Bonfire (cantrip), heated a metal corkscrew
  • Intimidation 17 — the prisoner refused: "You really think I'm going to give you information about my own father?" The party realized this was Durth, Xardorok's son
  • Virel drove the corkscrew into his eye — Blinded condition. Follow-up intimidations failed (8, 11).
  • Kane un-wild-shaped and cast Healing Word: +11 HP (to 12 HP)
  • Virel twisted the corkscrew: 5 piercing. Durth at 7 HP.
  • Avarath Persuasion 10 — fails. "Haven't you suffered enough?"
  • Virel twisted again: 5 piercing. Durth at 2 HP.
  • Kane's second Healing Word: +10 HP (to 12 HP)
  • Virel Intimidation 17 with advantage — breaks him. "I'll tell you one thing."
  • Thors turned away. Rune stood by the door with his bow nearby, challenging: "Are you really okay with this?"
  • Durth's terms: "You promised to put an arrow in my head as soon as I say it."
  • Information: "You know where the fortress is. That's where dad is. That's where the dragon is." Thors: "And that's where the heart is."
Auto-Crit — By Consent

DM ruled auto-crit because Durth was bound and wanted to be killed. Rune fired the longbow at point-blank range.

Transcript math: ~22 damage. Enough to drop past Healing Word's reach. Durth cannot be revived without Resurrection.

Another arrow was already nocked as the session faded to black — pointed at no one yet.

Pax's Notes — Avarath's Journal

Private notes, observations, and things to think about. Pax's notes only — not shared with the rest of the party.

What Avarath Did This Session

  • Read over the Whalen contract before signing. Confirmed it was clean.
  • Went alone to investigate the docks. Connected the séance's "ferry" to the Easthaven Ferry. Failed both checks; still, the lead was mine.
  • Joined the party to revisit the ferry the next morning.
  • During combat with the invisible duergar: moved to investigate noises, Perception came up empty, held position. Missed a Maul opportunity attack on the Tiny fleeing dwarf (disadvantage, rolled 11).
  • Carried Durth back to the inn's wine cellar with the others.
  • When Virel drew the corkscrew: slapped Durth awake (one of mine, still not proud).
  • Spoke twice to Durth as Virel tortured him. Both Persuasion checks failed. He was never going to listen.
  • Did not stop Virel. Did not raise my sword to her. Did not leave the room.
  • At the end: told Thors I seek vengeance, not justice. Meant it.

Things Avarath Knows That The Party Does Not

  • I was the first to walk the docks. If I'd rolled better, maybe we'd have found the duergar in the evening, not the morning. Maybe Durth would not have had time to study the map. Maybe.
  • Durth's line about respect, not love, stayed with me. House Solvane was a family of quiet people who did not speak of love either. I know what it is to serve a name instead of a heart. I understand him more than I am comfortable with.
  • The necklace is still around Virel's neck. I saw it during the cellar scene. No one else seemed to look.
  • I do not trust Speaker Whalen as much as Thors or Kane. He was too eager. 5,000 gp of family stones to a stranger in one evening is either desperation or a trap, and I cannot tell which.

What Avarath Is Thinking About

  • I am failing at the oath I thought I was swearing. I swore to the empty halls of House Solvane, not to Virel, not to a ranger with a bow already drawn, not to a druid healing a man so another can hurt him again. I do not know what House Solvane would have called the room we were in tonight.
  • The father-killing-son dynamic. Xardorok would kill his own child for failure. My family was taken by something I may have let in. The difference matters. It has to matter.
  • I did not act against Virel tonight. I told myself it was because the dwarf was important. That was not the whole reason. I hesitated because I am not sure I can take her, and I am not sure the party would side with me if I tried.
  • Rune nocked a second arrow at the end. It was not pointed at anyone. Not yet. I should know what I will do before it points at someone.
  • Sun Blight Fortress. The dragon. The heart. This is what the soothsayer said. "Destroy the heart." The path is now literal. And whatever is driving the winter that took my home is tied to that same path.
