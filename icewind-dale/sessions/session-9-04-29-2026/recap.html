Session Summary

The session opened where Session 8 left off — the party heading to a temple in Easthaven to cure Virel's chardalyn curse. At a Temple of Helm, the priestess Salen Para identified the chardalyn amulet as a desecrated object suffused with magic from the Lower Planes, and cured Virel's curse with Greater Restoration in a matter of minutes. Inside the temple, the party met Jack — a warforged warlock in jester's robes and a permanent white mask — who was already there investigating the Lost Spire. Virel adopted him immediately; Avarath did not trust him. The party then buried Kane at the temple, with a Sending spell notifying his Archdruid Arundel of the Order of the Fang.

The bulk of the session — roughly an hour — was logistics: selling Speaker Whalen's family gemstones to a jeweler (Jack's nat 20 persuasion secured 375 gp each, well above the expected 250), buying splint armor for Avarath and Thors, boots of the Winterlands for Rune and Thors, healing potions for Virel, studded leather for Jack, and debating sled dog economics. The party decided to investigate the Lost Spire — the upside-down wizard tower northeast of Easthaven — before heading to Targos for the murder bounty and the stolen cauldron.

Travel to the tower was uneventful. The Lost Spire itself is a structure from the ancient Netherese floating city, impaled upside-down into the tundra. The party entered through a burrowed tunnel, slid down into inverted rooms lit by magical flames that burn downward, and explored a library, a workshop, and a laboratory. They found books on Netherese wizards, mind flayer-like creatures, illusion magic, and consciousness transfer — plus four potions of resistance and a mysterious iron key. The laboratory contained the molted carapace of a dead humanoid ant creature in a cage. A study room had a permanent Silence spell over it. The session ended mid-exploration with no combat encounters, no enemies, and no immediate danger.

Roleplay
Act I — The Cure

The party arrived at a Temple of Helm in southwest Easthaven to cure Virel's chardalyn curse.

  • Avarath passed a Religion check (16, despite −1 INT) to identify Helm's symbol — a gauntlet with an eye. DM ribbed him: "You're a paladin, you have a minus one to religion."
  • Priestess Salen Para — an armored elven woman with long blonde hair — detected the evil in Virel's bag before it was even opened
  • Salen identified the amulet as chardalyn — magic from the ancient floating city fused with Icewind Dale's ice. Suffused with the magic of the Lower Planes; a desecrated object
  • Virel's reaction to being touched for the cure: "Touch me and I will cut your hand off."
  • After hesitation, Virel allowed Greater Restoration. A glow passed through her body from shoulder to extremities. Alignment returned to neutral evil (her original)
  • Salen noticed Virel's innate darkness even after curing the curse — a brief moment of concern before shaking it off. The curse amplified something already there
  • Salen took the chardalyn amulet for proper destruction
  • Avarath on curing Virel: "Do we have to? I mean, it's not like she's going to..."
Roleplay
Act II — Meeting Jack

Inside the temple, the party encountered a warforged jester who would become their newest member.

  • Jack was already at the temple, asking Salen about the wizard Dzaan and the Lost Spire
  • Warforged in jester robes (red and black diamond pattern), white mask, dreadlock-like jester hat. Inorganic, stiff movements. Permanent smile — impossible to read
  • Rune (Perception 19) noticed one arm is a dark black gauntlet with elongated fingers — mechanical in nature. No weapons visible
  • Jack's introduction: "My name is Jack. It's nice to meet you all. I'm told greetings are important."
  • Virel instantly adopted him: "I like this one. We should take him with us." Meanwhile, Jack heard a voice only he could hear: "Don't tell them. Not yet." — patron unknown
  • Avarath was suspicious: "And what do you intend to do there?" — "Research." / "I don't trust this guy, but well."
  • Insight checks against Jack at disadvantage — his permanent mask means "you might as well be looking at a picture"
Roleplay
Act III — Kane's Funeral

The party laid Kane Whitefang to rest at the Temple of Helm, with a Sending spell notifying his druid order.

  • Thors had been carrying Kane's Dinosuchus raptor body since Session 8
  • Rune asked about burial. Thors suggested contacting Kane's druid order: "I wonder if we'd be able to send a notice to someone that would give him the burial he would like."
  • Rune provided the name: Archdruid Arundel, Order of the Fang. Salen cast Sending (25-word limit) to notify him
  • Arundel responded: "I'm so sorry to hear that. If you can keep him there, I will send someone to collect him."
  • Salen created a coffin with Selune's symbol, then used an anti-magic field to revert Kane from Dinosuchus to his orc form for proper burial
  • Thors' eulogy (somber-joking): "I regret that he never paid me back."
  • Rune placed a painted fishing lure on the coffin: "We didn't know you for very long, but we all got hooked."
  • Virel (awkwardly): "He was a good comrade."
  • Jack (after): "If I should die, please don't do this. Just burn me like the wizard."
Shopping
Act IV — Shopping and Logistics

The party spent roughly an hour on commerce and travel planning. Jack proved his worth as the party's new face.

  • Gem sale: Jack (CHA 20, +7 Persuasion) handled the jeweler. Nat 20 persuasion secured 375 gp each (750 total) — well above the expected 250 resale value. Jack immediately handed all money to Rune
  • Jack declined a tip: "Good faith. To prove usefulness."
  • DM on Avarath's charisma: "Your paladin is very charismatic, but he also has been rolling garbage on all charisma things since you met him."
  • Virel claimed the gems as "ours." Jack: "Who the fuck is we?"
  • Split: 15 platinum each + 15 party fund
  • Purchases: Boots of the Winterlands (Rune, Thors — 75 gp each), Healing potions (Virel ×2, Rune ×1), Splint armor (Avarath, Thors — 160 gp each with chainmail trade-in), Studded leather (Jack — 30 gp + leather trade)
  • Virel received a +1 quarterstaff — DM retroactively awarded it from the mountain rescue reward he forgot to send
  • At the blacksmith, the dwarven woman noticed Jack's torso is metal. Jack: "It is an old war wound."
  • Sled dog economics debated extensively — 20 gp per sled, 50 gp per dog, each dog pulls 360 lbs. Tabled for Bryn Shander
  • DM explained the module structure above-table: "The way the first two chapters of this book are designed is that you have to do a certain amount of quests..."
  • Party chose the Lost Spire (northeast, closer) over Targos (northwest, on-road). Jack led.
Travel
Act V — Into the Wilderness

The party left the safety of Easthaven for the first time, following the lake northeast toward the tower.

  • Jack led the party along the lakeshore. About 8 hours of walking
  • DM rolled a d8 for random encounters — no encounter triggered. No blizzard either (blizzard die only rolls on encounter)
  • Rune navigated once they left the lakeshore (Survival check with advantage from Thors' help action — passed)
  • Avarath, tempting fate: "Really, there's nothing going to happen, right?"
  • Rune gave Virel the Scroll of Fireball during the walk — a peace offering for stealing the cursed necklace. "It's a sign of trust." Virel can use the scroll but can't learn it until level 5
  • Arrived in early evening: a 20-foot irregularly shaped spur of rock jutting from the tundra. Smooth, shiny, utterly out of place — as if thrown into the ground
Exploration
Act VI — The Lost Spire

The upside-down wizard tower from the ancient Netherese floating city, impaled into the tundra. Explored room by room with no enemies encountered.

  • Entry tunnel: A 5-foot diameter tunnel burrowed into the rock at a steep angle. Nature check revealed claw/burrow markings — something carved this. Not natural
  • Rune went first (Athletics fail — 2 bludgeoning on landing). Thors followed (Athletics 22, graceful). Avarath fine. Virel last — "jets right past" the catchers, lands on her tailbone
  • Entry room: Upside-down sconces with magical flames burning downward. Two stone statues of a wizard in robes flanking an ajar wooden door 8 feet up
  • Library: Most books destroyed by time. Found Mysteries of the Pharaoh (telepathic funnel-shaped Underdark creatures) and Wizards in the Hollow (three Netherese wizards — Avarath gave it to Virel)
  • Workshop: Thors found a small iron key in the wreckage — too small for a door, possibly for a lockbox
  • Alchemy room: Shattered glass, frozen liquids. A metal chest bolted to the ceiling (was the floor before inversion). Thors stacked broken tables (Survival 23), unlocked the chest with the key. Four potions tumbled out — all four PCs caught one (DEX save DC 10, all passed). Potions of Resistance: Fire (Avarath), Force (Rune, traded from Jack), Cold (Jack, traded from Rune), Acid (Thors)
  • Laboratory (next level down via rope): Two damaged metal cages. One contained a dead humanoid ant creature — four arms, hollow carapace (molted, not dead). Not from the Underdark. The burrowed tunnels match its biology
  • More books: Ajamar's Guide to the Fantastic (illusion spells, children's textbook) and The Unfettered Mind (consciousness transfer into a brain in a jar — "very absurd magic")
  • Virel cast Detect Magic — heavy reading of illusory magic from a side room
  • The Silence Room: Permanent Silence spell. No sound can be created or heard. DM: "Not even a dangerous thing — just a study room where young wizards could study without distraction."
  • Session ended mid-exploration. More tower below. Thors: "We're going to the upside down."
