## Session Summary

The session opened where Session 8 left off — the party heading to a temple in Easthaven to cure Virel's chardalyn curse. At a Temple of Helm, the priestess Salen Para identified the chardalyn amulet as a desecrated object suffused with magic from the Lower Planes, and cured Virel's curse with Greater Restoration in a matter of minutes. Inside the temple, the party met Jack — a warforged warlock in jester's robes and a permanent white mask — who was already there investigating the Lost Spire. Virel adopted him immediately; Avarath did not trust him. The party then buried Kane at the temple, with a Sending spell notifying his Archdruid Arundel of the Order of the Fang.

The bulk of the session — roughly an hour — was logistics: selling Speaker Whalen's family gemstones to a jeweler (Jack's nat 20 persuasion secured 375 gp each, well above the expected 250), buying splint armor for Avarath and Thors, boots of the Winterlands for Rune and Thors, healing potions for Virel, studded leather for Jack, and debating sled dog economics. The party decided to investigate the Lost Spire — the upside-down wizard tower northeast of Easthaven — before heading to Targos for the murder bounty and the stolen cauldron.

Travel to the tower was uneventful. The Lost Spire itself is a structure from the ancient Netherese floating city, impaled upside-down into the tundra. The party entered through a burrowed tunnel, slid down into inverted rooms lit by magical flames that burn downward, and explored a library, a workshop, and a laboratory. They found books on Netherese wizards, mind flayer-like creatures, illusion magic, and consciousness transfer — plus four potions of resistance and a mysterious iron key. The laboratory contained the molted carapace of a dead humanoid ant creature in a cage. A study room had a permanent Silence spell over it. The session ended mid-exploration with no combat encounters, no enemies, and no immediate danger.

## Key Events

### Act I — The Cure (~0:00–20:00)

- Party arrives at a Temple of Helm on the southwest side of Easthaven
- Avarath passed a Religion check (16, despite -1 INT) to identify Helm's symbol. DM ribbed him: "You're a paladin, you have a minus one to religion"
- Priestess **Salen Para** — an armored elven woman — detects the evil in Virel's bag before Virel even opens it
- Salen identifies the amulet as **chardalyn** — a substance created when the ancient floating city's magic fused with Icewind Dale's ice. Suffused with magic of the Lower Planes; considered a desecrated object
- Virel's initial reaction to being touched: "Touch me and I will cut your hand off"
- After some hesitation, Virel allows the Greater Restoration. Her alignment returns to neutral evil (her original — "I did a terrible job" playing it)
- Salen notices Virel's innate darkness even after curing the curse — a "brief moment of concern" before shaking it off
- Salen takes the chardalyn amulet for proper destruction

### Act II — Meeting Jack (~20:00–27:00)

- **Jack** was already at the temple, talking to Salen about the wizard Dzaan and the Lost Spire
- Warforged in jester robes (red and black diamond pattern), white mask, dreadlock-like jester hat. Inorganic, stiff movements. Permanent smile — you can't read his face
- Rune (Perception 19) notices one arm is a dark black gauntlet with elongated fingers — mechanical in nature
- Jack introduces himself: "My name is Jack. It's nice to meet you all. I'm told greetings are important."
- **Virel instantly adopts him**: "I like this one. We should take him with us." Simultaneously, **Jack hears a voice only he can hear**: *"Don't tell them. Not yet."* — patron unknown. No one else heard this.
- Avarath is suspicious: "And what do you intend to do there?" — "Research."
- Avarath: "I don't trust this guy, but well."
- Party accepts Jack. He knows where the Lost Spire is located

### Act III — Kane's Funeral (~27:00–40:00)

- Thors has been carrying Kane's body (still in Dinosuchus raptor form) since Session 8
- Salen offers burial on the temple's sacred grounds
- Rune asks about burial; **Thors** suggests contacting Kane's druid order: "I wonder if we'd be able to send a notice to someone that would give him the burial he would like." Rune provides the name: Archdruid Arundel, Order of the Fang. Salen casts **Sending** — "Your druid Kane has bravely fallen in battle. He is at the temple."
- Arundel responds: "I'm so sorry to hear that. If you can keep him there, I will send someone to collect him."
- Salen creates a coffin with Selune's symbol, then uses an **anti-magic field** to revert Kane from Dinosuchus to his orc form for proper burial
- **Eulogies:**
  - **Thors** (somber-joking): "I regret that he never paid me back."
  - **Rune**: Places a painted fishing lure on the coffin. "We didn't know you for very long, but we all got hooked."
  - **Virel** (awkwardly): "He was a good comrade."
  - **Jack** (after): "If I should die, please don't do this. Just burn me like the wizard."

### Act IV — Shopping and Logistics (~40:00–100:00)

- **Decision debate**: Tower (northeast, closer but off-road) vs. Targos (northwest, on-road, bounty + cauldron). DM explains module structure above-table: "The way the first two chapters of this book are designed is that you have to do a certain amount of quests before you get to the next chapter."
- **Gem sale**: Jack (CHA 20, +7 Persuasion) handles the sale at the Easthaven jeweler. Rolls a nat 20 on persuasion. Gems sell for 375 gp each (750 total) — well above the expected 250 each. Jack immediately hands the money to Rune. Split: 15 platinum each + 15 party fund
- **Jack declines a tip**: "Good faith. To prove usefulness."
- Jack on Avarath's charisma: DM — "Your paladin is very charismatic, but he also has been rolling garbage on all charisma things since you met him." Jack: "Other charisma characters?"
- **Purchases**: Boots of the Winterlands (Rune, Thors — 75 gp each), Healing potions ×2 (Virel — 100 gp), Splint armor (Avarath, Thors — 160 gp each with chainmail trade-in), Studded leather (Jack — 30 gp + leather trade)
- **Virel receives a +1 quarterstaff** — DM retroactively awards it from the Session 3 mountain rescue reward he forgot to send
- Party decides on the tower. Jack leads.

### Act V — Travel to the Lost Spire (~100:00–108:00)

- Party walks along the edge of the lake heading northeast. Jack knows the route
- DM rolls a d8 for encounters — no encounter. No weather issues either (blizzard die only triggers on encounter rolls)
- Uneventful travel. Approximately 8 hours walking. Arrive in early evening
- A 20-foot irregularly shaped spur of rock juts out of the tundra — smooth, shiny surface worn by wind. Utterly out of place, as if thrown into the ground

### Act VI — The Lost Spire (~108:00–168:00)

- **The entrance**: A 5-foot diameter tunnel burrowed into the rock formation at a steep angle. Nature check reveals claw/burrow markings — something carved this tunnel. Not natural
- **Sliding in**: Rune goes first (Athletics fail — takes 2 bludgeoning landing wrong). Thors follows (Athletics 22 — lands gracefully). Avarath follows (lands fine). Virel last (fails — "jets right past" the catchers, takes bludgeoning damage on her tailbone)
- **Entry room**: Upside-down sconces with magical flames burning downward. Two stone statues of a long-faced wizard in robes flanking an ajar wooden door — flush with what used to be the floor (now the ceiling). Door is 8 feet up from where they stand
- **Library** (south room): Bookshelves spilled their contents onto the ceiling-turned-floor. Most books destroyed by time and moisture. Found: *Mysteries of the Pharaoh* (telepathic funnel-shaped monsters of the Underdark — likely mind flayers), *Wizards in the Hollow* (chronicles three Netherese wizards — Avarath found it, gave it to Virel)
- **Workshop/Alchemy rooms** (north): Shattered equipment, two overturned work tables. Thors finds a **small iron key** — too small for a door, possibly for a lockbox. Adjacent room: shattered glass, frozen liquid puddles. A **metal chest bolted to the ceiling** (was bolted to the floor before the tower inverted). Thors stacks tables to reach it (Survival 23), unlocks it with the key. Four potions tumble out — party catches them (DEX save DC 10, all pass)
  - **Potions of Resistance**: Force (Jack → traded to Rune), Fire (Avarath), Cold (Rune → traded to Jack), Acid (Thors)
- **Rune gives Virel the Scroll of Fireball** as a peace offering for stealing the necklace — "It's a sign of trust." Virel can use the scroll now but can't learn it until she gets 3rd-level spell slots at level 5
- **Laboratory** (next level down, via burrowed tunnel + rope): Two large damaged metal cages. One contains a **dead humanoid ant creature** — four arms, carapace only (molted/empty shell, no bones). Not a creature anyone recognizes. Not from the Underdark. No signs of anything living
- More books found: *Ajamar's Guide to the Fantastic* (illusion spells for young wizards — children's textbook), *The Unfettered Mind* (how to transfer consciousness into a brain in a jar — "very absurd magic")
- **Virel casts Detect Magic**: Heavy reading of **illusory magic** from a side room
- **The Silence Room**: A small room with a permanent Silence spell. No sound can be created or heard inside. DM explains above-table: "This room is strictly a study room where young wizards could study without distraction. It's not even a dangerous thing."
- Session ends mid-exploration. More tower remains below.
