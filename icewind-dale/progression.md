## The Core: Paladin 2 → 6 (Non-Negotiable)

Every optimization path starts with Paladin 6. The features are too strong to skip.

### Level 3 — Oath of Vengeance

**Vow of Enmity** (Channel Divinity)
- BA, 10 ft. range, 1 minute duration
- Advantage on all attack rolls against one creature
- This is the engine. Advantage = more hits = more smites = more crits
- 1/Short Rest — use on the scariest enemy every fight

**Oath Spells (Always Prepared, don't count against prepared limit):**
- 3rd: Bane, Hunter's Mark
- 5th: Hold Person, Misty Step

**Hunter's Mark** — 1d6 extra damage per hit, concentration, BA to cast and move
- Competes with Bless for concentration, but better for single-target damage
- With Extra Attack (level 5): 2d6 extra per round on one target

### Level 4 — Feat

See **Feat Analysis** section below.

### Level 5 — Extra Attack

- Two attacks per Action — doubles your damage output
- With Vow of Enmity: two attacks with advantage = ~19% chance to crit at least once per round
- Greatsword baseline: 2 × (2d6+3) = avg 20/round, plus Graze on misses

**2nd-Level Spells Unlocked (Vengeance oath spells: Hold Person, Misty Step):**
- **Find Steed** — summon a warhorse or similar. It acts on your initiative, shares your self-targeting spells. Huge mobility in Icewind Dale's open tundra.
- **Aid** — no concentration, +5 max HP to 3 creatures for 8 hours. Cast it every morning.
- **Misty Step** (Oath) — BA teleport 30 ft., no concentration. Stacks with Fey Step for insane mobility.
- **Hold Person** (Oath) — paralyzed target = auto-crit in melee. Smite on an auto-crit is devastating.

### Level 6 — Aura of Protection

- **All allies within 10 ft. get +CHA mod (+3) to ALL saving throws**
- One of the strongest features in the entire game
- This is the breakpoint. After this, evaluate multiclass vs staying.

## Feat Analysis (Level 4)

### Tier 1: The Top Picks

#### Great Weapon Master (2024 version) — RECOMMENDED

The 2024 GWM is reworked and excellent:
- **Passive:** When you hit with a Heavy weapon, add your **proficiency bonus** to damage (+2 at level 4, scales to +6)
- **Active:** When you score a critical hit or reduce a creature to 0 HP, you get a **bonus action melee attack**

Why it's best for Avarath:
- +2 damage on every hit with Greatsword/Maul, no downside (the -5/+10 gamble is gone in 2024)
- At level 5 with Extra Attack: +2 on each hit = +4 damage/round for free
- Crit/kill → BA attack synergizes perfectly with **Vow of Enmity** (advantage = more crits)
- At level 5 with Vow: ~19% to crit per round → frequent BA attacks
- Scales with proficiency bonus — gets better every tier

**Math at Level 5 with GWM + Vow of Enmity:**
- 2 attacks, advantage, Greatsword: 2 × (2d6 + 3 + 2) = avg 24/round
- Plus Graze on misses (5 damage per miss)
- Plus ~19% chance of a bonus action 3rd attack
- Plus smite on crits for massive burst

#### +2 STR (to 18)

- +1 to all attacks, damage, Graze, Topple DC (now 14), Athletics
- Simple, reliable, no wasted value
- Better than GWM at levels 4-5 if you're not critting often (i.e. before Vow is online every fight)

### Tier 2: Strong but Situational

#### Polearm Master (requires weapon switch to Glaive/Halberd)

- BA attack every round (1d4+STR) — consistent extra damage
- Reaction attack when enemies enter your reach (10 ft. with polearms)
- Would require giving up Greatsword (losing Graze) and Maul (losing Topple)
- **Not recommended** — you lose weapon masteries and the family greatsword is a bond item

#### Sentinel

- When you hit with an opportunity attack, target's speed becomes 0
- Can make OA even if target uses Disengage
- Reaction attack when ally within 5 ft. is attacked
- Good for protecting the backline, but Vengeance is aggressive, not defensive

#### Resilient (CON)

- CON save proficiency: +3 on concentration checks
- Matters for maintaining Bless, Hunter's Mark, Wrathful Smite
- More valuable at higher levels when you have better concentration spells
- Can take this later if needed

### Tier 3: Avoid

- **+2 CHA** — CHA is great at level 6+ for Aura, but at level 4 you need damage/survival NOW. Take this later.
- **Savage Attacker** — reroll damage dice once per turn. Math is mediocre with 2d6 (averages only +1.6 damage).
- **Heavy Armor Master** — damage reduction doesn't scale well.

### Verdict

**Take Great Weapon Master at 4.** The passive +Prof damage has no downside and scales forever. The BA attack on crit/kill synergizes with Vow of Enmity's advantage. It's the highest-value feat for this build at this level.

## After Paladin 6: Multiclass Evaluation

### Option A: Stay Paladin (Simple, Strong)

| Level | Feature |
|-------|---------|
| 7 | **Relentless Avenger** — When you hit with OA, move up to half speed without provoking. Chase-down ability. |
| 8 | Feat/ASI — pick up +2 STR or CHA |
| 9 | 3rd-level spells — **Revivify**, Haste (if oath), Spirit Shroud |
| 10 | Aura of Courage (immune to Frightened in 10 ft.) |
| 11 | **Improved Divine Smite** — +1d8 radiant on every melee hit, no resource cost |

**Pros:** Level 11 Improved Divine Smite is amazing. Aura range expands to 30 ft. at 18. Simple.
**Cons:** Paladin 7-10 is relatively weak per-level compared to multiclass options.

### Option B: Paladin 6 / Sorcerer X (Smite Machine)

Dip into **Draconic Sorcerer** or **Divine Soul Sorcerer** after Paladin 6.

**What you gain:**
- More spell slots = more smites (Sorcerer slots work for smiting)
- **Sorcery Points + Quickened Spell** — Quicken a spell (like Hold Person) as BA, then attack the paralyzed target (auto-crit) and smite
- Shield spell (reaction, +5 AC) — huge survivability boost
- Absorb Elements — halve elemental damage and add it to next attack. Critical in Icewind Dale (cold damage everywhere)
- Con save proficiency from Sorcerer 1 if it's your first class... but it's not, so you'd need Resilient (CON) separately

**Breakpoints:**
- Sorcerer 1: Shield, Absorb Elements, 4 cantrips
- Sorcerer 2: Font of Magic (Sorcery Points)
- Sorcerer 3: Metamagic (Quickened Spell + one other), 2nd-level Sorc spells
- Sorcerer 5: 3rd-level slots for bigger smites

**Cons:** Delays Extra Attack if you dip before Paladin 5. Delays Aura if you dip before Paladin 6. Only multiclass AFTER Paladin 6.

### Option C: Paladin 6 / Fighter 2 (Action Surge Nova)

**What you gain:**
- **Fighter 1:** Fighting Style (Defense for +1 more AC, or Dueling if you ever sword-and-board), Second Wind (BA heal)
- **Fighter 2:** **Action Surge** — one additional Action per Short Rest

**Action Surge + Vow of Enmity + Smite = the biggest nova round in the game.**
- Action: 2 attacks with advantage → Smite on both
- Action Surge: 2 more attacks with advantage → Smite on both
- That's 4 attacks with advantage, all potential smite targets
- On a boss turn, this is easily 60-80+ damage in one round

**Cons:** Only 2 levels of value. After Fighter 2, diminishing returns unless you go to Fighter 3 (Champion for expanded crit range on 19-20, which is spicy with Vow).

### Option D: Paladin 6 / Warlock 2-3 (Short Rest Smites)

Dip **Hexblade** or **Fiend** Warlock.

**What you gain:**
- Warlock spell slots recharge on Short Rest = more smites per day
- Eldritch Blast for ranged option (you currently have none beyond 120 ft.)
- Invocations: Agonizing Blast, Devil's Sight (see through magical darkness — huge in Icewind Dale)
- If Hexblade: use CHA for weapon attacks (less relevant since your STR is already high)

**Cons:** Only 2 slots that recharge on SR. Thematic stretch unless you tie the pact to the pond entity.

### Recommended Path

**Paladin 6 → Fighter 2 → Paladin or Sorcerer**

- Paladin 6 gets you the core (Extra Attack, Aura, Smite, Vow)
- Fighter 2 gives Action Surge for devastating nova rounds
- Then either return to Paladin for Improved Divine Smite at Paladin 11, or pivot to Sorcerer for spell slot fuel and Quickened Spell

**Alternative if you want simplicity:** Straight Paladin. Improved Divine Smite at 11 is a passive +1d8 radiant on every hit that requires nothing. Can't argue with free damage.

## Equipment Priorities

### Immediate Needs (Levels 2-4)

1. **Cold Weather Clothing** — if you don't already have it, this is survival-critical in Icewind Dale. Prevents CON saves against extreme cold.
2. **Oil for the Lamp** — perpetual darkness in the Dale means light sources matter
3. **Rope (50 ft.)** — you don't have any. Basic adventuring essential.
4. **A ranged option** — 5 javelins at 30/120 is your only ranged attack. Consider a longbow if proficient (you are — martial weapons).

### Magic Item Wishlist

| Priority | Item | Why |
|----------|------|-----|
| 1 | **+1 Greatsword** | Direct upgrade to hit and damage on every attack |
| 2 | **Gauntlets of Ogre Power** (STR 19) | Frees up ASI for feats instead of STR bumps |
| 3 | **Cloak of Protection** | +1 AC and saves — stacks with Aura of Protection |
| 4 | **Amulet of Proof Against Detection and Location** | Thematic — hide from the entity that saw you |
| 5 | **Ring of Warmth** | Resistance to cold damage + comfortable in extreme cold. Perfect for Icewind Dale. |

### Weapon Strategy

- **Greatsword** (default): Graze guarantees damage even on a miss. Best for sustained DPR.
- **Maul** (situational): Topple for knocking enemies Prone. Use when Rathis needs Sneak Attack setup or when the party wants to dogpile a big target.
- **Javelin** (ranged fallback): Only 30 ft. normal range. Consider buying a longbow for 150/600 ft.
- Keep both Greatsword AND Maul on you — swapping weapons is free (once per turn as part of an attack in 2024 rules).
