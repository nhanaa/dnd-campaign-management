## Backstory

Avarath Solvane was the youngest son of a minor Eladrin noble house in the Feywild. House Solvane was old, proud, and quiet — they didn't seek power, they simply endured.

One night, an unnatural winter swept through the court. Not snow, not frost — something deeper. The halls froze from the inside out. By morning the court was gone. Everyone he knew, gone. He walked out of the ruins carrying his family's greatsword and nothing else.

He's spent the years since chasing fragments. A name half-heard. Magic that felt familiar. Leads that went cold. The latest one pointed to Icewind Dale, a land gripped by a winter that shouldn't exist. It feels like what took his home. He doesn't have proof. He has a sword and a promise.

He swore his oath not to a god, not to an order. He swore it to the empty halls of House Solvane. Whatever did this, he will find it. And it will answer for what it took.

### Secret (DM-given, campaign tie-in)

As a child, Avarath went near a pond forbidden by the elders of his village. In the light of the moon, the pond showed him a new world. While he was looking through, something on the other side saw him. He ran — the pond froze solid instantly.

Years later, when the ice came and destroyed House Solvane, Avarath knew he was partly at fault. Whatever that creature was, it saw him as a child and learned there was a world beyond for it to freeze.

This adds a **guilt layer** to Avarath's determination — he isn't just seeking justice; he's seeking atonement for something he may have caused.
