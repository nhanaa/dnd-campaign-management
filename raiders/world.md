# World — Raiders of the Serpent Sea

## System
- **System:** D&D 5e
- **Setting:** Grimnir — Norse mythology-inspired world born from the destruction of the Lost Lands
- **Campaign Style:** Sandbox after level 2; leveling tied to completing certain campaign areas
- **Theme:** Viking seafaring, survival against harsh elements, Ragnarok

## Key Lore

- **The Yoten:** World-destroying giants from the Lost Lands. Boda was their leader.
- **The Vanir:** Heroic beings who fought the Yoten; now the Stone Court gods (petrified).
- **Aldyhn:** Leader of the Vanir, created Grimnir using Mirgal's magic. Aesgor carries a yarn effigy to Aldyhn.
- **Thonir:** Son of Aldyhn, slew Boda, now rules the Halls of the Slain.
- **Hel:** Lady of the Dead, Thonir's lover. Resides in the Underworld.
- **Mirgal:** Vanir traitor whose magic birthed Grimnir.
- **The Rainbow Spear:** Mirgal's weapon, shattered by Thonir. Pieces scattered among clans. If reassembled = Ragnarok.
- **The Witchking:** Emerged 300 years ago from ironwood forests, united the Baendur against raiders. Still alive via magic, works through Ironwood Witches to recover spear pieces.
- **The Long Winter:** Two devastating winters, failing harvests, perpetual fog. Strange lights atop Turnfjall.
- **The Gathering:** Every 8 years the raider clans unite. Critical political event.

## Aesgor's Story Hooks

- Rune of the Vigilant — birthmark marking obligation to watch for Yoten return
- Family legacy bond — what is the legacy?
- Hatred of the Yoten — will be tested if true Tuss nature is revealed
- Tuss secret — if discovered, could mean exile or death

## NPCs

### Allies
- **Hrolf** — Veteran Hallharn warrior, commands what remains of the raiding party (~5–6 men). Same tribe as Aesgor. Damaged internally (no proficiency bonus on attacks, gains exhaustion easily). Respects strength and the old rites; personally burns the dead. Prayed to Hel directly after the zombie attack. His authority over the raiding party eroded after the ruined farmstead — he defers to the group now.
- **Siddhe** — A small child magically displaced from another world. Grandparents still alive in her homeland, which she describes as endless green farmland. Was playing outside their house when a bright light carried her to Kekel. Traveling with the party; Aesgor has promised to return her home. Can bring downed allies back to consciousness (some kind of innate ability). Revived Glug in the beach fight by physically jumping on her chest and bit-slapping her awake.

### Hallharn crew (surviving after the beach battle)
The raiding party started at nearly 60 warriors. After the beach attack, only five named crew remain beside Hrolf:
- **Yursa**
- **Bardi** — Went down in round 2 of the beach fight (second archer volley).
- **Dispute**
- **Hercia**
- **Asta**

None of them proved useful in the beach battle. Hrolf's comment: *"They were literally meat shields. They just slowed down the enemy by a turn."*

### Enemies Encountered
- **The Unknown Enemy Clan** — Unrecognized raider clan that attacked the party on the beach outside Kekel. Carries a clan shield bearing a stylized stalking predator — *tiger-shaped, but not definitively a tiger*. Unrecognized by any of the party, including Hrolf. Their commander ordered the withdrawal with: *"We are done here. Let them die on the beach."*
- **The Tortured Zombie Raider** — A single animate corpse that attacked the party at the ruined farmstead past Kekel. Armed with battleaxe and shield, wore a rusted breastplate, had Undead Fortitude (AC 18). **No fingernails** — torn off, as if tortured before death. The dead are not supposed to rise in Grimnir. Hrolf was genuinely shaken and prayed to Hel.

## Locations

- **Kekel** — The "misplaced tower." Party were the first to explore it in generations (+1 Glory). Location of the white raven feather, the iron bust of a handsome man with lips sewn shut, and the augury globe.
- **The Ash Desert / Fanged Sands** — Volcanic soil, ash, rock, and continuous snow. Land has no vegetation to speak of. Travel is miserable; food is scarce.
- **The Red Cairn Trading Post** — A long-derelict dock and trading stop. Nothing salvageable. Marked by a red-painted stone cairn signifying the end of a known trail.
- **The Ruined Farmstead ("Double Nowhere")** — Three weather-beaten wooden buildings on a hill. Former inhabitants murdered in their beds 2–4 years ago. Used by the party as emergency shelter. Now also the burial site of the zombie raider.

## Story

### Session 1 — The Misplaced Tower of Kekel
The party's boat was sabotaged; they killed the saboteurs and reached a ruined tower (Kekel). Inside, they found a child (Siddhe) hiding in debris, an augury globe showing visions of past/present/future, and a scuffle downstairs with wicker sentries attacking Hrolf and his companions. Loot included a moonstone gem, augury bones from dead rats, three healing vials, a **white raven feather**, and the **iron bust of a handsome man with his lips sewn shut**.

### Session 2 — Beach of the Slain and the Road to Nowhere
- **The Beach Battle.** A near-TPK tutorial for new squad combat mechanics. Enemy boarders from the unknown clan landed and burned both friendly longships along with their own. Volley fire dropped three of five party members in round 1. The party survived only via potions, Aesgor holding at 1 HP, Marzena's crossbow crit, Siddhe child-bit-slapping Glug conscious, and Tree-Hammer sharing his own healing potion. Enemy commander ordered withdrawal: *"Let them die on the beach."*
- **The Pyre and the Valkyries.** As the party burned the 35–40 dead, **only Aesgor** saw three winged, flame-haired women appear in the smoke to collect the slain. Gaynor named them immediately: the armies of the final battle are being reinforced. Tree-Hammer retrieved a **grayish feather** from their smoke; touching it gave him a vision of a peaceful silver-sea beach, and he spoke an old word — *luau*.
- **The Road to Nowhere.** Seven days east-northeast from Kekel through ash and snow. A red-stone trail cairn near a ruined trading post. A "mutated" moose (killed, yielded ~31 rations; nearly killed Marzena on a charge crit, saved by a retconned grapple). A frozen caravan of 3 dwarves and 2 elves — foreign displaced persons who froze to death in summer clothes, buried diamond dust, pearls, silver.
- **"Double Nowhere."** Hrolf led the party excitedly to what he remembered as the town of Nowhere. It was instead three weather-beaten wooden buildings on a hill — inhabitants murdered in their beds 2–4 years ago. After the ruined settlement, Hrolf's authority over the raiding party effectively ended.
- **The Storm and the Zombie.** A dark storm of biting wind and rock pellets drove the party back to shelter at the ruined farmstead. On Marzena's first watch, a zombie raider (AC 18, Undead Fortitude, missing fingernails) burst through the door. Marzena nearly died trying to solo it while the party slept. Glug finally killed it with a **flaming animal-skin rug thrown as a net**. Hrolf burned the corpse and prayed directly to Hel. **Session ended on the mystery of the walking dead.**
