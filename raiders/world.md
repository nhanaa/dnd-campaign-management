# World — Raiders of the Serpent Sea

## System
- **System:** D&D 5e (2014 PHB)
- **Setting:** Grimnir — Norse mythology-inspired world born from the destruction of the Lost Lands
- **Campaign Style:** Sandbox after level 2; leveling tied to completing certain campaign areas
- **Theme:** Viking seafaring, survival against harsh elements, Ragnarok

## Key Lore

- **The Yoten:** World-destroying giants from the Lost Lands. Boda was their leader.
- **The Vanir:** Heroic beings who fought the Yoten; now the Stone Court gods (petrified).
- **Aldyhn:** Leader of the Vanir, created Grimnir using Mirgal's magic. Aesgor carries a yarn effigy to Aldyhn.
- **Thonir:** Son of Aldyhn, slew Boda, now rules the Halls of the Slain.
- **Hel:** Lady of the Dead, Thonir's lover. Resides in the Underworld.
- **Mirgal:** Vanir traitor whose magic birthed Grimnir.
- **The Rainbow Spear:** Mirgal's weapon, shattered by Thonir. Pieces scattered among clans. If reassembled = Ragnarok.
- **The Witchking:** Emerged 300 years ago from ironwood forests, united the Baendur against raiders. Still alive via magic, works through Ironwood Witches to recover spear pieces.
- **The Long Winter:** Two devastating winters, failing harvests, perpetual fog. Strange lights atop Turnfjall.
- **The Gathering:** Every 8 years the raider clans unite. Critical political event.
- **The Oath of Fellowship:** Raider blood-ritual. Slit palm, spill blood, recite the formula on your life, clan, and honor. Failure to uphold results in the **Curse of the Exile**.
- **Varang:** A clanless raider. In Nowhere, *"pretty much everyone in this town."*
- **Birth runes:** Every PC bears one on a palm. Aesgor's ᚲ (Rune of the Vigilant) is not unique — Kinnikade reads them on every PC.
- **Naglfar (implied, not yet named in-world):** Igyll's dread vision — a massive ship of nails, the dead crewing every square inch, a half-ruined woman at the prow. End-of-the-world material; Hel-adjacent. DC 30 History; nobody placed it.

## Aesgor's Story Hooks

- Rune of the Vigilant — birthmark marking obligation to watch for Yoten return
- Family legacy bond — what is the legacy?
- Hatred of the Yoten — will be tested if true Tuss nature is revealed
- Tuss secret — if discovered, could mean exile or death

## NPCs

### Allies
- **Hrolf** — Veteran Hallharn warrior, commands what remains of the raiding party (~5–6 men). Same tribe as Aesgor. Damaged internally (no proficiency bonus on attacks, gains exhaustion easily). Respects strength and the old rites; personally burns the dead. Prayed to Hel directly after the zombie attack. His authority over the raiding party eroded after the ruined farmstead — he defers to the group now. **Drove the Oath of Fellowship at the Nowhere docks** (Session 3) once Silorphin confirmed the War Clan colors. His vengeance is the lynchpin.
- **Siddhe** — A small child magically displaced from another world. Grandparents still alive in her homeland, which she describes as endless green farmland. Was playing outside their house when a bright light carried her to Kekel. Traveling with the party; Aesgor has promised to return her home. Can bring downed allies back to consciousness (some kind of innate ability). Revived Glug in the beach fight by physically jumping on her chest and bit-slapping her awake.

### NPCs at Nowhere
- **Igyll** — Tiefling. Curled horns, golden eyes, pointed teeth, light red skin. Formerly a quartermaster. Squats by a hide lean-to at the edge of town, dropping fingernails and toenails into a fire. Has a dread vision of a ship of nails (Naglfar). Offers to assemble a crew for **8 gp**. Holds a rumor on Captain Thornbeard (won't share without payment). Sold a unique-locations map to **the Dealer** at the Raiders Hall.
- **Silorphin** — Thin, short elf. Dockmaster at Nowhere. Confirmed the **War Clan** was in Nowhere a week before the party's arrival, bartering weapons for wheat. Witnessed the Oath of Fellowship and called it dramatic.
- **Jarl Beowyn** — Dwarven woman, long silver braided hair and beard. Found training her guards by knocking them flat. Offers paid dirt-work on **Toki** (the refugee leader). Philosophy: *"I gotta put them on their toes, even though I put them on their asses."*
- **Toki** — Halfling. Leader of the refugee tents outside Nowhere. *"More of a troublemaker than anything else."* Jarl Beowyn wants legal cover to arrest her.
- **Perrin and Narsh** — Human and half-orc shipbuilders at Shipyard #11. Sitting on an almost-complete **Explorer-class longship** whose captain skipped payment (1,000 gp to finish, 6,500 gp new).
- **Kinnikade the Rune Thrower** — Nine-foot figure of blue-black wood, thin arms, long white tunic. In a tree grove at #12. Draws a second rune beneath each PC's birth rune with a vision attached. **Kin to the wicker sentries from Kekel** (DM said so explicitly). *"There are things that even I cannot speak of."* Trust the visions, not him.
- **Captain Thornbeard** — Named only as a rumor (Igyll). Likely festival-challenge target.
- **The Dealer** — Operates out of the **Raiders Hall** in Nowhere. Bought Igyll's unique-locations map. Location of the Hall itself still unknown (Igyll can't enter it).
- **Sculpty the Grey** — Mentioned in passing. A creditor in Nowhere whom a beggar owed coin to.

- **Yursa**
- **Bardi** — Went down in round 2 of the beach fight (second archer volley).
- **Dispute**
- **Hercia**
- **Asta**

None of them proved useful in the beach battle. Hrolf's comment: *"They were literally meat shields. They just slowed down the enemy by a turn."*

### Enemies Encountered
- **The War Clan** (formerly "Unknown Enemy Clan") — Named in Session 3 by Silorphin the dockmaster of Nowhere. They were in Nowhere about a week before the party arrived, bartering weapons for wheat. Their clan sigil has been changed (the predator sigil from the beach was an old logo). Hrolf has sworn vengeance on the raider who led the beach attack — **before the snows melt**. Their commander's withdrawal order at the beach: *"We are done here. Let them die on the beach."*
- **The Tortured Zombie Raider** — A single animate corpse that attacked the party at the ruined farmstead past Kekel. Armed with battleaxe and shield, wore a rusted breastplate, had Undead Fortitude (AC 18). **No fingernails** — torn off, as if tortured before death. The dead are not supposed to rise in Grimnir. Hrolf was genuinely shaken and prayed to Hel.

## Locations

- **Kekel** — The "misplaced tower." Party were the first to explore it in generations (+1 Glory). Location of the white raven feather, the iron bust of a handsome man with lips sewn shut, and the augury globe. **Aesgor's rune vision points him back here — *"twice you must walk those halls."***
- **The Ash Desert / Fanged Sands** — Volcanic soil, ash, rock, and continuous snow. Land has no vegetation to speak of. Travel is miserable; food is scarce.
- **The Red Cairn Trading Post** — A long-derelict dock and trading stop. Nothing salvageable. Marked by a red-painted stone cairn signifying the end of a known trail.
- **The Ruined Farmstead ("Double Nowhere")** — Three weather-beaten wooden buildings on a hill. Former inhabitants murdered in their beds 2–4 years ago. Used by the party as emergency shelter. Now also the burial site of the zombie raider.
- **Nowhere (the real town)** — River town further up the tributary northeast of Kekel, two banks divided by the river. Wooden homes, half a dozen longships, farmland ringing sparse forest. Fairings (copper-per-ride rowboats) ferry between banks. Numbered map hooks: **#4** Eye of the Storm Feasting Hall (inn); **#6** Refugee tents (Toki); **#7** Shops/market (mundane goods, pawn shops at quarter value); **#8** Earl's Hall (Jarl Beowyn, prison, drunk tank); **#11** Shipyard (Perrin + Narsh + abandoned Explorer-class); **#12** Kinnikade's grove. Longship market ~6,500 gp new.
- **Falgore** — Place of white marble. Where the woman who cursed Gaynor still stands "everlasting" (per Kinnikade). Specific named destination.
- **The Well of Wisdom** — Home of the **Volva**. Tree-Hammer's destination per Kinnikade; the gray feather is the key.
- **The Raiders Hall** — A building somewhere in Nowhere. Home of **the Dealer**. Igyll cannot enter it. Exact location not yet found.

## Story

### Session 1 — The Misplaced Tower of Kekel
The party's boat was sabotaged; they killed the saboteurs and reached a ruined tower (Kekel). Inside, they found a child (Siddhe) hiding in debris, an augury globe showing visions of past/present/future, and a scuffle downstairs with wicker sentries attacking Hrolf and his companions. Loot included a moonstone gem, augury bones from dead rats, three healing vials, a **white raven feather**, and the **iron bust of a handsome man with his lips sewn shut**.

### Session 2 — Beach of the Slain and the Road to Nowhere
- **The Beach Battle.** A near-TPK tutorial for new squad combat mechanics. Enemy boarders from the unknown clan landed and burned both friendly longships along with their own. Volley fire dropped three of five party members in round 1. The party survived only via potions, Aesgor holding at 1 HP, Marzena's crossbow crit, Siddhe child-bit-slapping Glug conscious, and Tree-Hammer sharing his own healing potion. Enemy commander ordered withdrawal: *"Let them die on the beach."*
- **The Pyre and the Valkyries.** As the party burned the 35–40 dead, **only Aesgor** saw three winged, flame-haired women appear in the smoke to collect the slain. Gaynor named them immediately: the armies of the final battle are being reinforced. Tree-Hammer retrieved a **grayish feather** from their smoke; touching it gave him a vision of a peaceful silver-sea beach, and he spoke an old word — *luau*.
- **The Road to Nowhere.** Seven days east-northeast from Kekel through ash and snow. A red-stone trail cairn near a ruined trading post. A "mutated" moose (killed, yielded ~31 rations; nearly killed Marzena on a charge crit, saved by a retconned grapple). A frozen caravan of 3 dwarves and 2 elves — foreign displaced persons who froze to death in summer clothes, buried diamond dust, pearls, silver.
- **"Double Nowhere."** Hrolf led the party excitedly to what he remembered as the town of Nowhere. It was instead three weather-beaten wooden buildings on a hill — inhabitants murdered in their beds 2–4 years ago. After the ruined settlement, Hrolf's authority over the raiding party effectively ended.
- **The Storm and the Zombie.** A dark storm of biting wind and rock pellets drove the party back to shelter at the ruined farmstead. On Marzena's first watch, a zombie raider (AC 18, Undead Fortitude, missing fingernails) burst through the door. Marzena nearly died trying to solo it while the party slept. Glug finally killed it with a **flaming animal-skin rug thrown as a net**. Hrolf burned the corpse and prayed directly to Hel. **Session ended on the mystery of the walking dead.**

### Session 3 — The Oath and the Rune Thrower
- **Storm day at the farmstead.** The blizzard worsened; party rode it out. Two Roll20 mini-games (Tree-Hammer wins Ship Captain Crew for 1 gp from Hrolf; Marzena wins Beetle).
- **The tributary.** Two more days east-northeast. Glug, the Grimnir fish-kin, slipped into the water with Aesgor's Guidance and rolled an 18 net attack — 5 fish, one day's ration for the whole extended party.
- **Arrival at Nowhere.** River town. **Igyll** the tiefling at the edge describes Naglfar without naming it, points the party to Silorphin, Kinnikade, and the festival.
- **The Oath of Fellowship.** Silorphin confirms the **War Clan** sigil. Hrolf draws blood and swears vengeance "before the snows melt." Aesgor, Gaynor, and Marzena swear with him; Tree-Hammer refuses; Glug silent.
- **Jarl Beowyn's side-quest.** Paid dirt-work to incriminate Toki, the halfling refugee leader.
- **The Rune Thrower.** Kinnikade reads every PC, draws a second rune, delivers a vision: Gaynor → Falgore (curse-caster); Tree-Hammer → Volva of the Well of Wisdom (gray feather is the key); Marzena → ship of her own, return to her lands; Aesgor → twice to Kekel; Glug → a king pays a Varang, the blood of his family.
- **Level 2.** All five PCs.
- **Market and close.** Gaynor intimidates the gem merchant (22 Intimidation) for half-value on the moonstone, rest sold at quarter. 18 gp + 5 sp per member. Drunken-mead-horn moment narrowly avoided a bar fight. Path forward: festival, Captain Thornbeard.
