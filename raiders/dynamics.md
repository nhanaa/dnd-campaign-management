## Aesgor's Role in the Party
- **Primary Healer/Support** — Healing Word, Cure Wounds, Bless
- **Secondary Tank** — AC 18, medium armor + shield, decent HP
- **Explorer/Navigator** — Eye of the Navigator, Survival/Perception advantage in wilderness, swim speed
- **Scout (with Aware)** — Once/long rest burst of Perception/Investigation power

## Key NPCs

- **Hrolf** — Veteran Hallharn warrior. Same tribe as Aesgor (and Marzena). Damaged internally — no proficiency bonus on attacks, gains exhaustion when pushing himself. Authority has eroded as the raiding party has been whittled down; after "double nowhere" he effectively defers to the group. Prayed to Hel personally after the zombie attack. **In Session 3 he drove the Oath of Fellowship at Nowhere's docks** once Celorfin confirmed the Whar colors — bled his own palm and demanded the party swear with him to kill **Gulli**, the Whar raid leader, before the snows melt. Now also focused on getting Siddhe to the volv (aligned with Kinickan).
- **Siddhe** — Displaced child from another world. Traveling with the party. Aesgor has promised to find her way home. Innately able to stabilize downed allies; revived Glug in the beach fight by literally jumping on her chest and bit-slapping her awake.

### NPCs at Nowhere (Session 3)
- **Egill** — Female tiefling, former ship's quartermaster. Has the Naglfar dread-vision. Will assemble a crew for 8 gp; *"knows a thing or two"* and will share for the right price. Sold her **map of the world** to the Dealer at the Raid Leader's Hall.
- **Celorfin** — Male elf, dock steward. **Missing one arm.** Racist toward half-orcs; despises the refugees. Confirmed the Whar were in Nowhere a week before the party. Witnessed the Oath of Fellowship.
- **Beowyln** — Female dwarf, Jarl of Nowhere. Silver braid and beard. Trains by knocking guards down. Wants paid dirt on Toki. Found at the Great Hall (#8) — also jailhouse and **Stone Court house of worship** with alcoves per god.
- **Toki** — Halfling, leader of the refugee tents (#6). Beowyln's target.
- **Peron & Garsh** — Married couple at the shipyard (#11). Peron human male, Garsh half-orc male. Sitting on a half-built Explorer-class longship the party cannot afford (1,000 gp to finish, 6,500 gp new). Can also do **minor ship upgrades**.
- **Kinickan the Rune Thrower** — Male wicker, nine-foot wooden seer. Kin to the Kekel wicker sentries (DM said so). Reads runes and tattoos them; refuses to speak directly about more. **Insists Hrolf is right about Siddhe — protect her until she reaches the volv.** Trust the visions, not him.
- **Captain Thornbeard** (rumor only) — Likely festival ship-challenge target.
- **The Dealer** (rumor only) — Operates from the Raid Leader's Hall (a private club). Bought Egill's map of the world.

## Faction Relations

### Raider Clans
- **Hallharn** — Aesgor's clan. Known for Mother's Brew mead (sweet fruit honey mead).
- Other clans TBD as encountered.

### The Baendur
- Forest/hill-dwelling peoples. Historically subjugated by the Witchking, weakened after raiders overthrew him.

### Ironwood Witches
- Agents of the Witchking. Seeking to reassemble the Rainbow Spear.

### The Whar (formerly "Unknown Enemy Clan" / "War Clan")
- Named in Session 3 by Celorfin the dock steward of Nowhere.
- Attacked the Hallharn on the beach outside Kekel (session 2). Clan shield then carried a stylized stalking predator (tiger-shaped). They have since changed their sigil.
- Were in Nowhere a week before the party arrived, bartering weapons for wheat. They left.
- **Their raid leader is named Gulli** — he led the beach attack. **Hrolf has sworn to hunt him down and kill him** before the snows melt. The Oath of Fellowship binds Aesgor, Gaynor, and Marzena to the same; failure = Curse of the Exile. Tree-Hammer refused; Glug ambiguous.

### The Stone Court (Gods)
- Petrified Vanir, now divine. Aesgor draws power from the entire court.
- **Aldyhn** — Leader of the Vanir, Aesgor carries a yarn effigy to Aldyhn.

## Plot Threads

- **The Oath of Fellowship clock (active).** Aesgor, Gaynor, and Marzena sworn to kill **Gulli**, the Whar raid leader from the beach attack — *before the snows melt* — or suffer the Curse of the Exile. Tree-Hammer refused. Glug ambiguous. **The campaign's main hard deadline.**
- **Captain Thornbeard.** Named twice in Session 3 — by Egill as a rumor-for-sale and as a likely festival mark. Probable ship-challenge target.
- **The Feast of Serpents.** In **2 days**. Annual festival honoring the Stone Court for the crops (mediocre since the Long Winter). Challenge any captain for their vessel during it. Competition varies per captain. The party's primary path to a ship.
- **Naglfar (Egill's vision).** Massive ship of nails, the dead crewing every inch, half-ruined woman at the prow. Egill destroys nails to stop it being built. Not recognized in-world (DC 30 History). End-of-the-world material; Hel-adjacent.
- **The fingernail link.** Egill's nails-in-the-fire ritual + the zombie raider's torn-off fingernails = the same thing, almost certainly. Aesgor has the players' connection but no character knows it yet (no check passed).
- **Falgore.** White marble. Where Gaynor's curse-caster *"yet lives… everlasting."* Specific named destination from Kinickan.
- **The Volva of the Well of Wisdom.** Tree-Hammer's destination via the gray feather, per Kinickan. Where Siddhe must be brought (Hrolf and Kinickan agree).
- **Twice you must walk those halls.** Aesgor's Kinickan vision. Return trip to **Kekel** required.
- **The Varang, the king, and drawn swords.** Glug's vision. A king pays a clanless raider; Glug's family blood drawn. Personal-arc seed.
- **Toki & the refugee camp side-quest.** Beowyln wants legal cover to arrest Toki the halfling. Pay not promised. Aesgor has ethical concerns. Note: Celorfin (dock steward) also despises the refugees — there is a faction here.
- **The Dealer at the Raid Leader's Hall.** Egill sold her map of the world to him. The Hall is a *private club* — location not yet found.
- **Tree-Hammer's private vengeance-quest.** Reason he refused the Oath. Now explicit at the table.
- **Tree-Hammer's gray feather.** Still hidden from the party. Kinickan reacted to it harder than any other rune. The table noticed; the characters have not confronted it.
- **Kinickan's nature.** Kin to the wicker sentries from Kekel ("a much more refined version"). Trust the visions, not the seer.
- **Who raises the dead?** Zombies are not supposed to exist in Grimnir. The zombie raider at "double nowhere" was missing all its fingernails — tortured before death. Hrolf prayed directly to Hel. Likely connected to Naglfar.
- **Ragnarok's harvest.** Valkyries are actively collecting the slain from pyres to reinforce the armies of the final battle. Aesgor is the only one with clear sight of them; Gaynor understood the implication instantly.
- **The iron bust (lips sewn shut).** Carried from Kekel. Still unexplained.
- **Siddhe's home.** Endless green farmland — not Grimnir. Aesgor has sworn to return her. The seer-path now points at Kinickan and the Volva of the Well of Wisdom (Hrolf and Kinickan agree she must reach the volv).
