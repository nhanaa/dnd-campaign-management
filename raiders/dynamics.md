## Aesgor's Role in the Party
- **Primary Healer/Support** — Healing Word, Cure Wounds, Bless
- **Secondary Tank** — AC 18, medium armor + shield, decent HP
- **Explorer/Navigator** — Eye of the Navigator, Survival/Perception advantage in wilderness, swim speed
- **Scout (with Aware)** — Once/long rest burst of Perception/Investigation power

## Key NPCs

- **Hrolf** — Veteran Hallharn warrior. Same tribe as Aesgor (and Marzena). Damaged internally — no proficiency bonus on attacks, gains exhaustion when pushing himself. Authority has eroded as the raiding party has been whittled down; after "double nowhere" he effectively defers to the group. Prayed to Hel personally after the zombie attack. **In Session 3 he drove the Oath of Fellowship at Nowhere's docks** once Silorphin confirmed the War Clan colors — bled his own palm and demanded the party swear with him before the snows melt.
- **Siddhe** — Displaced child from another world. Traveling with the party. Aesgor has promised to find her way home. Innately able to stabilize downed allies; revived Glug in the beach fight by literally jumping on her chest and bit-slapping her awake.

### NPCs at Nowhere (Session 3)
- **Igyll** — Tiefling, former quartermaster. Has the Naglfar dread-vision. Will assemble a crew for 8 gp; holds a Captain Thornbeard rumor (unsold). Sold a unique-locations map to the Dealer at the Raiders Hall.
- **Silorphin** — Thin elf dockmaster. Confirmed the War Clan was in Nowhere a week before the party. Witnessed the Oath of Fellowship.
- **Jarl Beowyn** — Dwarven woman, silver braid and beard. Trains by knocking guards down. Wants paid dirt on Toki.
- **Toki** — Halfling, leader of the refugee tents. Beowyn's target.
- **Perrin & Narsh** — Shipwrights. Sitting on a half-built Explorer-class longship the party cannot afford (1,000 gp to finish, 6,500 gp new).
- **Kinnikade the Rune Thrower** — Nine-foot wooden seer. Kin to the Kekel wicker sentries (DM said so). Reads runes; refuses to speak directly. Trust the visions, not him.
- **Captain Thornbeard** (rumor only) — Likely festival ship-challenge target.
- **The Dealer** (rumor only) — Operates from the Raiders Hall. Bought Igyll's map.

## Faction Relations

### Raider Clans
- **Hallharn** — Aesgor's clan. Known for Mother's Brew mead (sweet fruit honey mead).
- Other clans TBD as encountered.

### The Baendur
- Forest/hill-dwelling peoples. Historically subjugated by the Witchking, weakened after raiders overthrew him.

### Ironwood Witches
- Agents of the Witchking. Seeking to reassemble the Rainbow Spear.

### The War Clan (formerly "Unknown Enemy Clan")
- Named in Session 3 by Silorphin the dockmaster of Nowhere.
- Attacked the Hallharn on the beach outside Kekel (session 2). Clan shield then carried a stylized stalking predator (tiger-shaped). They have since changed their sigil.
- Were in Nowhere a week before the party arrived, bartering weapons for wheat. They left.
- **Hrolf has sworn vengeance** on the raider who led the beach attack — to be killed before the snows melt. The Oath of Fellowship binds Aesgor, Gaynor, and Marzena to the same; failure = Curse of the Exile. Tree-Hammer refused; Glug ambiguous.

### The Stone Court (Gods)
- Petrified Vanir, now divine. Aesgor draws power from the entire court.
- **Aldyhn** — Leader of the Vanir, Aesgor carries a yarn effigy to Aldyhn.

## Plot Threads

- **The Oath of Fellowship clock (active).** Aesgor, Gaynor, and Marzena sworn to kill the War Clan raider who led the beach attack — *before the snows melt* — or suffer the Curse of the Exile. Tree-Hammer refused. Glug ambiguous. **The campaign's main hard deadline.**
- **Captain Thornbeard.** Named twice in Session 3 — by Igyll as a rumor-for-sale and as a likely festival mark. Probable ship-challenge target.
- **The Festival.** In a few days. Challenge any captain for their vessel. Competition varies per captain. The party's primary path to a ship.
- **Naglfar (Igyll's vision).** Massive ship of nails, the dead crewing every inch, half-ruined woman at the prow. Igyll destroys nails to stop it being built. Not recognized in-world (DC 30 History). End-of-the-world material; Hel-adjacent.
- **The fingernail link.** Igyll's nails-in-the-fire ritual + the zombie raider's torn-off fingernails = the same thing, almost certainly. Aesgor has the players' connection but no character knows it yet (no check passed).
- **Falgore.** White marble. Where Gaynor's curse-caster *"yet lives… everlasting."* Specific named destination from Kinnikade.
- **The Volva of the Well of Wisdom.** Tree-Hammer's destination via the gray feather, per Kinnikade. Possibly also where Siddhe's seer-return ends.
- **Twice you must walk those halls.** Aesgor's Kinnikade vision. Return trip to **Kekel** required.
- **The Varang, the king, and drawn swords.** Glug's vision. A king pays a clanless raider; Glug's family blood drawn. Personal-arc seed.
- **Toki & the refugee camp side-quest.** Jarl Beowyn wants legal cover to arrest Toki the halfling. Pay not promised. Aesgor has ethical concerns.
- **The Dealer at the Raiders Hall.** Igyll sold her unique-locations map to him. Hall location not yet found.
- **Tree-Hammer's private vengeance-quest.** Reason he refused the Oath. Now explicit at the table.
- **Tree-Hammer's gray feather.** Still hidden from the party. Kinnikade reacted to it harder than any other rune. The table noticed; the characters have not confronted it.
- **Kinnikade's nature.** Kin to the wicker sentries from Kekel ("a much more refined version"). Trust the visions, not the seer.
- **Who raises the dead?** Zombies are not supposed to exist in Grimnir. The zombie raider at "double nowhere" was missing all its fingernails — tortured before death. Hrolf prayed directly to Hel. Likely connected to Naglfar.
- **Ragnarok's harvest.** Valkyries are actively collecting the slain from pyres to reinforce the armies of the final battle. Aesgor is the only one with clear sight of them; Gaynor understood the implication instantly.
- **The iron bust (lips sewn shut).** Carried from Kekel. Still unexplained.
- **Siddhe's home.** Endless green farmland — not Grimnir. Aesgor has sworn to return her. The seer-path now points at Kinnikade and the Volva of the Well of Wisdom.
