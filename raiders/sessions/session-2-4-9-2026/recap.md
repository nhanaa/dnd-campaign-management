## Session Summary

Picking up at the ruined tower of Kekel, the battered remnants of the raiding crew walked out into a full-fledged beach battle against a boarding party from an unknown clan. What was billed as a "tutorial encounter" to show off the new war-squad, shield-wall, and archer-squad mechanics nearly became a TPK — three of five party members were dropped inside a single round as volleys of flaming arrows tore through the beach. The party clawed their way back with borrowed potions, a feral child bit-slapping Glug awake, and Hrolf's axe; the enemy eventually disengaged with the chilling order "let them die on the beach."

As the pyres of friend and foe burned, Aesgor alone caught the silhouettes of three winged, flame-haired women appearing in the smoke — Valkyries — collecting the slain to reinforce the armies of Ragnarok. A single gray feather drifted to Tree-Hammer after they left; touching it granted a vision of a peaceful silver-sea beach, and an old word rose unbidden in his mind: *luau*. The party then turned east-northeast, chasing Hrolf's faded memory of a town called Nowhere, through days of ash and snow, a red stone cairn marking a long-dead trade post, and a brutal mutated moose that nearly flattened Marzena in a single charge (saved only by a retconned grapple — "activate the Omega 13").

The "town" Hrolf remembered turned out to be three weather-beaten buildings — their owners long-murdered in their beds, animals tied up and starved. After pushing onward into an incoming sand-and-cold storm, the party was forced to shelter back in the ruined farmstead. That night, on Marzena's watch, a lone zombie raider smashed through the door — silent, fingernails torn from its hands as if tortured before death. It nearly killed her while the rest of the party slept through the fight, and only Glug's creative use of a burning animal-skin rug and a flaming net attack finally put it down. Hrolf, shaken, prayed to Hel herself to stop sending her creatures — because the dead do not rise in this world. Not supposed to, anyway.

## Key Events

### Act I — The Battle on the Beach
- **Recap & loot sort** (Vidaltje): Moonstone gem, augury bones from the rats, three healing vials (one already used in the tower fight), two curiosities — the **white raven feather** and the **iron bust of a handsome man with lips sewn shut**.
- **The landing**: Enemy longship lands, two friendly longships and the enemy hull all set aflame by flaming arrows. Corpses already cover the ground, swiftly buried in wet snow.
- **Tactical debut**: DM introduces War Squad (4-man melee), Shield Wall Squad (3-man defensive), and Archer Squads firing AoE volleys from the enemy longship 100 ft offshore.
- **Round 1 near-wipe**: First archer volley (DC 16 Dex, 9 piercing / half on success) drops Glug and Marzena, leaves Aesgor at 1 HP. Second volley (11 piercing) takes out Bardi of the crew.
- **Hrolf and Siddhe arrive late** — Hrolf hands his potion to Glug, Siddhe attempts to stabilize and fails (twice).
- **Turning point**: Aesgor at 1 HP walks up clutching his holy symbol and drops a war-squad raider with his light crossbow — *"Shoot the war squad — the one man left — with his light crossbow. 16, does that hit? 16 is a hit, yes. Damage: 10. Beautiful. You see, it's not so bad — you just killed another one."* Marzena stands the wall and drops two more with a 9-piercing crit; Tree-Hammer feeds his potion to Gaynor (*"I'm doing way too much healing for a barbarian"*).
- **Enemy withdrawal**: The distant commander yells, *"We are done here. Let them die on the beach,"* and the enemy ships pull away from shore.
- **Final cleanup**: Glug returns and joins the line; Tree-Hammer charges and javelins for 8; Marzena finishes the last man — *"Your friends chose the sea, you chose the soil. I'll make sure you're six feet down."*
- **Siddhe's wake-up**: Earlier, the child had run onto Glug's chest and literally bit-slapped her conscious. ("Wake up! We still need you!")
- **The enemy shield**: Carried a stylized predator — long and stalking, more savage than a wolverine, *vaguely* tiger-shaped but not definitely so. Unknown clan to any of the party or Hrolf.

### Act II — The Pyre and the Valkyries
- Party builds a proper funeral pyre for the ~35–40 dead (their crew + enemy boarders). Hrolf insists: *"Warriors who fought in battle are to be burnt."*
- **Aesgor alone** spots three grim-faced, armored women appearing within the flames. The DM's narration: *"Large bird-like wings are tucked against their bodies. Their long hair erupts out from behind them as a flame itself."* They beat their wings once and vanish, returning one by one to collect the slain.
- Gaynor, without prompting: *"The armies for the final battle are being reinforced with those deaths."*
- **The Grayish Feather**: After the Valkyries are gone, Tree-Hammer sees something fall. The DM: *"As you touch it, you have a brief glimpse of a beach caressed by the gentle waters of a silvery sea. It is warm and peaceful here."* Tree-Hammer, quietly: *"I'm reminded of an old word. **Luau.**"*
- The party gains **1 Glory Point** for being the first generation to explore the misplaced tower of Kekel.

### Act III — Night in the Tower, and a Promise
- **Watch order**: Marzena → Aesgor → Tree-Hammer → Glug. (Gaynor, predictably, takes none.) Glug eventually falls asleep on fourth watch.
- **Aesgor comforts Siddhe**: The child wakes crying on Aesgor's watch, missing her grandparents. Aesgor promises they will find a way to take her home. Siddhe describes the world she comes from — *huge tracks of farmland, plains of green* — something none of them have ever dreamed possible.

### Act IV — The Road to Nowhere
- **Day 4–6**: Three days of ash-and-snow trudging east-northeast from Kekel toward a town Hrolf once visited called **Nowhere**.
- **Day 6 — The Red Cairn**: A pile of red-painted stones on the snow. Hrolf identifies them: *trail markers, red means the end of a trail.* They find a ruined dock and a derelict trading caravan stop. Nothing salvageable.
- **Day 7 — The "Mutated" Moose**:
  - Aesgor (+3 Survival) tracks a large creature through outcroppings.
  - Staged ambush: Tree-Hammer sneaks forward as *"a normal-sized forest creature, just a regular squirrel."* Gaynor holds Hex + Eldritch Blast to trigger the attack.
  - Tree-Hammer's opening grapple **fails** — the moose shakes him off.
  - The moose charges Marzena and **crits** with its antlers: 20 + 6 charge damage = **26**. Marzena has 12 HP. She would be dead instantly.
  - **"Activate the Omega 13."** Vidaltje invokes a retcon; Tree-Hammer's grapple is retroactively allowed to succeed, the moose never reaches Marzena. ("Any other campaign I would have still revived that shit...")
  - Gaynor finishes it with a hexed Eldritch Blast.
  - **Butchering**: Marzena (14), Aesgor (crit 18 → 8 days), Tree-Hammer (1 → 1 day), Gaynor (final → 12 days). **Total: ~31 rations**, enough to feed the extended party of 7 for nearly a week.
- **Day 8 — The Frozen Caravan**: Bones of three dwarves and two elves, none dressed for winter. Displaced foreigners who froze to death. One had tried to bury something — **pouch of diamond dust (25 gp), two pearls (100 gp each), 11 silver pieces**.

### Act V — "It's Double Nowhere"
- **Day 9**: Hrolf excitedly leads the party to what he remembers as Nowhere. It is not a town. Three weather-beaten wooden buildings on the slopes of a hill. Fields gone to rock. Roofs collapsing. *"It is nowhere, but it's... oh, you know what I mean." / "It's double nowhere."*
- **Search**: Animals starved in their ties; the former inhabitants were **murdered in their beds 2–4 years ago**. A single crate yields 2 days of old rations. Nothing else of value.
- Hrolf is shaken — *"a bad omen to stay here"* — and defers leadership: the raiding party is now too small for him to command.
- Party presses on toward the real Nowhere, eastward.

### Act VI — The Storm and the Zombie Raider
- **Storm**: 30–40 minutes out from the farmstead, a dark storm sweeps in from the east. Warm rain turns to biting wind; sand and rock whip across exposed flesh. Hrolf: *"I smell death, and she is coming."*
- **Retreat to the farmstead**. CON saves vs. the debris storm: Glug (the Grimnir fish-kin) fails, takes 5 cold damage; rest take 2.
- **House rule**: Sleeping in medium armor requires a DC 16 CON save to get a full long rest. Aesgor swaps scale for gambeson; Glug does the same with his chainmail; both set their shields aside.
- **Marzena's first watch** — the door **bursts open**.

### The Zombie Raider
- **A lone zombie raider** — armored, shield, battleaxe — bursts into the farmhouse. Marzena is surprise-attacked.
- **Critical error**: Marzena tries to solo the zombie without yelling for help. The rest of the party rolls Perception with disadvantage to wake up; Gaynor deliberately sleeps through most of the fight ("recharging spells on a short rest").
- Tree-Hammer, dryly: *"All you had to do was scream 'we're under attack' and everyone would have won."*
- **Round 1–2**: Zombie has **AC 18** and brutal hit points for a level 1 party. Marzena gets cut down to zero. Glug finally wakes up, throws spear for 8 piercing, and triggers the alarm.
- **Glug's save**: Picks up shield, jumps to Marzena, Lay on Hands for 5. Aesgor follows with Sacred Flame (saved) then Healing Word for another 5.
- **The rug gambit**: Glug grabs an animal-skin rug from the floor, **lights it in the fire**, and tries to throw it using net-attack rules as a flaming entangle. First throw misses. Hrolf intervenes (gaining exhaustion) to land 2 damage with his Skagox.
- **The kill**: Marzena crits with Skagox, Gaynor crits with hexed Eldritch Blast — but **Undead Fortitude** saves it at 1 HP. Glug's second flaming-net attempt with flank advantage hits; d6 fire damage (3) — zombie fails the DC 8 Undead Fortitude check and finally collapses, staring as the flames take it.
- **Inspiration** awarded to Glug for next session for the flaming-rug maneuver.
- **Post-fight examination**: The zombie had **no fingernails** — torn off, as if tortured before it died.
- **Hrolf burns the corpse** on a makeshift pyre, prays to **Hel**, asking her to accept this abomination and to *"please, Hel, do not send any more of your creatures to us. We are not a threat to you."* The dead coming back is **not supposed to happen in this world.**
- **Loot**: A rusted but salvageable breastplate that *reeks of death* — anyone who wears it must roll CON saves against vomiting.

**Cliffhanger**: The party stands over the burning zombie in a storm-lashed ruin, far from Nowhere, with no answers to how a corpse rose to fight them.

### Things Aesgor knows that the party does not
- **The Valkyries are real and they are harvesting.** If Gaynor's interpretation is right, the armies of the final battle are being reinforced with every death. Aesgor saw this with his own eyes. No one else did.
- The **white raven feather** from Kekel last session + the **grayish feather** Tree-Hammer got from the Valkyrie smoke this session are a matched pair of something. Both feathers trigger on death/afterlife scenes. Aesgor should watch for a third.
- The **iron bust of a handsome man with lips sewn shut** (from Kekel) is still unexplained. Aesgor is carrying it. Ask about it at the first real settlement.
- Siddhe's homeworld is **not Grimnir**. It sounds like a place of plenty and green — possibly *our* world. She was teleported here by a bright light. Other people have apparently done the same.
- **Aesgor has made a personal promise** to return Siddhe home. This is a character anchor. Any divination magic (Augury, later Clairvoyance, Commune, Locate Creature) should be spent working toward this if there's no active crisis.

### What Aesgor is thinking about
- **The tiger-shaped sigil** on the enemy clan shield. Ask literally everyone about it — Hrolf didn't recognize it, but someone in a real town might. Might be connected to the Witchking or something newer.
- **Who is torturing corpses and reanimating them?** The zombie had its fingernails ripped out *before* it died. That is not ordinary necromancy — it suggests ritual. Combined with the fact that the dead are not supposed to rise in this world at all, this points to an outside force.
- The **Stone Court** probably doesn't have a direct answer to the zombie question. Hel specifically manages the dead; Hrolf prayed to her directly. Aesgor might consider doing the same, or asking Aldyhn via his yarn effigy.
- The **Rune of the Vigilant** — is it already active? The Valkyrie sight feels like a Vigilant One perk surfacing. If so, a new rune may be appearing soon.
- Need to pick up more spell slots at level 2. Current loadout felt thin: Bless, Cure Wounds, Healing Word, Shield of Faith — plus Fog Cloud and Expeditious Retreat as domain spells. For the kinds of encounters this DM is running, **Healing Word as a bonus action** is the single most important slot. Prioritize it every day.
- Glory is **1**. The DM clearly intends the glory system as long-term social currency. Start thinking about who to spend it on and when.
