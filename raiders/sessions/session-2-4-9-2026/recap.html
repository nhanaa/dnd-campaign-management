Session Summary

Picking up at the ruined tower of Kekel, the battered remnants of the raiding crew walked out into a full-fledged beach battle against a boarding party from an unknown clan. What was billed as a tutorial for new squad mechanics nearly became a TPK — three of five party members were dropped inside a single round as volleys of flaming arrows tore the beach apart. The party clawed their way back with borrowed potions, a feral child bit-slapping Glug awake, and Hrolf's axe. The enemy eventually disengaged with the chilling order, "let them die on the beach."

As the pyres burned, Aesgor alone caught the silhouettes of three winged, flame-haired women in the smoke — Valkyries — collecting the slain to reinforce the armies of Ragnarok. A gray feather drifted to Tree-Hammer after they left. Touching it gave him a vision of a peaceful silver-sea beach, and an old word rose unbidden: luau.

Then, days of ash-and-snow trudging east-northeast, a near-death "mutated" moose ("Activate the Omega 13"), a murdered farmstead that turned out to be "double nowhere," and a final, horrifying encounter with a single zombie raider — a thing that should not exist in Grimnir. Hrolf, shaken, prayed directly to Hel.

Timeline

Act I · Combat · The Beach

The Battle on the Beach

Enemy longship lands. Two friendly hulls and the enemy's own ship are already burning. The party meets the new squad mechanics at level 1 — and nearly dies to them.

  • Round 1 archer volley (DC 16 Dex, 9 dmg) drops Glug and Marzena. Aesgor at 1 HP.
  • Second volley for 11 piercing takes out Bardi of the crew.
  • Aesgor clutches his holy symbol and walks up at 1 HP to drop a war-squad raider with his crossbow.
  • Marzena's 9-damage crit drops two raiders with one shot.
  • Tree-Hammer feeds his potion to Gaynor: "Doing way too much healing for a barbarian."
  • Enemy commander calls the retreat: "We are done here. Let them die on the beach."
  • Marzena drops the last raider: "Your friends chose the sea, you chose the soil. I'll make sure you're six feet down."
  • Siddhe revived Glug earlier by physically jumping on her chest and bit-slapping her awake.
Act II · Mystery · The Pyre

Valkyries in the Smoke

The party builds a proper funeral pyre for ~35–40 bodies. Hrolf insists that warriors who fought in battle must be burned.

  • Only Aesgor catches three grim-faced, armored women with bird-like wings and hair like flame appearing in the smoke. They beat their wings once and vanish, returning to collect the slain one by one.
  • Gaynor, unprompted: "The armies for the final battle are being reinforced with those deaths."
  • Tree-Hammer retrieves a grayish feather that fell after the last Valkyrie vanished. Touching it gives a brief vision of a "beach caressed by the gentle waters of a silvery sea. It is warm and peaceful here." Tree-Hammer speaks an old word: luau.
  • Party earns +1 Glory Point for being the first generation to explore Kekel.
Act III · Roleplay · Night in the Tower

Siddhe's Homesickness

Watches pass without incident. On second watch, Aesgor finds Siddhe crying quietly in the dark.

  • She misses her grandparents and worries about her grandfather's failing legs.
  • She describes her homeworld — endless green farmland, huge tracts of fertile soil. None of the party have ever dreamed such a place existed.
  • Aesgor promises to find a way to take her home.
  • Glug eventually falls asleep on fourth watch. Nothing happens.
Act IV · Exploration · The Road

Three Days to the Cairn, Seven Days to Nothing

  • Day 6: A pile of red-painted stones. Hrolf identifies them as trail markers — red means the end of a trail. A ruined trading post with a broken dock. Nothing salvageable.
  • Day 7: The "mutated" moose hunt (see Combat).
  • Day 8: Bones of three dwarves and two elves, frozen to death in summer clothes. Displaced foreigners. One had tried to bury a pouch of treasure.
Act IV · Combat · The Moose

The "Mutated" Moose

A staged ambush that immediately went sideways.

  • Tree-Hammer's opening grapple fails — the moose shakes him off.
  • The moose charges Marzena and crits for 20 + 6 = 26 damage. Marzena has 12 HP.
  • "Activate the Omega 13." Vidaltje invokes a retcon; Tree-Hammer's grapple is retroactively allowed.
  • Gaynor finishes it with a hexed Eldritch Blast.
  • Four Survival checks yield ~31 rations total. Aesgor crits his butchering for 8 days.
Act V · Exploration · "Nowhere"

It's Double Nowhere

Hrolf excitedly leads the party to what he remembers as Nowhere. It is not a town.

  • Three weather-beaten wooden buildings on a hill. Fields gone to rock. Roofs collapsing.
  • Inhabitants were murdered in their beds 2–4 years ago. Animals starved in their ties.
  • Gaynor: "It's double nowhere."
  • Hrolf is shaken, declares the place a bad omen, and defers leadership — the raiding party is now too small for him to command.
  • One crate yields 2 days of old rations.
Act VI · Storm · The Retreat

The Storm Drives Them Back

Thirty minutes past the farmstead, a dark storm of biting wind, warm rain, and wind-driven rock pellets forces the party to retreat to shelter. Hrolf smells death coming. CON saves — Glug (the Grimnir) takes 5 cold damage, the rest take 2. They make it back to the ruined farmstead and set watches.

Marzena's first watch. The door bursts open.

Act VII · Combat · The Dead Raider

The Thing That Should Not Exist

A lone zombie raider — armored, shield, battleaxe, AC 18 — bursts through the door and ambushes Marzena mid-watch. She tries to handle it alone instead of screaming for help, and the rest of the party (wearing their bedrolls instead of armor) rolls Perception at disadvantage to wake up. Gaynor deliberately sleeps through two rounds to conserve spells.

  • Rounds 1–2: Marzena vs. the zombie, solo. Slashing and piercing barely scratch it. She's cut down to zero.
  • Glug wakes and throws a spear for 8 piercing, triggering the alarm.
  • Tree-Hammer rages, dashes to stand up, misses.
  • Glug's save: Picks up his shield, jumps to Marzena, Lay on Hands for 5. Aesgor follows up with Sacred Flame (saved) then Healing Word for 5 more.
  • The rug gambit: Glug grabs an animal-skin rug, lights it in the fire, and throws it using net-attack rules as a flaming entangle. First throw misses.
  • Hrolf intervenes with his Skagox (gaining exhaustion) for 2 damage.
  • The final round: Marzena crits with the Skagox, Gaynor crits with a hexed Eldritch Blast — but Undead Fortitude saves the zombie at 1 HP.
  • The kill: Glug's second flaming-net attempt hits with flank advantage for 3 fire damage. The zombie fails its DC 8 Undead Fortitude check and crumples as the flames consume it, staring back as it goes.
  • Glug earns inspiration for next session.

Post-fight: The corpse has no fingernails — torn off, as if tortured before it died. Hrolf burns it on a separate pyre and prays directly to Hel: "Please, Hel, do not send any more of your creatures to us. We are not a threat to you." In Grimnir, the dead do not rise. Whatever this was, it was not supposed to happen.

The only salvage: a rusted breastplate that reeks of death. No one claims it.

Combat Encounters

1. The Beach Battle

War Squad (4) · Shield Wall Squad (3) · 2× Archer Squads (5) · Commander

A near-TPK tutorial encounter. Three players dropped in round 1 to volley fire. The party's five allied crew did functionally nothing — "they just slowed down the enemy by a turn." Turning point came when Aesgor and Marzena held the line at 1 HP while the rest recovered. The enemy eventually withdrew on the commander's order.

2. The "Mutated" Moose

1× Moose (not actually mutated — party joke) · Charge 3d6+ · Retcon required

A staged ambush with Tree-Hammer as stealth scout. The moose's charge crit would have instantly killed Marzena — saved only by an Omega 13 retcon allowing Tree-Hammer's grapple to retroactively succeed. Finished by a hexed Eldritch Blast from Gaynor. Yielded enough meat to feed the party for nearly a week.

3. The Zombie Raider

1× Zombie Raider · AC 18 · Undead Fortitude · Armored, shield, battleaxe

Marzena was surprise-attacked on her watch and — critically — tried to solo it without yelling for help. The rest of the party rolled Perception at disadvantage to wake. Gaynor deliberately slept through two rounds to conserve spell slots. Marzena was cut to zero; Glug saved her with Lay on Hands. Aesgor followed with Healing Word.

The killing blow required Glug's creative use of an animal-skin rug, lit on fire, thrown as a net attack for 3 fire damage — which busted the zombie's Undead Fortitude save (DC 8) on the second attempt. Earned Glug inspiration for next session.

The corpse had no fingernails — torn off, as if tortured before death. Hrolf burned it on a separate pyre and prayed directly to Hel. The dead do not rise in this world. It is not supposed to happen.

Pax's Notes — Aesgor's Journal

Private notes, observations, and things to think about. Pax's notes only — not shared with the rest of the party.

What Aesgor did this session

  • Walked out of Kekel alongside Marzena into the beach fight — "I shall lead. We'll go together."
  • Entered the fight with zero spell slots (burned in the tower last session). Most of the first combat was crossbow and Sacred Flame.
  • Dropped to 1 HP from the first archer volley, then calmly walked forward and dropped a war-squad raider with his crossbow. "With a hand clutching his entire body now that he's injured, he's just going to walk slowly up and shoot the war squad..."
  • Drank his own healing potion instead of saving Marzena (the party teased him as a "selfish cleric"). In-character: this was the right call — he had to be standing to heal anyone later.
  • Later crossbow shots went wide. "A school should just stick to spellcasting."
  • Only Aesgor saw the Valkyries — three grim-faced, armored women with bird-like wings and hair like flame, appearing in the pyre smoke to collect the slain. Nobody else noticed until he pointed it out. The Rune of the Vigilant may be reacting to proximity to Ragnarok-adjacent events.
  • Took second watch. Found Siddhe crying quietly, missing her grandparents. Promised her he would find a way to take her home. "It smells like a paradise," when she described endless green farmland — nothing in Grimnir is like that.
  • Best Survival in the party (+3). Tracked the moose on Day 7, then butchered it for 8 days of rations on a crit. ("I'm just eating fucking chunks of it as I go.")
  • Originated the "mutated moose" running joke — "If it turns out this moose is actually mutated or something..."
  • Cast Sacred Flame on the zombie raider (saved) and Healing Word on Marzena during the zombie fight.
  • First to theorize about the zombie: "It's a product of someone turning it after torturing it. Maybe."

Things Aesgor knows that the party does not

  • The Valkyries are real and they are harvesting. If Gaynor's interpretation is right, the armies of the final battle are being reinforced with every death. Aesgor saw this with his own eyes. No one else did.
  • The white raven feather from Kekel last session and the grayish feather Tree-Hammer got from the Valkyrie smoke this session are a matched pair of something. Both feathers trigger on death/afterlife scenes. Watch for a third.
  • The iron bust of a handsome man with lips sewn shut (from Kekel) is still unexplained. Aesgor is carrying it. Ask about it at the first real settlement.
  • Siddhe's homeworld is not Grimnir. It sounds like a place of plenty and green — possibly our world. She was teleported here by a bright light. Other people have apparently done the same.
  • Aesgor has made a personal promise to return Siddhe home. This is a character anchor. Any divination magic (Augury, later Clairvoyance, Commune, Locate Creature) should be spent working toward this if there's no active crisis.

What Aesgor is thinking about

  • The tiger-shaped sigil. Ask literally everyone about it at the first real settlement. Hrolf didn't recognize it. Might be connected to the Witchking, or something newer.
  • Who is torturing corpses and reanimating them? The zombie's fingernails were ripped out before it died. That is not ordinary necromancy. Combined with the fact that the dead are not supposed to rise in this world, this points to an outside force and a deliberate ritual.
  • The Stone Court probably doesn't have a direct answer. Hel manages the dead; Hrolf prayed to her directly. Aesgor might consider doing the same, or asking Aldyhn via the yarn effigy.
  • The Rune of the Vigilant — is it already active? The Valkyrie sight feels like a Vigilant One perk surfacing. If so, a new rune may be appearing soon.
  • Need to pick up more spell slots at level 2. Current loadout is thin — Healing Word as a bonus action is the single most important slot. Prioritize it every day.
  • Glory is 1. The DM clearly intends the glory system as long-term social currency. Start thinking about who to spend it on and when.

  • Is there a way to test Siddhe's memory for details that would identify her home — a map, a constellation, a language word? Eye of the Navigator could tie into this.
  • The white raven from Aware — has it shown up since Kekel? If not, why not?
