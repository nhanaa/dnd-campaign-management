## Session Summary

After burning the zombie at the ruined farmstead, the party rode out the worsening blizzard for another day — passing the time with two Roll20-built games of chance (Ship Captain Crew and Beetle) and a dice-betting bit Hrolf attempted and the party correctly refused. Two more days east-northeast through ash and snow brought them to a tributary where **Glug** (the Grimnir, fish-kin) slipped into the water with Guidance from Aesgor and speared five fish for the extended party, and from there up to the real town of **Nowhere** — a two-sided river settlement of longships, refugees, and six pages of plot hooks.

At the edge of town they met **Igyll**, a tiefling former quartermaster dropping handfuls of fingernails and toenails into a fire to stop the building of a dread-vision ship — "a massive ship made of nails, with the dead hovering every square inch of it, sailing towards land with a woman, half her face ruined, standing at the prow." She recommended three paths forward: the dockmaster, the rune thrower, and the upcoming festival's captain-ship challenge. At the docks, **Silorphin** — a thin short elf — confirmed that a ship flying the enemy clan's colors had been here days ago bartering with what he now called the **War Clan**. Hrolf drew his dagger, bled his palm into the dock boards, and swore vengeance "before the snows melt" — then turned and demanded the party swear the **Oath of Fellowship** with him. Aesgor, Gaynor, and Marzena swore it; Tree-Hammer refused and stayed free. Glug did not speak to it either way.

The rest of the session was town-exploration: **Jarl Beowyn** (a silver-braided dwarven woman drilling her troops in the Earl's Hall) offered paid dirt-work to incriminate **Toki**, the halfling running the refugee tents; **Perrin and Narsh** at the shipyard (11) showed off the half-finished Explorer-class longship abandoned by its captain — 6,500 gold new, 1,000 owed to complete, impossible on the party's 56 gp; and **Kinnikade the Rune Thrower**, a nine-foot figure of blue-black wood, drew a new rune beneath each PC's birth rune and delivered a cryptic vision tied to it. Everyone leveled up to 2 on the spot. The party crossed the river, fumbled through a pawn-shop market that wouldn't pay more than a quarter value, and bailed a woman out of the river who'd taken Kinnikade's vision literally and walked off the dock. The session closed with the path forward explicit: learn about the captains, then win a festival challenge for their ship.

## Key Events

### Act I — The Storm, the Dice, the Fish
- **Storm**: The blizzard that drove them back to the farmstead last session worsened overnight. The party agreed unanimously to ride out the day rather than risk exhaustion checks in a whiteout. *"Because we shouldn't just start getting… once you fail the third one, then yeah,"* Aesgor on the exhaustion math.
- **Aesgor's long-rest afflicted rolls**: Day-1 storm rest **9**, Day-2 morning-ritual rest **15** — both safe, no psychic damage. (The Tuss button has a low proc rate, but the DM is *very* excited about what happens when it fires.)
- **Ship Captain Crew**: DM introduced a full Roll20-built dice mini-game ("I hope you guys enjoy this because I spent a fuck ton of time getting this ready"). Five tokens — blue, green, red, yellow, black (plus an unused mauve for an absent player Alex). **Tree-Hammer won** outright and earned a single gold piece from Hrolf; Aesgor's last turn landed him at exactly 51, one shy of a tie-beating win.
- **Beetle**: Second mini-game, build a bug from d6 rolls. **Marzena won**, **Gaynor** second. **Tree-Hammer finished last** — *"I take last on this one. From first to last, really."* A fourth mini-game (chess) was offered and unanimously declined.
- **Hrolf's silver-per-fail bet**: Hrolf offered to wager a silver per round Tree-Hammer failed to roll a 6, for one silver on success. The party correctly identified it as *"literally a stupid bet"* and passed.
- **Day 10–11 travel**: Storm abated. Two days plodding east-northeast, rations running low as Siddhe and Hrolf keep drawing from the pool. Tree-Hammer dropped to one ration. *"We've been giving them to these NPCs that have been leeching resources from us."*
- **Day 12 — the tributary**: First trees in days (stunted). **Tree-Hammer scaled a sparse pine in squirrel form** to forage (Beastborn). Aesgor cast Guidance on him — still no acorns, no pine nuts, nothing. DM narration: *"Aesgor grabs your tail and says, Take my love!"*
- **Glug's fishing play**: **Glug slipped into the river** as the party's Grimnir fish-kin, with Aesgor casting Guidance on him before the dive. Marzena named the mechanical reality aloud — *"he is a fish thing."* Glug ambushed the fish with advantage on his net attack (Ambusher trait, underwater stealth), rolled an **18**, and pulled in **five fish** — one day's ration for the whole extended party.
- **Marzena crossbow-fished** for the record. (*"I fucking tried to fish with my crossbow."* — It did not work mechanically.)
- Hrolf remembered that Nowhere is actually further up the tributary, not at his ruined farmstead of two sessions ago. The party crossed and continued north.

### Act II — Arrival at the Real Nowhere
- **Day 13 — Nowhere at last**: A river town cut in two, wooden homes clustered on both banks, half a dozen longships moored mid-stream, farmland ringing sparse forest. Dwarves, elves, dragonborn, and humans mixing in the rocky streets. A light dusting of snow from the storm they'd just ridden out. After three sessions of ash desert, this is the first real settlement.
- **The tiefling at the edge**: *"A woman with curled horns, light red skin, and pointed teeth at the very edge of town… She stares at you with golden eyes, but does not rise to greet you, focused as she is in dropping something into the flames."* Tree-Hammer approached. Gaynor: *"Poacher and greeter, anyway. Greetings, Kellion."*
- **Her name is Igyll**. Tiefling. Formerly a **quartermaster**. Now she throws fingernails and toenails into a fire all day.
- **The nail detail** — the DM reminded the table that the zombie raider from last session had its nails torn off before death. Aesgor asked if any of them would know what the nails-burning ritual meant in-world; DM said no (no check passed). The link is on the table but not yet something the party *knows*.

### Act III — The Ship Made of Nails
- **Igyll's vision**: *"I keep having visions or dreams, nightmares of a ship, massive ship made of nails, with the dead hovering every square inch of it, sailing towards land with a woman, half her face ruined, standing at the prowl. She is the end of us all."* She destroys nails to try to stop the ship being built.
- Someone at the table offered the classic *"you should eat less cheese at night"* deflection. Igyll: *"Cheese is too good. I'll never stop eating cheese."*
- Marzena tried a History check at DC 30 to identify what she was describing. Got nowhere. (**Naglfar** — the Norse ship of the dead — was not recognized in-world.)
- **The three threads Igyll hands them**:
  - The **Dockmaster (Silorphin)** — knows about ships.
  - The **Rune Thrower (Kinnikade)** — the wisest person she knows.
  - The **Festival** in a few days — *"You can probably challenge another captain for their vessel if you're good enough."*
- **Igyll's offer**: For 8 gold she'll assemble a crew. She used to have a map with unique locations but sold it to the **Dealer** at the **Raiders Hall** (a place she can't enter). She's also heard a rumor about **Captain Thornbeard** — won't share it without payment. Marzena offered a vial of lead-based ink (worth 10 gp); Igyll refused. *"She literally puts her hand out. 'I guess you'll have to find out, wouldn't you? Give me your nails.'"*
- The party moved on. Igyll threw more nails into the fire.

### Act IV — The Docks, the War Clan, the Oath
- **Silorphin**: *"A thin, short elf barks at a mixed assembly of humans, gnomes, and half-orcs working along the waterfront."* Dockmaster.
- Gaynor showed him the enemy shield from the beach. Silorphin: *"Yeah, of course I've seen that. There was a ship here just a few days ago, maybe a week, with those colors. They sold us wheat, they bartered for weapons, and they left. They call themselves the War Clan."*
- **Hrolf snapped**. *"The war clan, you say? Damn them! I've had enough of them and their blatant disregard for the rest of the clans."* He drew his dagger, sliced his palm, spilled blood on the dock. *"Aldehan, hear me. I swear upon my blood that before the snows melt, I will slay the raider from the war clan for his violence against my people. I swear this vengeance against him."*
- He turned to the party. *"Will you join me? Swear it and oath to me and one another. Share your sword, your shield, your spells, and I will do the same. And Skalds will sing stories of us together for the glory we shall earn throughout the ages."*
- **Aesgor, instantly**: *"Aesgor for vengeance. Yes."* Then the formal oath, palm slit: *"I am Aesgor, and I swear upon my life, my clan, and my honor that I shall uphold the oath of fellowship with these companions who stand with me."* Immediately after: *"I'm losing too much blood, so someone else please go ahead."*
- **Gaynor**, grimly: *"More corpses on my path to Valhalla. I am Gaynor, and I swear upon my life, my clan, and my honor that I shall be with all the oath of fellowship of these companions who stand beside me."*
- **Marzena** swore twice — first on her clan the Halharn, then *"I swear upon my clan, my honor, and the Lady Luta. I shall hold them as a fellowship."* First time **Lady Luta** has been spoken aloud; Marzena's patron deity.
- **Tree-Hammer REFUSED**. *"I spilled blood over a few words, but I've got a hammer and you're going to need weapons."* *"I mean, I ain't cutting my hand, and I ain't speaking an oath. I fix weapons, and you're going to need weapons fixed. I'm already in a quest for vengeance, and another will only add to my time."* DM confirmed: *"The only one who's not actually swearing the oath is Tree-Hammer."* He'll help. He isn't bound.
- **Glug** didn't speak to it. Paladin oath concerns were raised (Gaynor: *"If you're a paladin, you're going to have double oath, or you may be breaking your oath by taking another oath"*) — outcome unclear in play, table moved on.
- **The Curse of the Exile** awaits any oath-taker who fails to kill the War Clan raider before the snows melt.
- Silorphin to Hrolf: *"Wow, that was really dramatic, guys."* Pointed them to the shipbuilders at 11 and the upcoming festival competition.

### Act V — The Town's Threads
- **Jarl Beowyn (the Earl's Hall, #8)**: The party passed a hanged body with its hands cut off on a platform in the courtyard — a thief who'd tried to steal a fairing (the copper-per-ride rowboats ferrying between banks). Tree-Hammer bonded with a guard by spitting at the corpse.
- Inside they found the Jarl: *"A dwarven woman with long braided silver hair and beard has just finished knocking over a tall human wielding a guard's baton to the ground in front of a humble wooden home. She's busy berating him for not anticipating her attack."* *"I gotta put them on their toes, even though I put them on their asses."*
- **The Toki side-quest**: Beowyn has a problem with the refugees in the tents agitating for strained resources. She wants dirt on their leader **Toki** — a halfling — enough to arrest her. *"I need something that will give me the reason to arrest her. Just being a pain in the ass is not enough."* Payment was pointedly not mentioned. Aesgor: *"Well, but if we do, you better prepare a payment since you're the one that requested it."*
- Reminder from DM: Aesgor's promise to return **Siddhe to her home** is still the main campaign quest — not sworn on but the character's running anchor.

### Act VI — The Explorer at Shipyard #11
- **Perrin and Narsh**: the human and half-orc running the shipyard, directing humans, elves, and Grimnir craftspeople.
- **Three longships**: two skeletons and one almost-complete **Explorer-class longship**. *"The captain who commissioned it never finished payment and has disappeared. We haven't bothered finishing it since it hasn't been paid for for… well over a week now."* Two-thirds paid. 1,000 gold outstanding. Full cost new: **6,500 gold**.
- Party current total: 56 gold. *"You don't have enough money at this point in time."*
- **Gaynor**: *"How dare you assume by the King of Toads that you can't afford your stinking ship. I'll burn it down overnight. Let's go."*
- **The table spun up a bit**: someone floated taking "a small loan of a thousand gold coins," then Gaynor escalated to ship-merchant-then-murder-them, then to *"Who can we mug? We'll just go and clear out the pikey camp. Take all their shit."* then to casino cons. None of it actually money on the table. Aesgor's takeaway, quieter: *"We just learned some games earlier this session. We can use them here."*

### Act VII — The Rune Thrower
- **The grove at #12**: *"A small tree grove surrounds a clearing near the river's edge. A campfire burns near a stack of wood, and a tall man stands on the opposite side of the fire. As you approach, you realize this individual is made of blue-black wood and stands almost nine feet tall, his arms poking through the white tunic he wears, as thin as twigs."*
- Crucial DM note: *"He looks like a much more refined version of those creatures that basically took out Hrolf's party if you remember at the very beginning."* — Kinnikade is **kin to the wicker sentries that attacked Hrolf and Aesgor in Kekel in Session 1**.
- **Kinnikade's opening**: *"Show me your birth runes, and I will show you how this world of ours ends."*
- Every PC already bears a **birth rune** on the palm of their hand (Aesgor's ᚲ / Rune of the Vigilant is canon; the others now join him). Kinnikade draws a **second rune** directly beneath each, with a vision.

- **Gaynor's rune** — *"She yet lives, the one who cast the curse on you. I see her. She stands everlasting upon the white marble of Falgore. Speak with her, for only this god accursed you. Perhaps only she might undo what should never have been."* Gaynor's curse has a face, a name, and a place: **Falgore**, white marble.
- **Tree-Hammer's rune** — A cold frigid wind blew across his shoulders as Kinnikade drew. *"In this rune I see much turmoil in you, from your past, from your present, a hard choice made."* Kinnikade zeroed in on the **gray feather** he'd been hiding from the party (*"He's been hiding that fact"*). When Tree-Hammer presented it: *"His eyes widen and he bites his wooden lip. This is for the Volva. Find the Volva of the Well of Wisdom."* Kinnikade knows more but refuses to speak it. *"There are things that even I cannot speak of. You must request this information from… the Well of Wisdom."*
- **Marzena's rune** — a seafloor shape. *"I see you stand upon a ship of your own, but that is only the very beginning. One day you shall return to your lands after you have righted a great wrong. And if you so want, you shall lead them."* Then a warning: *"Your enemy is crafty, but not so wise. They have made mistakes. They have left the trail. I see a man in the far north protected by a hide of a great bear. I see a slain man, too rich, too slow. And I see a terrible forest."* He closed with a prayer to **Aldin** *"that she might protect him from those who watch the watchers."*
- **Aesgor's rune** — a Y-shape drawn beneath the ᚲ. *"A place I see. A terrible place. A place that seems unreal. An old tower. It rises from nothing. It returns to nothing. But inside you shall find a whisper of the truth."* And the refrain: *"Twice you shall walk those halls. Twice you must walk those halls."* Direct callback to the ruined tower of **Kekel**. Aesgor: *"I shall take that and be more confused."*
- **Glug's rune** — Kinnikade's eyes widened and looked past him. *"In this rune, I see a king passing coin to a Varang. I see a glimmer of drawn swords. I see the blood of your family. In this rune, I see that fate must take you to this Varang."* DM clarified: *"A Varang is a raider that is clanless. Which you realize is pretty much everyone in this town."*
- **Everyone leveled to 2** immediately after. (Aesgor now has a fifth cleric spell slot open for preparation; he drafted **Rings of the Wise** into his sheet to evaluate.)

### Act VIII — The Market and the Drowning Believer
- **The fairing crossing**: One copper per person ferried them east. Hrolf and Siddhe came along (Siddhe is still with the party — no seer delivery yet).
- **The beggar, Sculpty the Grey debt**: A man approached for coins. Marzena: *"No, get a fucking job."* Then gave him a gold anyway. Aesgor: *"You started that out so adversarial and you still gave him a coin."*
- **The drowning woman**: A woman walked past the party in a daze and right off the end of the dock into the river. The party pulled her out. She'd taken Kinnikade's vision literally: *"The rune thrower told me that something bad would happen. This must be it, right?"* Gaynor, deadpan: *"Maybe if you paid more mind to what was in front of you than the words of the rune thrower, then you would not befall."*
- **The pawn shops**: Mundane shops only — *"you doubt there are any deals to be had here."* Gaynor rolled **22 Intimidation** on the gem merchant. DM allowed the moonstone (40 gp) to sell at half (20 gp) instead of the standard quarter. Everything else at quarter. **Net party haul: ~18 gold each plus 5 silver.**
- **Tree-Hammer kept the gray feather** private from the party and the merchants. DM, explicit: *"I don't think Tree-Hammer has let you guys know that he has it. He's been hiding that fact."*
- **Healing potions redistributed**: Tree-Hammer took Marzena's potion last session and passed it on to Hrolf; Glug gave another to Aesgor earlier. The **third and last** now also sits on Aesgor's belt.
- **Bendor wine** (the "Bendover" joke ran for five minutes): Common 5 sp / bottle, fine 10 gp, frost wine 30 gp. No one bought any.
- **+1 crossbow bolts × 10**: 125 gold, unaffordable.
- **Drunken revelers**: A woman stumbling through the crowd spilled mead on Gaynor, then handed him a horn of mead in apology. He took it. DM confirmed that had he refused, it would have been a fight.

### Act IX — Session Close
- DM's prompt to the table: *"What's the main goal you have in this town?"* Answer in unison: **Get a ship.** How: the festival challenge. Homework: learn about the captains. Likely target: **Captain Thornbeard**.
- Gaynor: *"Get that stupid fucking competition and rip somebody off out of their ship."*
