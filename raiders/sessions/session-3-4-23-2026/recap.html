Session Summary

After burning the zombie at the ruined farmstead, the party rode out the worsening blizzard for another day — passing the time with two Roll20-built games of chance (Ship Captain Crew and Beetle) and a dice-betting bit Hrolf attempted and the party correctly refused. Two more days east-northeast brought them to a tributary where Glug, the party's Grimnir fish-kin, slipped into the water with Guidance from Aesgor and speared five fish for the extended party. From there up to the real town of Nowhere — a two-sided river settlement of longships, refugees, and six pages of plot hooks.

At the edge of town they met Egill, a tiefling former quartermaster dropping handfuls of fingernails and toenails into a fire to stop the building of a dread-vision ship — "a massive ship made of nails, with the dead hovering every square inch of it, sailing towards land with a woman, half her face ruined, standing at the prow." At the docks, Celorfin — a thin short elf — confirmed a ship flying the enemy's colors had been here days ago bartering as what he called the Whar. Hrolf bled his palm on the dock and swore vengeance "before the snows melt," then demanded the party join him in the Oath of Fellowship. Aesgor, Gaynor, and Marzena swore. Tree-Hammer refused and stayed free. Glug did not speak to it.

From there: Jarl Beowyln offered paid dirt-work on the halfling refugee leader Toki; Peron and Garsh at the shipyard showed off the half-finished Explorer-class longship the party cannot afford; and Kinickan the Rune Thrower, a nine-foot figure of blue-black wood, drew a new rune beneath each PC's birth rune and delivered a cryptic vision tied to it. Everyone leveled to 2 on the spot. The market paid pennies on the gold, the river took a woman who trusted the rune thrower too literally, and the session closed with the path forward explicit: learn about the captains, then win a festival challenge for their ship.

Timeline

Act I · Exploration · The Storm, the Dice, the Fish

Riding Out the Storm

  • The blizzard outside the farmstead worsened. The party unanimously agreed to ride it out. Aesgor, running the exhaustion math: "Because we shouldn't just start getting… once you fail the third one, then yeah."
  • Aesgor's long-rest Afflicted checks: 9 on the storm rest, 15 on the morning-ritual rest. Both safe — no psychic damage, no short-term madness.
  • Ship Captain Crew: DM-built Roll20 dice mini-game (five tokens, plus an unused mauve for an absent player). Tree-Hammer won, earning one gold from Hrolf. Aesgor landed at exactly 51 on his last roll — one short of a tie-beater.
  • Beetle: Second mini-game. Marzena won, Gaynor second, Tree-Hammer last — "I take last on this one. From first to last, really." Chess was offered and unanimously declined.
  • Hrolf's silver-per-fail bet: offered one silver on a 6 vs. paying a silver on every failed roll. Party identified it as "literally a stupid bet" and passed.
  • Day 10–11 travel: Storm abated. Two days east-northeast through ash and snow. Rations running thin — Siddhe and Hrolf keep drawing from the pool. Tree-Hammer to one ration. "We've been giving them to these NPCs that have been leeching resources from us."
  • Day 12 — the tributary: First trees in days, stunted and bare. Tree-Hammer scaled a sparse pine in squirrel form to forage. Aesgor cast Guidance on him. No acorns, no pine nuts, nothing. DM narration: "Aesgor grabs your tail and says, Take my love!"
  • Glug's fishing play: Slipped into the tributary as the party's Grimnir fish-kin with Aesgor's Guidance. Marzena named it aloud — "he is a fish thing." Ambusher advantage + underwater stealth. 18 on the net attack. Five fish — one day's ration for the whole extended party. Quietly the session's biggest material win.
  • Marzena crossbow-fished for the record — "I fucking tried to fish with my crossbow." It did not work mechanically.
  • Hrolf remembered that Nowhere is further up the tributary, not at his old ruined farmstead of two sessions ago. The party crossed and continued north.
Act II · Exploration · Arrival at the Real Nowhere

Day 13 — Nowhere at Last

  • A river town cut in two, wooden homes clustered on both banks, half a dozen longships moored mid-stream, farmland ringing sparse forest. Dwarves, elves, dragonborn, and humans mixing in the rocky streets.
  • A light dusting of snow from the storm they'd just ridden out. After three sessions of ash desert — the first real settlement.
  • The tiefling at the edge: "A woman with curled horns, light red skin, and pointed teeth at the very edge of town… She stares at you with golden eyes, but does not rise to greet you, focused as she is in dropping something into the flames."
  • Tree-Hammer approached. Gaynor greeted her first: "Poacher and greeter, anyway. Greetings, Kellion."
  • Her name is Egill. Tiefling. Formerly a quartermaster. Now she throws fingernails and toenails into a fire all day.
  • The nail detail: the DM reminded the table that the zombie raider last session had its nails torn off before death. Aesgor asked if any of them would know what she was doing in-world. No check passed. The link is on the table but not something the party knows yet.
Act III · Revelation · The Ship Made of Nails

Egill's Vision

  • "I keep having visions or dreams, nightmares of a ship, massive ship made of nails, with the dead hovering every square inch of it, sailing towards land with a woman, half her face ruined, standing at the prowl. She is the end of us all."
  • Egill destroys nails to try to stop the ship being built.
  • Someone at the table offered the classic "you should eat less cheese at night" deflection. Egill: "Cheese is too good. I'll never stop eating cheese."
  • Marzena tried a DC-30 History check to identify the vision. Got nowhere. Naglfar — the Norse ship of the dead — was not recognized in-world.
  • Three threads from Egill: the Dockmaster Celorfin (ships), the Rune Thrower Kinickan (the wisest person she knows), and the upcoming Festival — "You can probably challenge another captain for their vessel if you're good enough."
  • Egill's offer: 8 gold for her to assemble a crew. She used to own a map of unique locations — sold it to the Dealer at the Raid Leader's Hall. Also holds a rumor about Captain Thornbeard. Marzena offered a vial of lead-based ink (10 gp) for it; Egill refused. "I guess you'll have to find out, wouldn't you? Give me your nails."
  • The party moved on. Egill threw more nails into the fire.
Act IV · Roleplay · The Docks, the Whar, the Oath

The Oath of Fellowship

  • Celorfin: "A thin, short elf barks at a mixed assembly of humans, gnomes, and half-orcs working along the waterfront." Dockmaster.
  • Gaynor showed him the enemy shield from the beach. Celorfin: "Yeah, of course I've seen that. There was a ship here just a few days ago, maybe a week, with those colors. They sold us wheat, they bartered for weapons, and they left. They call themselves the Whar."
  • Hrolf snapped. Drew his dagger. Sliced his palm. Bled onto the dock: "Aldehan, hear me. I swear upon my blood that before the snows melt, I will slay the raider from the war clan for his violence against my people. I swear this vengeance against him."
  • Hrolf turned to the party. "Will you join me? Swear it and oath to me and one another. Share your sword, your shield, your spells, and I will do the same. And Skalds will sing stories of us together for the glory we shall earn throughout the ages."
  • Aesgor, first in: "Aesgor for vengeance. Yes." Palm slit. Oath recited. Then: "I'm losing too much blood, so someone else please go ahead."
  • Gaynor, second: "More corpses on my path to Valhalla." Full oath.
  • Marzena, third: swore once on her clan the Halharn, then again: "I swear upon my clan, my honor, and the Lady Luta. I shall hold them as a fellowship." First time Lady Luta is named in the campaign. Added unprompted: "I do not do this lightly."
  • Tree-Hammer refused. "I spilled blood over a few words, but I've got a hammer and you're going to need weapons. I ain't cutting my hand, and I ain't speaking an oath. I'm already in a quest for vengeance, and another will only add to my time." DM: "The only one who's not actually swearing the oath is Tree-Hammer."
  • Glug did not speak to it. Gaynor flagged the paladin double-oath problem aloud; the table moved on without resolution.
  • The Curse of the Exile awaits any oath-taker who fails to kill the Whar raider before the snows melt.
  • Celorfin, on the whole ritual: "Wow, that was really dramatic, guys." Pointed them to the shipbuilders at 11 and the festival.
Act V · Social · The Town's Threads

Jarl Beowyln and the Toki Problem

  • The Earl's Hall (#8): a hanged body with its hands cut off on a courtyard platform. A thief who tried to steal a fairing — the copper-per-ride rowboats ferrying between banks.
  • Tree-Hammer bonded with a guard by spitting at the corpse: "No one are these thieves. I'll spit in the ground over beside him as well."
  • Jarl Beowyln: "A dwarven woman with long braided silver hair and beard has just finished knocking over a tall human wielding a guard's baton to the ground in front of a humble wooden home. She's busy berating him for not anticipating her attack."
  • Her philosophy: "I gotta put them on their toes, even though I put them on their asses."
  • The Toki side-quest: Beowyln wants dirt on Toki — the halfling running the refugee tents — enough to arrest her. "I need something that will give me the reason to arrest her. Just being a pain in the ass is not enough." Payment was pointedly not mentioned.
  • Aesgor closed that: "Well, but if we do, you better prepare a payment since you're the one that requested it."
  • DM reminder to the table: getting Siddhe home to the seers is still the main campaign quest — Aesgor's anchor, even unsworn.
Act VI · Exploration · The Explorer at Shipyard #11

Peron, Garsh, and a Ship Too Expensive

  • Peron and Garsh — the human and half-orc running the shipyard, directing humans, elves, and Grimnir craftspeople.
  • Two longship skeletons and one Explorer-class ship two-thirds complete — the captain who commissioned it abandoned payment and vanished. "We haven't bothered finishing it since it hasn't been paid for for… well over a week now."
  • Price to finish: 1,000 gold. Full new-build value: 6,500 gold. Party total: 56 gp.
  • Gaynor, at the insult of being told he couldn't afford it: "How dare you assume by the King of Toads that you can't afford your stinking ship. I'll burn it down overnight. Let's go."
  • The table spun up a scheme arc — Gaynor driving: loan a thousand gold → ship-merchant → murder the loaners → mug the refugee camp → casino cons. None of it money on the table.
  • Aesgor's quieter takeaway: "We just learned some games earlier this session. We can use them here." (Festival-challenge thinking.)
Act VII · Revelation · The Rune Thrower

Kinickan and Five Visions

  • The grove at #12: "A small tree grove surrounds a clearing near the river's edge. A campfire burns near a stack of wood, and a tall man stands on the opposite side of the fire. As you approach, you realize this individual is made of blue-black wood and stands almost nine feet tall, his arms poking through the white tunic he wears, as thin as twigs."
  • Crucial DM tell: "He looks like a much more refined version of those creatures that basically took out Hrolf's party if you remember at the very beginning." Kinickan is kin to the wicker sentries from Kekel.
  • Kinickan's opening: "Show me your birth runes, and I will show you how this world of ours ends."
  • Every PC already bears a birth rune on the palm of their hand. Aesgor's ᚲ (Rune of the Vigilant) is canon; the others now join him. Kinickan draws a second rune directly beneath each — and delivers a vision.
  • Gaynor's rune: "She yet lives, the one who cast the curse on you. I see her. She stands everlasting upon the white marble of Falgore. Speak with her, for only this god accursed you. Perhaps only she might undo what should never have been." Gaynor's curse now has a face, a sex, and a place — Falgore, white marble.
  • Tree-Hammer's rune: a cold frigid wind blew across his shoulders as Kinickan drew. "In this rune I see much turmoil in you, from your past, from your present, a hard choice made." Kinickan zeroed in on the gray feather Tree-Hammer had been hiding from the party. "His eyes widen and he bites his wooden lip. This is for the Volva. Find the Volva of the Well of Wisdom." Kinickan knows more but will not say: "There are things that even I cannot speak of."
  • Marzena's rune — a seafloor shape: "I see you stand upon a ship of your own, but that is only the very beginning. One day you shall return to your lands after you have righted a great wrong. And if you so want, you shall lead them." Plus a warning: "Your enemy is crafty, but not so wise. They have made mistakes. They have left the trail. I see a man in the far north protected by a hide of a great bear. I see a slain man, too rich, too slow. And I see a terrible forest." Closed with a prayer to Aldin "that she might protect him from those who watch the watchers."
  • Aesgor's rune — a Y-shape beneath the ᚲ: "A place I see. A terrible place. A place that seems unreal. An old tower. It rises from nothing. It returns to nothing. But inside you shall find a whisper of the truth. Twice you shall walk those halls. Twice you must walk those halls." A direct callback to Kekel.
  • Glug's rune: Kinickan's eyes widened. "In this rune, I see a king passing coin to a Varang. I see a glimmer of drawn swords. I see the blood of your family. In this rune, I see that fate must take you to this Varang." DM clarified: a Varang is a clanless raider — "pretty much everyone in this town."
  • Everyone leveled to 2 immediately after the scene closed.
Act VIII · Social · The Market and the Drowning Believer

Across the River

  • The fairing crossing: one copper per person east across the river. Hrolf and Siddhe came along.
  • The beggar: a man approached for coins to pay his debt to Sculpty the Grey. Marzena: "No, get a fucking job." Then threw him a gold anyway. Aesgor: "You started that out so adversarial and you still gave him a coin."
  • The drowning woman: a woman walked past the party in a daze and straight off the end of the dock. The party rescued her. She'd taken Kinickan's vision literally: "The rune thrower told me that something bad would happen. This must be it, right?" Gaynor, deadpan: "Maybe if you paid more mind to what was in front of you than the words of the rune thrower, then you would not befall."
  • The pawn shops: mundane only. Gaynor rolled 22 Intimidation on the gem merchant. DM allowed the moonstone (40 gp) to sell at half (20 gp); everything else at quarter value. Net party haul: ~18 gold + 5 silver each.
  • Tree-Hammer kept the gray feather private — even from the merchants. DM, explicit: "I don't think Tree-Hammer has let you guys know that he has it. He's been hiding that fact."
  • Healing potion consolidation: Tree-Hammer took Marzena's potion last session and passed it to Hrolf. Glug gave one to Aesgor. The third and last now also sits on Aesgor's belt.
  • Bendor wine (the "Bendover" joke ran five minutes): common 5 sp, fine 10 gp, frost wine 30 gp. No one bought.
  • +1 crossbow bolts × 10: 125 gold — unaffordable.
  • Drunken revelers: a woman bumped Gaynor, spilled mead, then handed him a horn of mead in apology. He accepted. DM later revealed that refusing would have been a fight.
