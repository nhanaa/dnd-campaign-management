## Session Summary

The party finally settles into Nowhere proper — a meal at the **Eye of the Storm** feasting hall, a private-club errand at the **Raid Leaders' Hall**, a midnight expedition across the river to the refugee camp, a long rest, and a busy festival-eve morning of dock-side intelligence work. The arc of the night is pivot after pivot: away from killing **Toki** the halfling refugee leader (who turns out to be an unpaid activist, not a criminal), toward a free crew through mediation; from "the Dealer wants Toki gone" to "the Dealer's map is the prize, and *gone* might just mean *gone with us*"; from challenging **Had Thornbeard** head-on to engineering a festival-day pink-slip race using the retiring **Captain Lurx**'s ship as a loaner.

**Glug** carries the Raid Leaders' Hall — gets the door password ("Mother's Milk") from the innkeeper Zelan, identifies their target Varang (**Asvard**) by playing a wordplay drink-refill game with the half-elf intermediary **Rufus**, and later proposes the mediation-with-Toki idea that DM Ogre Smash hands inspiration for on the spot. **Gaynor** runs telepathic negotiation with **the Dealer**, a tall blonde woman with a quarter-orc jaw who reads the room like a casino floor. **Marzena** drags the party across the river ("It's the perfect time to commit crime, baby"), then talks the party off the assassination ledge once she sees the kids running around the camp. **Aesgor** spends the session as the dry chorus — Guidance-cantripping with full RP wand-and-vein theatre, voicing the doomed challenger's threat with "I'm gonna eat your fucking liver!", narrating Tree-Hammer's rage as "Squirrel begins frothing at the mouth and goes rabid," and reframing Glug's "I kill people" overshare to keep the next crew chat from souring. **Tree-Hammer** gets dragged into a 1v1 duel by a self-promoting raider trying to earn a ship berth — and proceeds to humiliate him over five-plus rounds, the flunky failing to shield-push him prone every single round before Tree-Hammer cuts his legs out from under him and puts an axe to his neck.

The session ends just past midnight on festival-eve. Three captain dossiers are now in hand (Scooty / Had / Lurx), all three with material weaknesses. The path forward: festival-day at the **Feast of Serpents**, with three live levers — a pink-slip race against Had using Lurx's loaner ship, mediation between Toki's refugees and the Jarl, and a warehouse-3 visit to **Asvard** for whatever Glug's second oath demands.

## Key Events

### Act I — The Eye of the Storm (Feasting Hall #4)

- **Forgotten campfire vision (retconned in).** DM Ogre Smash apologizes — "I made a huge mistake. There's an event that should have happened" — and rolls back to a campfire from two sessions ago. The fire turns unnatural; **the iron bust face** (lips sewn shut, carried in Marzena's pack) tries to speak from the flames, fails, sees Siddhe and *smiles*. Then the fire explodes into kaleidoscopic visions: *a great ship sits beneath the sky with no stars; an upside-down tower rises from a field of lava; a mass of serpents rises.* Should have been a short-term-madness check. *"The permanent madness we just forget,"* — Gaynor.
- **The innkeeper Zelan.** Tuss innkeeper of the Eye of the Storm — Sabertooth-from-X-Men build, matronly graying hair, yellow teeth. Carries a leather-belt amulet of two crossed wooden hammers, the symbol of the **twin gods Nark and Idin**. Husband died in a raid years back ("tried to catch an arrow with his throat"). Genuinely warm. The fish stew smells horrendous and tastes phenomenal.
- **The two ship dossiers.** Zelan points out two crews drinking at her tables and gives the party the names she has:
  - **The Grey** — Captain **Scooty** (S-K-U-T-I). Middle-aged, missing half his hand, crew loves him, recent raids gone poorly.
  - **The Raven Ship** — Captain **Hadd Thornbeard** (H-A-D-D, double D). Only six months in command after winning the ship from the previous captain. Crew don't have a feel for him yet but say he's *quite courageous*.
- **Rumor roll.** Tree-Hammer asks for rumors and burns a d8 to learn that the **Whar clan** (formerly the predator-sigil, then the dancing horse) **has changed its mascot again, this time to a wolf**. The previous-mascot detail confirms how often they reflag.
- **The Dealer and the password.** Zelan won't sell maps but knows of *the Dealer* — operating from the **Raid Leaders' Hall**, the captains' private drinking club. Entry requires a password. Zelan has it and won't give it away. Five-gold offer is rejected outright; her persuasion check is a disaster and she puts the money back on the table out of guilt. Glug retries with **Aesgor casting Guidance** (full RP — *"Mr. Gaynor. Please stand still. This is my stick. Injected into his vein."*) plus Gaynor's Help action; rolls a 14 with advantage and gets the password — **"Mother's Milk."**
- **Asvard, the unemployable Varang.** Glug pays Zelan a few extra gold to ask about *real assholes in town*. She offers up **Asvard** (A-S-V-A-R-D) — a Varang who beat a man to death for no reason a few days ago, takes any job for any price, currently renting from one of the **number-three warehouses** in the dock district. The Jarl and her men patrol the warehouses; **Celorfin** holds the keys.
- **Oryx, the loose carpenter.** Marzena spots the only half-orc in the building and introduces herself ("Marzena is a confection often used in the decoration of cakes"). Half-orc carpenter **Oryx**'s longship sank a few weeks back; he's looking for crew work. She doesn't hire him, but slips him a hip-dagger business card with her name scrawled on it in someone else's blood. *Builder*-class crew specialist — half-time fortifications, minor ship repairs.
- **The shadow-puppet myth.** A thin man in green emerges from behind a curtain and casts shadow puppets on the wall — giants and serpents and herds of horses and warriors clashing — until he collapses and rolls back under the curtain leaving only his head behind. The audience claps; Glug slow-claps. DM identifies it as a re-enactment of *A Wild World* — the creation myth, **Mirgal** (the Loki-aspect god) murdered, his body made into the world Grimnir.

### Act II — The Raid Leaders' Hall (Private Club #5)

- **Approach.** Up a series of rickety stairs at the back of an unusually tall building. Glug at the door: *"Mother's Bloody Milk"*; corrected to *"Mother's Milk"*; doorman lets them in. Hallway with multi-colored smoke gaps (DM's regretful tech compromise). Hrolf and Siddhe come along.
- **The captains' room.** Eight round tables, each repurposed from a raider's shield, polished and lacquered. Brazier of colored smoke on each. A wicker bartender — kin to **Kinickan** by build — serving drinks with extending wooden fingers. Conversation stops as the party walks in, then resumes in whispers. **Hadd Thornbeard** is at one of the captains' tables; one corner table holds a single lone older man.
- **Rufus the half-elf.** Glug brings the corner-table half-elf his Raider wine; he names himself *Rufus* and tells Glug straight that "your kind don't come into here very often." Glug fishes for "**Asbar**" — Rufus picks up his drink, repeats *"Asvar, Asvar,"* finishes it, sets the glass down empty, and stares at it. Glug refills it from his own raider wine. Rufus warms up: *yeah, no captain wants him on their crew, you'll find him around — and the Dockmaster knows everybody.* Then telegraphs an offer of a different kind ("I can always use more companionship"); Glug: *"That's a really nice guy. He definitely noticed me guys looking at him. I let him know that was a thing. So he's cool."* Marzena, from across the room, insists he *"was giving the glass eyes,"* repeatedly, three times. DM, exasperated: *"He was literally saying, blind me with alcohol, I can tell you shit."*
- **The Hadd / Lurx leak.** While wandering the room, Aesgor overhears Hadd Thornbeard say to one of his table-mates: *"Lurks is looking to retire. That's funny what love will do for you."* First mention of **Captain Lurx** (L-U-R-X) — a third captain — and her retirement leverage point, all in one sentence.
- **The Dealer.** Gaynor sits with Rufus and asks telepathically *if he is the Dealer*. Shocked face — Rufus is not, but he handles dealings with that person. Two gold pieces vanish off the table into Rufus's pocket; he leaves to fetch her. Fifteen minutes later she arrives — tall, blonde, a slightly elongated jaw and oversized teeth ("hinting of orc ancestry — possibly a quarter-orc"). Trails her fingers down Gaynor's back as she passes. Reads the entire room as a habit. Gaynor opens with *"I heard from a little bird with horns that you're in possession of a map."* She confirms — Egill's map of the world. Best offer so far is **70 gold**. She hasn't sold yet.
- **The Toki ask.** Pivot: she'd rather not sell at all. *"There is a halfling who is causing me, in my business, disruption."* She wants **Toki** *no longer in town*. Newcomers have already heard the name. Gaynor accepts the framing — *"We can't have these kinds of ne'er-do-wells in law-abiding towns like this, can we?"* — and proposes paying her a visit first, *"to get ourselves a little shred of goodwill."* Dealer agrees: if Toki is no longer a bother, she no longer needs the map. She also offers a **healing potion** for **30 gp, paid in advance, delivered next day**. As she leaves she **winks at Tree-Hammer**.

### Act III — The Pikey Camp, by Night (#6)

- **The launch.** Marzena: *"It's the perfect time to commit crime, baby. Let's go rent a boat, cross the river, and then fucking sneak into the pikey town."* No PC has Stealth proficiency. Marzena and Tree-Hammer share the highest Dex in the party (14). They go anyway. One copper per crossing.
- **The arrival.** A community of multi-colored canvas tents on the northwest edge of Nowhere. Fire pits, skewered rabbits and birds cooking. Half-orcs, dragonborn, tieflings — about thirty people. An angry halfling with her back to them: *"That sour-crusted dwarf — don't cut the precious trees, don't build homes, don't, don't, don't. She's insufferable…"* — sees the party, face turns purple, stomps off into a tent.
- **The kids who change the plan.** Half-orc and halfling children run up trying to sell foraged berries and nuts. Hrolf buys a handful from the kids. Marzena does the same for a copper. The half-orc child sprints back to her mother yelling *"we have money for food now!"* Gaynor, undeterred, considers blasting them. Marzena: *"Thank fucking god he hasn't got Second Wind yet."*
- **Confronting Toki.** They walk up to her tent. Marzena opens: *"There's a lot of people that want you out of this town. Why?"* Toki — half-elf, halfling, the angry one — turns out to be the camp's only voice. *"No one's giving them jobs. No one's giving them food. We can't even build houses because they won't let us cut down the sparse trees that are available because they have to be for the longships. We're just asking for basic human rights."* She doesn't know who the Dealer is. She does know **Celorfin** the dock steward refuses to hire half-orcs or tieflings.
- **The pivot.** Marzena, audibly: *"I have a gut feeling that these pikeys, these carnies, are not going to be the assholes that everybody else is making them out to be — but that's my opinion."* Glug: *"I don't know if you noticed, but there were kids around there. I'm not really into stabbing kids. There's really no honor in that."* Tree-Hammer reads the table: *"Well, it seems like there are three of the five of us who have decided that killing this person is not the option they want to go with."* Gaynor — still a holdout — *"I'll quite happily fry a fucking halfling to get what I need."*
- **Glug's mediation pitch (DM-rewarded).** *"Perhaps we could be the mediators and say, hey, all they need is homes. So let's help them with that and problem solved."* DM grants Glug **inspiration** on the spot for stating it out loud.
- **Toki's counter-offer.** If the party can find homes and jobs for the refugees, **Toki and a number of them will join the party as crew — for free.** Better than Egill's eight gold. The refugees include people who've crewed ships before but whom no captain will hire. Two levers: **the Jarl** (housing authorization, biggest employer), and **Celorfin** (dock employment). The party heads back to the inn.

### Act IV — Long Rest, Funeral, and the Sven Duel

- **Long-rest tells.** Aesgor's Tuss-Afflicted long-rest die: 15. Safe, no psychic damage — but for the second long-rest in a row Gaynor catches him grabbing his head in pain on waking and trying to hide it. Aesgor cover-stories it as a hangover. Aesgor and Gaynor are bunking together; Hrolf and Tree-Hammer; Marzena, Glug, and Siddhe.
- **Zelan's slip.** During the morning's chatter (with Marzena, who pronounces her name correctly for once), Zelan lets slip that she's overheard **Lurx disparaging Hadd** behind his back: *Hadd is a terrible leader and not very smart.* Free intel for the festival angle.
- **Random event roll, morning at the docks.** A raft burns in the river — a warrior who died in a brawl last night is being given Valhalla rites. Aesgor: *"He will go to Valhalla."*
- **Sven's challenge.** Mid-rite, a young raider declares *it's time to prove himself* and looks for a sparring partner. He picks **Tree-Hammer** — *"Little furry one"* — though Glug at first thinks it's him (*"We both have fur. I'm sorry"*). Aesgor riffs the NPC's challenge in Sven's voice as the duel kicks off: *"I challenge you to a duel. I'm gonna eat your fucking liver!"* The raider asks for Tree-Hammer's next of kin to deliver his weapons after his death. Tree-Hammer: *"My next of kin is the forest. You can tell any tree you want."* The raider: *"My brother lives in Tunnarg."* (No name yet.)
- **The duel** — see Combat below.
- **The aftermath.** Sven's body burns on a second raft. Two pyres in one morning. *"That was entertaining. It was a little long, but fun."* — Aesgor.

### Act V — The Three Captain Dossiers

- **Crossing back to the captain ships.** They cross the river to the captains' moorings. **Celorfin** the dock steward greets them — *"You guys are really putting coins in my pocket for all these faerings."* Aesgor sends Glug off to Celorfin to ask about Asvard: *"Alright, you can do your thing, fish assassin. Figure out who you need to murder."* Celorfin, on hearing Glug's *"I kill people, and he's on the list"* / *"I follow the goddess Lucha…I'm a man, not a sir"* confessional, **happily backs away as soon as it's convenient** — but does confirm Asvard is renting in the warehouse district and won't have to be dealt with much longer.
- **Ship of Vipers (Lurx).** Gaynor and the rest charm an older Vipers crew member with Guidance from Aesgor:
  - Lurx is a **gnome** with a temper.
  - Crew is content with her, but the recent raids have brought too little loot.
  - She **lost a duel to Scooty** recently and **was spared** — Scooty *didn't want credit for beating a drunk woman.*
  - She's **fallen in love with a woman from a captured outpost** and wants to retire to live with her.
  - Lurx's snake-headed prow has been **freshly replaced** — recent damage.
  - She'll be at the festival tomorrow.
- **Raven Ship (Hadd).** Aesgor leads the questions cleanly. An older sailor talks:
  - Hadd has been their captain for **six months**.
  - Hadd was previously with the **Nattle clan** (led by **Horar Longspear**, a dwarf). His former crew and the Vipers' previous crew fought several times.
  - **Hadd's brother died in one of those fights**, against the previous Vipers crew. That's where the *itching to take over this ship* comes from.
  - Hadd won the captaincy in a **drinking game**.
  - Hadd doesn't always make the best choices; some of his crew get the shit jobs.
- **The Grey (Scooty).** Through deception/intimidation/bribery:
  - Crew loves Scooty.
  - Last few raids have been bad — but the crew believes in him.
  - Scooty has been **demanding loan repayments** from townspeople around Nowhere.
  - One of his crew thinks he has **way too much gold** for someone allegedly collecting on past dealings — the suspicion is **Scooty might be skimming from his own crew**.
- **The festival plan crystallizes.** Gaynor's pitch, refined over the night:
  - Borrow Lurx's loaner ship (she's retiring; persuasion angle: love).
  - Spill a drink on Hadd at the captains' table; provoke a re-challenge during the festival.
  - **Race for pink slips**, with sabotage on the rival ship to guarantee the win.
  - Tug-of-war and the **Thinker's Game** are also valid challenge formats. Aesgor: *"Challenge a man to the Thinker's Game. He's a dumbass."*
- **End of session.** Midnight. No warehouse run tonight. Asvard waits.

## Combat Encounters

### Tree-Hammer vs Sven (1v1 duel — Day 14, mid-morning, Nowhere docks)

**Trigger.** A young raider, watching the burning raft of last night's brawl casualty, declares *"It's time for me to get on that ship. I need to prove myself"* and challenges **Tree-Hammer** — *"little furry one"* — to a duel. The raider's name (revealed only after he's dead): **Sven**, brother in **Tunnarg**.

**Stats observed.**
- Sven: AC 14 (Tree-Hammer thinks 16 at first; corrected mid-fight). Two attacks per turn (axe). Has a shield-push ability that targets a STR save to knock target prone — used every round. Has a ship-deck-only ability that he couldn't use here.
- Tree-Hammer: AC 14, two attacks per turn, raging (advantage on STR saves, +2 rage damage on the first attack only — *"every other time"*).

**Round 1.** Sven goes first (Tree-Hammer's initiative was bad). Sven attempts shield-push → fails Tree-Hammer's STR save (with rage advantage). Sven swings the axe → 12 misses. Tree-Hammer rages. Tree-Hammer attacks — main hits for **7** (rage damage modifier was unset; corrected post-round).
> *"Squirrel begins frothing at the mouth and goes rabid."* — Aesgor narrating.

**Round 2.** Sven shield-push → fails again. Sven swings → misses. Tree-Hammer hits with off-hand — Gaynor flags the missing rage modifier; Tree-Hammer fixes the math.

**Round 3.** Sven shield-push → fails. Sven attacks → misses again, then misses again. *"Everyone starts laughing at him because the little guy is just not letting him move."* — DM.

**Round 4.** Sven finally connects with a **crit** on his first attack (slashing). Second attack lands too — a 16, which Tree-Hammer initially thought was a miss before clarifying his AC is 14, not 16. Sven is now past the shield for ~10, then another ~13 total damage stacking — Tree-Hammer's HP is finally in real trouble.

**Round 5.** Sven misses (15 hits AC 14… still slashes for some). Sven *"can't fucking hit"*; another swing for 3.

**Round 6.** *"He's pecking away."* Tree-Hammer eventually lands the kill blow — Sven goes down, *"blood spurting from wherever you hit him."* Tree-Hammer's finishing declaration:
> *"I will one-hit knock him, cut out his legs from underneath him, and then I'll put the second axe to his neck."*

**Aftermath.** Sven's body burns on a raft — second pyre of the morning. Aesgor pulls Sven's name out of the DM's "if asked" box: *"I can't tell your brother. You never told me your name. He did, though. I don't remember it. His name is Sven."*

**Heckling spotlight (Aesgor):**
- *"Squirrel begins frothing at the mouth and goes rabid."*
- *"I have a chicken hand, dumbass."*
- *"This is peak form of 1v1!"*

**No spell slots burned. No HP totals tracked at the table beyond Sven going down.** Tree-Hammer ate one crit. No other PCs in the fight.
