# Aesgor — Tactics & Tips

## Action Economy

### Standard Turn
- **Action:** Attack (warhammer), Cast a spell, Dodge, Help, etc.
- **Bonus Action:** Healing Word, Shield of Faith, Expeditious Retreat, Steadfast (spend Hit Die to heal)
- **Reaction:** Opportunity attack

### Optimal Turn Patterns
1. **Opening round (combat):** Bless (action, concentration) → position
2. **Healing emergency:** Healing Word (bonus action) + Attack/cantrip (action) — never waste action on Healing Word
3. **Self-preservation:** Steadfast (bonus action, spend HD to heal) + Dodge/Disengage (action)
4. **Ranged:** Sacred Flame (no cover benefit!) + Healing Word if needed

## Key Tactical Notes

### Steadfast is Insurance
- If dropped to 0 HP, spend a Hit Die to pop back up — but only 1 HD at level 1, so this is a one-shot safety net per rest
- Also usable as a bonus action while conscious for self-healing without a spell slot
- **Does NOT work if you took lightning damage since your last turn**

### True Nature — Use Sparingly
- Reveals Tuss heritage — social consequences if witnessed by wrong people
- +1 weapon damage, +5 speed, darkvision are solid in a pinch
- Disadvantage on Persuasion is rough for social encounters
- Best used when: outnumbered, in the dark, among trusted allies only

### Aware — Scout Mode
- Once per long rest, 1 hour of +5 and advantage on Perception/Investigation
- Cannot be surprised + advantage on initiative
- Activate before entering dangerous areas or dungeons
- The white raven/feather is a narrative clue-finder — lean into it for exploration

### Concentration Management
- AC 18 helps avoid hits, but no CON save proficiency yet
- Bless is the best low-level concentration spell — protects itself by boosting your own saves
- Shield of Faith is good but competes for concentration with Bless
- Consider War Caster or Resilient (CON) at level 4

### Spell Slot Conservation
- Only 2 first-level slots at level 1 — be stingy
- Use Guidance constantly (free d4 on ability checks)
- Sacred Flame for damage (no slots needed)
- Light instead of torches
- Save slots for Healing Word (emergency) and Bless (big fights)

## House Rule Considerations

### Whack-a-Mole Healing
- 1st time at 0 HP: no penalty
- 2nd time same combat: DC 15 CON save or 1 exhaustion
- 3rd+ times: automatic exhaustion each time
- **Implication:** Don't yo-yo heal. Keep people above 0 HP proactively with Bless (prevent damage) rather than reactive Healing Word

### Potion as Bonus Action
- Drinking a potion = bonus action (self)
- Administering to another = action
- Frees up spell slots when you have potions available

### Sleeping in Armor
- **Scale Mail (medium):** DC 16 CON save to avoid exhaustion if sleeping in it. Can regain HP.
- **Gambeson (light):** No penalty to sleep in.
- **Strategy:** Remove scale mail to sleep in gambeson (AC 14) for no penalty. Don gambeson = 30 seconds. Don scale mail = 2 minutes.

### Weather Threats
- CON saves against exhaustion in blizzards/freezing water
- High CON (+2) helps but isn't reliable
- Fog Cloud domain spell could be tactically relevant in storms (or useless — DM call)

### Enemy Behavior
- Predators drag downed creatures away to eat — stay near downed allies
- Wounded beasts flee (except defending lair/young)
- Soldiers may retreat to regroup — don't overextend
- Malicious foes will coup de grâce downed heroes — Healing Word range (60 ft) is critical

### Item Scarcity
- Spell components may need to be found through exploration
- Mention component needs to DM proactively
- **Key components to track:** Diamond (300 gp for Revivify at level 5)
