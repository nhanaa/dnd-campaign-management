# PF2e Remastered — Best Utility Build Path Tier List

> **System:** Pathfinder 2e Remastered (Player Core 1 & 2)
> **Variant Rule:** Free Archetype
> **Methodology:** Consensus of three expert perspectives — The Optimizer (resource efficiency and spell selection), The Storyteller (versatility across exploration, social, and downtime), and The Tactician (team-enabling utility and problem-solving). Rankings measure how much a class contributes outside of dealing damage — skill breadth, out-of-combat problem solving, support/buffing, healing, information gathering, exploration, and condition management.

---

## S Tier — Always Has the Answer

### Bard
The most versatile class in PF2e. **Versatile Performance** allows one skill (Performance) to substitute for multiple social skills, effectively giving free skill coverage. The occult spell list excels at divination, illusion, enchantment, and mental manipulation — perfect for social encounters, information gathering, and creative problem-solving. Composition cantrips (Inspire Courage, Inspire Defense) function in AND out of combat. Charisma-based with Occultism gives strong social and knowledge foundations. With Free Archetype, Bard fills any remaining utility gap effortlessly.

### Rogue
The undisputed skill monkey king. Most trained skills of any class at level 1. Fastest skill proficiency progression — reaching Expert, Master, and **Legendary** before any other class. By high levels, a Rogue can have Legendary in multiple skills simultaneously. **Skill Feats** every level (rather than every other level) means more skill options than anyone else. **Trapfinding** and **Trap Sense** cover dungeon exploration. The Rogue may lack spells, but raw skill coverage means they can attempt (and succeed at) more out-of-combat challenges than any other class.

## A Tier — Excellent Utility Toolkit

### Cleric
**Healing Font** provides free extra Heal spells (equal to Charisma modifier) that handle all between-combat healing without spending spell slots. The divine spell list is the gold standard for condition management: **Restoration**, **Remove Disease**, **Remove Curse**, **Neutralize Poison**, **Regenerate**. Divination spells (Augury, Divination, True Seeing) provide information gathering. The Cleric is the party's answer to "can we fix this?" — and the answer is almost always yes. *Dissenting opinion: The Storyteller argued S-tier for utility.*

### Druid
The primal spell list provides the broadest toolkit of any tradition: healing (Heal, Vital Beacon), weather control (Control Weather, Wind Walk), terrain manipulation (Shape Stone, Wall of Thorns), animal/plant communication (Speak with Animals/Plants), and exploration tools (Darkvision, Water Breathing, Tree Stride). **Wild Shape** enables scouting in animal form (bird for aerial recon, fish for underwater). Animal companions provide independent scouting and carrying capacity. *Dissenting opinion: The Storyteller argued S-tier for utility.*

### Investigator
This is where the Investigator **shines**. **Pursue a Lead** and **Clue In** make this the best investigation and information-gathering class in the game. Unmatched Recall Knowledge capability. Second-most trained skills after Rogue. **Devise a Stratagem** is clunky in combat, but the investigative toolkit is peerless for mysteries, puzzles, and social intrigue. If your campaign involves any detective work, Investigator is the best class in the game — their utility is that specialized and that good.

### Thaumaturge
**Diverse Lore** and **Esoteric Lore** allow Recall Knowledge checks against literally any creature, object, or phenomenon. **Implements** provide varied utility tools: Amulet for protection and warding, Lantern for detection and revealing hidden things, Mirror for deception and misdirection, Tome for knowledge and research. Charisma-based for social skills. The Thaumaturge is the party's monster encyclopedia and occult consultant — they always know what the party is fighting and how to exploit it.

### Wizard
The arcane spell list has the **widest spell selection** in the game. Prepared casting means choosing the exact right tool for the job each day — Dispel Magic, Identify Magic, teleportation spells, scrying, planar travel, Knock, Invisibility, Fly. No other class can match the breadth of situational utility the Wizard brings when properly prepared. The weakness is that you must predict what you'll need — unprepared Wizards have gaps. Curriculum spells in Remaster help ensure baseline coverage.

## B Tier — Useful Utility Options

### Champion
**Lay on Hands** provides efficient focus-point healing. **Divine Ally** options add utility: Steed for mounted travel, Blade for combat enhancement, Shield for protection. **Aura of Courage** helps the party resist fear effects. Protection-adjacent utility that's valuable but narrow.

### Commander
**Banner aura** provides +1 status bonus to Will saves in 30 feet — genuine defensive utility. Intelligence-based with tactical knowledge gives good skill coverage. **Tactics** can be used creatively outside combat for coordinated movement and positioning. With Free Archetype, Commanders pick up additional utility from dedications. Solid but utility is secondary to combat tactics.

### Kineticist
Element-specific utility without any resource management. **Water** impulses provide healing and water manipulation. **Air** provides flight and weather control. **Earth** provides construction, barriers, and terrain shaping. **Wood** provides healing and plant-based utility. All of this at-will, with no spell slots to conserve. The utility is more narrow than full casters (elemental-themed rather than broadly magical), but the unlimited usage compensates.

### Oracle
The same divine spell list utility as Cleric (condition removal, healing, divination) plus **unique mystery spells** that no other class can access. **Cosmos Mystery** provides teleportation and starlight-based divination. **Life Mystery** enhances healing beyond even Cleric capability. **Lore Mystery** grants knowledge and investigation tools. The curse mechanic adds caution to utility spell usage (pushing curse levels has consequences), but the breadth of unique tools is genuine.

### Ranger
Excellent **wilderness exploration** — Track, Hunt Prey for tracking outside combat, and nature-based skills cover survival, foraging, and creature identification. Animal companion provides independent scouting, carrying capacity, and companionship for watches. **Snare Crafting** adds trap utility. Strong but narrow — the Ranger's utility is heavily wilderness-centric and less useful in urban or social campaigns.

### Sorcerer
Spell list depends on bloodline, giving access to any of the four traditions. **Divine** and **Occult** bloodlines provide exceptional utility spells (divination, healing, mental manipulation). More spell slots than any other caster means more room to prepare utility spells alongside combat options. **Signature Spells** allow flexible heightening of key utility spells without dedicating extra slots. No unique utility mechanic like Bard's compositions or Cleric's font, but raw spell slot quantity means always having something available.

## C Tier — Limited Utility

### Barbarian
Athletics skill for climbing, swimming, and breaking things. Raw physical presence for Intimidation. But **Rage blocks concentrate actions** — no casting, no Recall Knowledge (without feat tax), no careful investigation while raging. No special exploration or social class features. The most one-dimensional class for utility purposes. Free Archetype helps less than for other martials because Rage prevents using most caster dedication spells.

### Fighter
Zero utility class features — the Fighter's out-of-combat value comes entirely from skills and Free Archetype. With Free Archetype providing a caster or skill dedication, Fighters can cover Intimidation for social pressure, Athletics for physical challenges, and basic spellcasting for utility. But the core class contributes nothing to non-combat situations.

### Magus
Access to the arcane spell list provides some utility options (Identify Magic, Dispel Magic, basic divination), but very limited spell slots (fewer than any other full caster) mean most slots go to combat spells for Spellstrike. Out of combat, the Magus is a worse Wizard with fewer resources. Free Archetype helps marginally.

### Monk
Great mobility for scouting and reaching difficult areas (wall running, water walking, incredible movement speed). **Ki spells** add minor utility. High saves make Monks reliable for triggering traps and resisting environmental hazards. But skill selection is limited and the class is fundamentally combat-focused.

### Psychic
The occult list has strong utility spells (divination, illusion, enchantment), but the Psychic has fewer slots than a Bard and no unique utility mechanic beyond Amps. A less versatile Bard for utility purposes, though Amped utility spells can be powerful when available.

### Summoner
**Eidolon** can scout independently, perform tasks at range from the summoner, or serve as a second body for complex challenges. Some spell utility from the bounded spell list. But limited slots and shared action economy make utility spell usage awkward — every utility spell is one fewer combat option.

### Swashbuckler
Charisma-based, giving strong social skills — Diplomacy, Deception, Intimidation, and Performance. The Panache mechanic encourages flashy, memorable social interactions. With Free Archetype, Swashbucklers can expand into full face-character territory. But utility beyond being a social specialist is limited.

---

## D Tier — Utility is an Afterthought

### Witch
The **familiar** provides scouting capability and extra cantrips — genuinely useful. **Cackle** sustaining hexes is nice for long-duration effects. Patron determines spell list, so utility options exist. But familiar dependency is fragile (if your familiar dies, you lose significant capability), the worst proficiencies of any caster undermine everything, and every utility niche the Witch could fill is done better by the class that natively owns that spell list. C-tier utility ideas trapped in a D-tier chassis.

### Guardian
Pure tank design with almost no utility outside "I protect people." Skills are limited, no utility class features. Intercept and Taunt are combat-only. The Guardian exists for combat and has virtually nothing to offer in exploration or social pillars.

### Gunslinger
Near-zero utility class features. No skill-enhancing abilities, no exploration tools, no social features. The class identity is "I shoot things" and that's the entire scope. Free Archetype can patch some gaps, but the core class contributes nothing to non-combat situations.

### Inventor
**Innovation** could theoretically solve problems creatively — armor innovations for environmental protection, construct innovations for scouting. But the **Unstable** mechanic means creative utility usage risks breaking your innovation. Better concept than execution for utility purposes. Crafting affinity helps with downtime activities.
