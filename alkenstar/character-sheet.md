## Appearance

Ancient Jistkan craftsmanship — intricate engravings worn smooth by millennia. Elegant and clearly not from this era, out of place in the grimy Ferrous Quarter. Soul gem faintly visible through the chest plating, pulsing with a dim light that intensified after the awakening. Traces of earth and vine now creep through joints and seams — the elements slowly making themselves at home in an ancient shell.

## Backstory

An ancient Jistkan automaton, deactivated for millennia somewhere in the Mana Wastes. Found and repaired by **Eric Dunne**, a young scrapper and inventor working in the Ferrous Quarter. Eric couldn't read the full Jistkan script etched into the soul gem casing — *VOREKANTHUS* — but he pulled a name out of it anyway. Sol.

Eric treated Sol like a person, not a machine. Sol helped around the workshop — not fully awake, more like a sleepwalker following instructions, but learning. It was the closest thing to a home Sol had known in thousands of years, even if he didn't understand that yet.

Then Eric vanished. No explanation, no goodbye. Sol was seized — maybe by debt collectors, maybe by someone who saw an ownerless construct as free labor. He was put to work in the Ferrous Quarter factories, hauling slag and moving earth. He didn't resist because he didn't know he could. He was still operating on autopilot: do what you're told, follow the last set of instructions.

Then someone important died, and Deputy **Anjelique Loveless** needed a scapegoat. Who better than a construct with no rights, no voice, no one to speak for him? The arrest warrant came. Sol saw Loveless's signature on it.

And something broke open. The soul gem cracked — maybe it was fear, the first real emotion Sol had felt. Maybe a Mana Wastes surge destabilized the ancient planar conduit inside him. The earth beneath the factory floor answered. Wood burst through the walls. Stone encased him like armor. Sol didn't understand what was happening, but for the first time, he moved on his own. He ran.

## Personality

Quiet and stoic. Observes more than he speaks. Still learning what it means to be awake — to choose, to want, to protect. When he does talk, it carries weight. Not cold, just deliberate. There's something underneath the silence — a slowly growing awareness that he cares about the people around him more than he expected to.

## Motivations

- **Find Eric Dunne** — the one person who treated him as more than parts. What happened to him?
- **Understand what he is** — the power inside him is ancient. What was Vorekanthus built to be? Why is the kinetic gate awakening now?
