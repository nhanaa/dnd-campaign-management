# PF2e Remastered — Best Overall Class Tier List

> **System:** Pathfinder 2e Remastered (Player Core 1 & 2)
> **Variant Rule:** Free Archetype
> **Methodology:** Consensus of three expert perspectives — The Optimizer (raw mechanical power), The Storyteller (versatility across all pillars of play), and The Tactician (party synergy and battlefield control). Rankings reflect overall class strength considering combat effectiveness, versatility, survivability, action economy, skill utility, and how Free Archetype enhances each class.

---

## S Tier — The Best of the Best

### Bard
The undisputed king of force multiplication. **Courageous Anthem** (Inspire Courage) provides +1 status bonus to attack rolls AND damage for the entire party, every single round, as a cantrip — no resource cost. At level 6, **Dirge of Doom** inflicts Frightened 1 on all enemies in an emanation with no saving throw. Stack those two and your party is effectively at +2 relative to enemies on attacks. The occult spell list gives access to Slow, Synesthesia, and top-tier control/debuff spells. Legendary in a social skill via Versatile Performance. With Free Archetype, Bard can grab a martial or caster dedication to fill any remaining gap. There is never a situation where a Bard has nothing meaningful to do.

### Fighter
The mathematical king of PF2e's bounded accuracy system. **Legendary weapon proficiency** gives +2 to hit over every other martial class at key breakpoints — in a system where a +1 translates to roughly 5% more hits and 5% more crits, this is enormous. **Attack of Opportunity** at level 1 controls enemy positioning and punishes casters. **Combat Flexibility** at level 9 gives daily feat swapping. Every buff the party provides is worth more on a Fighter because their base accuracy is higher. With Free Archetype, Fighters grab a caster dedication and lose nothing from their core identity.

### Kineticist
The unlimited resource class in a game built around resource attrition. Zero spell slots, zero focus points — just elemental blasts and impulses all day long. Equally effective in fight #1 and fight #6. Excellent battlefield control (walls, difficult terrain, hazardous zones), sustained damage via Elemental Blast scaling, and gate junctions that provide free resistances and immunities. Constitution-based, so inherently tanky. With Free Archetype, Kineticists patch the social/skill utility they'd otherwise lack.

## A Tier — Excellent with Minor Gaps

### Cleric
**Healing Font** (or Harm Font) provides free extra Heal/Harm spells equal to Charisma modifier, freeing up spell slots for buff/debuff/utility. The divine spell list excels at condition removal (Restoration, Remove Disease, Remove Curse), party buffs (Heroism, Freedom of Movement, Spirit Link), and divination. Cloistered Cleric provides full caster power; Warpriest provides frontline capability. The party's lifeline and the answer to "can we fix this condition?" Borderline S-tier — held back only because the divine list is the weakest for offensive/control compared to occult or primal.

### Champion
**Champion's Reaction** (Retributive Strike, Liberating Step, or Glimpse of Redemption) is the best defensive reaction in the game — it triggers to protect *allies*, reduces damage, and can punish or restrict the attacker. Legendary armor proficiency gives the highest AC possible. Lay on Hands provides efficient healing. Aura of Courage helps the whole party against fear. The gold standard for protecting teammates. Remastered Champion opened up alignment flexibility, which was a needed improvement. Offense is the gap — Champion hits adequately but never excels at dealing damage.

### Druid
The primal spell list is arguably the most versatile in the game: healing, blasting, battlefield control, summoning, and exploration tools in one package. Four orders provide dramatically different playstyles — Wild Shape for scouting and melee versatility, Storm for blasting, Animal for an independent companion adding action economy. With Free Archetype, a Druid can cover nearly any party gap. The "jack-of-all-trades" criticism is fair — Wild Shape builds sacrifice spellcasting, pure caster builds sacrifice martial capability — but the breadth of options keeps Druid firmly in A-tier.

### Thaumaturge
**Exploit Vulnerability** (Personal Antithesis) adds flat bonus damage equal to 2 + half level on every Strike against the target, with no save and no resource cost. Charisma-based martial with strong social skills. **Diverse Lore** and **Esoteric Lore** make Recall Knowledge checks against literally anything viable. Implements (Amulet, Lantern, Tome, Mirror, etc.) provide diverse out-of-combat tools. An underrated class that combines reliable damage, unmatched monster knowledge, and genuine utility.

### Ranger
**Hunt Prey** combined with **Precision Edge** provides reliable flat bonus damage on the first hit each round. **Flurry Edge** with Twin Takedown or Hunted Shot gives excellent action economy for dual-weapon or ranged builds. Animal companion builds add another body and effectively more actions. Strong wilderness exploration (Track, nature skills, terrain movement). Self-sufficient and versatile — the complete martial package.

## B Tier — Solid and Reliable

### Commander
The most debated class on this list. **Tactics** grant allies extra Strikes, Strides, and maneuvers as reactions or free actions outside their turns. **Pincer Attack** (1 action) makes all enemies off-guard to melee squadmates. **Pop, Drop, and Lock** (Master) gives 3 ally reactions. **Banner aura** provides +1 Will saves in 30 feet. The Commander generates the most valuable resource in PF2e — extra party actions — but is heavily party-dependent and can't function independently. Strong in coordinated parties, mediocre solo. *Dissenting opinion: The Storyteller argued A-tier; the Optimizer argued this was closer to C-tier.*

### Monk
**Flurry of Blows** (2 Strikes for 1 action) is outstanding personal action economy. Incredible mobility, all three saves progressing to at least Expert (and Will to Master), and ki spells add versatility. Mountain Stance and Crane Stance builds are defensively strong. The Monk excels at being hard to kill and covering ground, but doesn't create advantages for teammates — it benefits the party by being a self-sufficient striker who doesn't need support.

### Sorcerer
The most spell slots of any caster, thanks to spontaneous casting and bloodline slots. Bloodline choice determines spell list, giving access to any of the four traditions. **Signature Spells** allow flexible heightening. **Blood Magic** effects add flavor and mechanical value. Solid but lacks a unique team mechanic — Bard has compositions, Cleric has font, Druid has Wild Shape. More slots + Signature Spells is genuinely strong, but being good at "casting more spells" doesn't differentiate enough for A-tier.

### Magus
**Spellstrike** combining a top-rank spell with a weapon Strike delivers the highest single-hit burst damage in the game. **True Strike** + Spellstrike eliminates the main risk of missing. **Starlit Span** does this at range; **Inexorable Iron** provides temp HP for tanking. But the Spellstrike → Recharge cycle creates feast-or-famine action economy, limited spell slots restrict versatility, and out of combat the Magus is a worse Wizard. Burst king, but inconsistent over a full adventuring day.

### Barbarian
12 HP per level is the highest in the game. **Rage** provides a flat damage bonus on every hit that's massive at all levels. **Giant Instinct** with a d12 weapon + Rage hits like a truck. Simple, effective, durable. But Rage carries real costs: -1 AC penalty, inability to use concentrate actions (no casting, no Recall Knowledge without feat tax, no Seek). This makes Barbarian the most one-dimensional martial — incredible at hitting things, limited at everything else. *Dissenting opinion: The Storyteller argued C-tier due to Rage's anti-synergy with Free Archetype caster dedications.*

### Swashbuckler
The **Panache** system encourages dynamic, stylish play — Tumble Through, Demoralize, feint to gain Panache, then unleash a devastating **Finisher**. Charisma-based for strong social skills. Opportune Riposte gives defensive reactions. But Panache management can be fiddly — lose it on Finisher, must earn it back next round. High skill ceiling but inconsistent floor. Great duelist, limited team contribution.

### Rogue
The best skill progression in the game — most trained skills at level 1, fastest path to Expert/Master/Legendary proficiency. **Sneak Attack** with reliable Off-Guard (flanking, party debuffs) provides strong damage. **Debilitations** at higher levels add real debuffs (Enfeebled 1, -10 ft Speed). Reactive playstyle that rewards party coordination. The ultimate skill monkey, but needs allies to set up flanking reliably.

### Oracle
The most improved class in the Remaster. The **cursebound** mechanic is better designed than legacy — the power payoff is intentionally worth the curse cost. **Cosmos Mystery** has minimal curse penalty with strong offensive/utility mix. 2 focus points at level 1 is generous. Full divine caster with 4 slots per rank plus unique mystery spells that no other class can access. But harder to play optimally than Cleric and curse management adds variance that can punish new players. *Dissenting opinion: The Storyteller argued A-tier based on mystery spell uniqueness.*

### Wizard
The arcane list has the widest spell selection in the game, and prepared casting means always having the right tool for the job. Spell Blending and school specialization help efficiency. But the Remastered Wizard lost some of its former identity — thesis options feel weaker, the chassis is fragile (8 HP, poor Fortitude), and the class has no unique team mechanic comparable to compositions or font. A Bard or Cleric provides more team value; a Sorcerer has more raw slots for blasting.

## C Tier — Functional but Flawed

### Guardian
The dedicated tank class from Player Core 2. **Taunt** is a guaranteed -1 circumstance penalty (no save). **Intercept Attack** with physical resistance equal to half-level reduces total party damage taken. 12 HP per level. But the **Tank Fallacy** is real in PF2e — enemies with any intelligence can simply walk past you. Limited utility outside of protection, and Champion's Reaction is often more action-efficient for the ally-protection role. Solid concept, narrow execution.

### Psychic
**Amped cantrips** are unique and powerful — Amped Imaginary Weapon hits hard. The occult list is strong for control and debuffs. But 6 HP per level is the lowest of any class, and the Bard does occult support strictly better while also buffing the whole party. **Unleash Psyche** is feast-or-famine. A glass cannon in a system that punishes glass cannons.

### Investigator
**Devise a Stratagem** is brilliant for knowledge-focused play, and **Pursue a Lead** + **Clue In** make this the best investigation class in the game. Incredible out of combat — peerless knowledge, great skills. But Devise a Stratagem costs an action every combat turn, making action economy clunky in fights. Amazing out of combat, mediocre in combat = C-tier overall.

### Summoner
Two bodies on the field creates unique action economy potential. Eidolon customization is deep and flavorful. But the shared HP pool creates risk, the 3-action split between summoner and eidolon is complex to optimize, and tandem actions are unreliable. High ceiling when everything goes right, inconsistent floor when it doesn't.

### Inventor
**Innovation** customization (armor, weapon, construct) is interesting design space. Unstable abilities offer huge payoffs — but risk breaking your innovation on a failure. Too swingy for reliable contribution and too self-focused for consistent team play. Some builds (armor innovation) are solid; others (construct) struggle.

### Gunslinger
Cool flavor and genuine mechanical fantasy. **Fatal** trait on firearms produces massive critical hit damage. **Way of the Sniper** has strong burst alpha strikes. But reload tax devastates sustained DPR, the class has zero party support, zero battlefield control, and zero utility features outside of shooting things. A Ranger does the ranged damage role better with significantly more team value. The narrowest class design in PF2e.

---

## D Tier — Needs Help

### Witch
The consensus lowest-ranked class in PF2e Remastered. **Evil Eye** (no-save -1 to target's checks) combined with **Cackle** (sustain hex for free) is a legitimate debuff loop, and the familiar provides extra cantrips and scouting. But the Witch has the worst proficiencies of any full spellcaster, only one good save (Will), fragile familiar dependency (familiar dies = major problems), and no unique identity beyond "worse version of another caster." Every spell list the Witch can access is better served by the class that natively owns it. *Dissenting opinion: The Tactician argued C-tier, noting Evil Eye + Cackle has legitimate tactical value.*
