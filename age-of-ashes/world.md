## Breachill

A 170-year-old settlement of ~1,300 residents in eastern Isger, approximately 50 miles from the Druman border. Founded by human amnesiacs mentored by the wizard **Lamond Breachton**, whose bronze statue stands in Monument Circle at the town center (marble plaza with six wells). The town values self-sufficiency, perseverance, and community. Insulting Breachton's legacy is taken as a personal offense by most residents.

### The Call for Heroes

A monthly town council tradition where adventurers address local problems — missing shipments, livestock deaths, crop failures. The campaign begins with the PCs attending this gathering.

## Key Themes

- **Dahak**: Dragon god of destruction. The campaign's central antagonist force. Cults of Dahak are operating in and around Breachill. Zane's fire nightmares are connected to this threat.
- **Citadel Altaerein**: Abandoned Hellknight fortress (Order of the Nail) near Breachill. The party eventually acquires and rebuilds it as a base of operations.
- **Portal Network**: An ancient ring of portals discovered during the first arc. Leads to locations across Golarion.
- **Isger**: Chelaxian vassal state. Post-Goblinblood Wars. The Chitterwood nearby is where the goblin hordes emerged.

## Storyline

### Session 10 — Through the Hunter's Gate (2026-05-03)
- **Four weeks of downtime** since the Citadel was reclaimed. The Wildscale Conclave has been cleaning, repairing, and administering the fortress.
- **Zane Alphire arrives** in Breachill, drawn by visions of fire and a burning dragon-claw symbol. He recognizes the symbol on the Conclave's hiring poster as the *exact* one from his nightmares.
- The party (Birch, Varkos, Meepos via DM-mechanics, and now Zane) returns to the **Alseta Ring**. Birch and Meepos's ritual confirms the **Lotus Gate** (Alcera's) is permanently broken — physically and magically.
- The **Hunter's Gate** (Ketephys's, elven god of the hunt) accepts the leaf-shaped arrowhead Birch has been carrying. The gate activates.
- Stepping through: a hardened-lava tunnel haunted by a smoke-dragon shape ("**the Grand Obscurity**"). The party makes Reflex saves and runs through to the second gate.
- They emerge in a **temple of Ketephys** in the **Mwangi Expanse** — thousands of miles south, on another continent. The temple was raided by Cinder Claws days ago. A *white-dragon-headed wooden column* added by the cultists lies broken on the ground.
- Twenty **Ekujae** warriors (jungle elves) ring the temple. Birch's diplomacy disarms them. Lead warrior **Jahsi** invites the party home to **Akrivel**, the Ekujae canopy city.
- At Akrivel, the linguist **Nketiah** welcomes them. Tour: no shops, no currency, gold is "cursed" — only "purest warriors" wear gold.
- The Cinder Claws have placed a **magical curse** on the deep jungle: only the Ekujae are blocked. Animals and other clans pass through freely. The Akuje druids cannot dissipate it. The party may be able to walk through where the Ekujae cannot.
- Birch consults with the druids; Zane studies herbal healing. Session ends pre-feast.

## Locations (extended)

### Akrivel
**Ekujae jungle city built into the canopy of the Mwangi Expanse.** Wood-and-fabric platforms connected by vine bridges, accessed by a pulley elevator at the base. No shops, no currency. Trade is person-to-person, mediated by linguists. Gold is "cursed" — only senior warriors wear it. Linguists are marked by white face-paint. Distinctive features: a lioness chilling on a high branch, kids playing a kicking-dance game with ankle bracelets, druid tent covered in living leaves.

### Ketephys's Temple (Mwangi Expanse)
**Open-air jungle temple** of Ketephys, elven god of the hunt. Stone arches, branches and roots tangled into the architecture. Recently sacked by the Cinder Claws — vegetation burnt, blood on the stones, a half-finished white-dragon-headed wooden column added by the cultists and broken on the ground. The Hunter's Gate emerges directly into this temple.

### The Alseta Ring (Citadel basement)
**Six-gate elven portal nexus** beneath Citadel Altaerein. Six *ayudharas* (ancient elven teleportation portals) ring a central fountain with statues of six elven gods. Demi-plane-linked, one-to-one. Currently accessible:
- **Lotus Gate** (Alcera) — Permanently broken, physically and magically. Fragmented stone has been pocketed for later identification.
- **Hunter's Gate** (Ketephys) — Functional. Activated by inserting the leaf-shaped arrowhead Birch carries. Connects to the Ketephys temple in the Mwangi via a hardened-lava tunnel hosting *the Grand Obscurity*.
- **Other four gates** — Functional, gods unidentified, destinations unknown.

## Factions

### Wildscale Conclave
The party. Owners of Citadel Altaerein. Mission as of Session 10: investigate the Cinder Claw cult, including by walking through the cursed jungle in Mwangi where the Ekujae cannot.

### Ekujae
Jungle-elf guardians of the Mwangi Expanse. Multiple clans (Leopold, River, others). Worship varies — Ketephys/Yuelral/Wildsong druidic traditions all present. Tradition: chop enemies and feed them to nature. Currently locked out of much of their own jungle by a Cinder Claw curse. Potential allies of the Wildscale Conclave.

### The Cinder Claw
Dragon-claw-symbol cult. Confirmed cross-continental (Breachill + Mwangi). Operating in the jungle; placed a magical barrier targeting only the Ekujae. Tied (per player suspicion) to Dahak. Composition includes baboon-creatures and boggard-cultists per Zane's visions.
