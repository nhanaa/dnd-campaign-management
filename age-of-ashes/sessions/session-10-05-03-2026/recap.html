Session Summary

Zane Alphire arrives in Breachill chasing a vision of fire he can't shake — and finds the town intact, the Citadel in fresh repair, and a "Now Hiring" poster bearing the exact dragon-claw symbol from his nightmares. He climbs the hill to the fortress, gets vetted by Birch (no necromancy, no Wildsong), demonstrates a kineticist's pull on a stray rock, and is hired into the Wildscale Conclave on the spot. The introductions are warm but careful: Birch swears him to secrecy before letting him anywhere near what's downstairs.

What's downstairs is the Alseta Ring — six gates, six elven gods, and a working portal to somewhere very far away. Birch and Meepos magically confirm what Meepos's notes had been heading toward: the Lotus Gate (Alcera's) is broken beyond their skill to repair, but the Hunter's Gate (Ketephys's, elven god of the hunt) accepts the leaf-shaped arrowhead Birch has been carrying. Mist swirls. Monkey calls and humid air spill into the chamber. The party gears up, steps through, and finds themselves in a lava-glassed tunnel where something massive is breathing behind them — a half-formed dragon of fire and smoke. They run, save against the flames, and stumble out into a ruined open-air temple of Ketephys in the Mwangi Expanse, thousands of miles from home.

The temple is surrounded by twenty Ekujae warriors with bows half-drawn. Birch's diplomacy and elven tongue defuses them. Their leader, Jahsi — gold-jeweled, calm, a worshipper of Yuelral — tells them the Cinder Claws raided this temple days ago. He invites the party home to Akrivel, a city built into the canopy of the jungle. There they meet Nketiah, the village's linguist, who welcomes them with an enthusiasm both Zane and Varkos read as off. The Ekujae have been cursed: any of their kin who walks into the deep jungle ends up back where they started after thirty minutes. Animals get through. Other humanoid clans get through. Only the Ekujae are locked out — and the Cinder Claws are somewhere on the other side of that barrier. Birch consults with the Akuje druids. Zane studies herbal medicine at their healing tent. Varkos sweats and looks for something to barter for. The session ends just before the welcome feast, where Nketiah has promised to tell her favorite story — about the day the Ekujae locked something called the Grand Obscurity inside a gate.

Timeline

Act I · Roleplay · Hiring at the Citadel

The New Hire

  • Four weeks of downtime. Varkos and Birch have been cleaning the Citadel with the goblin "Bumble Brashers." Meepos has been administering bureaucracy and identifying loot in the basement. The staircase down to the Alseta Ring is now repaired.
  • Zane arrives in Breachill — mid-April, sunny. The fire from his vision hasn't come to pass, but he sees the Wildscale Conclave's "Now Hiring" notice in the newspaper and recognizes the symbol on it as the exact one from his nightmares: a burning dragon's claw. He walks up to the Citadel.
  • The interview. Varkos rappels from the battlements with a grappling gun. Birch sets down his magical broom and water buckets. Zane introduces himself as a healer with a kit and "good with my hands." Birch tries Wildsong on him to see if he's a druid (no). Birch's first hard question: "Your abilities have nothing to do with raising the dead, are they?" Zane: no. Birch is satisfied.
  • The demonstration. Zane pulls a rock through the air past the party's faces — a kineticist's first proof. Varkos: "Not bad. Not bad." Zane is hired.
  • Backstory exchange. Varkos recaps the Citadel arc — the Bloody Blades, the Dracodile, the high-elf necromancer Vaas (Varkos beheaded with a baseball swing), the Cinder Claw cult underground. Birch names his "biggest regret": the apprentice necromancer who escaped. Varkos's solution: "If we ever meet her again, we can just cut off her legs."
  • The warning. They tell Zane the cult is well-connected, possibly cross-continental, and invite him to back out before things get serious. "I have made that decision to join. Then I will not back down. I'm not that type of person."
Act II · Mystery · The Alseta Ring

Six Gates, Six Gods

  • The Alseta Ring. Six gates, six elven god statues around a central fountain. Meepos is already down there with notes spread across the stones, working on the gate carved with moons, vegetation, and falcons.
  • Identifying the gates. With a nat 20 Religion/Society, Birch knows these are ayudharas — ancient elven teleportation portals, far older than current civilizations, demi-plane-linked, one-to-one only. The Lotus Gate (Alcera, the campaign's titular goddess) sits opposite the broken statue. The Hunter's Gate (Ketephys, elven god of the hunt — Birch keeps calling him "Cataphis," Varkos riffs on it as "Catachan" / "Catface") sits in front of the moon/falcon archway.
  • The ritual. Birch and Meepos perform a Wildsong-anchored attunement ritual — branches, feathers, rocks, three sentences carved into the floor — to investigate the Lotus Gate. The verdict is final: physically and magically broken. Varkos pockets a chunk of the broken stone for later identification. Birch's Crafting check confirms the gate could be repaired, but only by someone with master-level elven knowledge that has been "long gone" for thousands of years.
  • The arrowhead fits. Birch's nat-20 Perception spots that one of the leaves carved into the Hunter's Gate is exactly the shape of the arrowhead in his pack. He inserts it. The mist forms. Forest sounds, monkey calls, exotic flowers, deep humidity. "That explains a lot," Birch says. Recall Knowledge: the monkey screeches sound similar to but higher-pitched than the cult baboons they fought below the Citadel.
  • Secrecy. Birch makes Zane swear not to spread word of the gate before they continue.
  • Loot prep. Meepos's identifies are processed. Varkos claims the +1 breastplate, takes a Cinder Claw dragon-scale gauntlet (scale type unknown), and accepts a fear gem for his Intimidating Strikes. Zane takes an invisibility potion. Antidotes go around. Meepos's downtime activity transferred a striking rune onto Varkos's halberd.
Act III · Hazard · The Lava Tunnel

The Smoke-Dragon

  • Through the gate, into fire. A hardened-lava tunnel about 100 feet long, glowing orange in the cracks, second gate visible at the end. The air smells of smoke. The ground was once stone; now it has melted and re-frozen into glass.
  • The crevices blink. As they advance, light flickers in the cracks. Varkos warns the party: "I recommend that we probably make haste getting out of here."
  • Something behind them. A growl too big for the tunnel. They look back — a dragon-shape of smoke and fire forming in the cracks, jaws opening, fire growing inside.
  • Reflex saves all around. Varkos rolled 6, spent a Hero Point to reroll, succeeded; invoked Quick-Tempered as a free action and ran. Zane rolled 17 on Expert Reflex — declined Varkos's offer of a Hero Point reroll. Birch and Meepos through the second gate just behind Varkos. Zane last; the heat peaked as he stepped into the mist.
  • Armor in Earth, always-on. Mid-scramble, Zane declared his standing posture: he'd recast every 10 minutes by default. The DM gave him narrative permission to treat it as permanent during exploration.
Act IV · Roleplay · Ketephys's Temple

Twenty Bows, Half-Drawn

  • Out of the mist into a jungle. Sticky humidity, stone arches, branches and roots tangled into the temple's architecture. Recently sacked: vegetation burnt, blood on the stones, an open-air pavilion in the middle of the deep jungle.
  • The white-dragon column. A half-finished wooden column with a white dragon's head carved on top, snapped off the column and lying broken on the ground. Varkos's open Perception reveals the column doesn't belong to this temple at all — the aesthetic is wrong, and the head was broken from it recently. The Cinder Claws planted it.
  • The bows. Twenty Ekujae warriors ringed around the temple — dark braided hair, dark skin, warrior face paint. Bows charged but not loosed. Sense Motive reveals they're scared, not aggressive.
  • Birch defuses. Hands raised, greeting in elven, Diplomacy success. The bows lower.
  • Jahsi. The leader steps forward — gold jewelry on his arms, no drawn weapon, calm. "I am Jahsi of the Leopold clan. My mother is of the River clan." He worships Yuelral, elven goddess of magic and crystals; her edicts require herbal healing and patience over magic. He politely declines Zane's healing offer for the still-fresh wound on his neck.
  • Where they are. A high Society/Nature check places the party in the Mwangi Expanse, thousands of miles south of Breachill, on another continent. The bow-armed warriors are Ekujae — jungle-elf guardians, multiple clans, "we do not eat our enemies; we chop them to give them to the nature."
  • The temple's recent past. Cinder Claws raided this temple days ago. They left several Ekujae dead and wounded Jahsi himself with the trinket he wears around his neck. Jahsi believes the elders may want to make alliance with the Wildscale Conclave once they understand who killed the cultists back home.
Act V · Exploration · Akrivel in the Canopy

The City in the Trees

  • ~9 miles to Akrivel. The walk takes a few hours through humidity that would kill a Breachill native. Around noon they arrive at a city built entirely in the canopy: vine bridges, wood-and-fabric platforms, a pulley elevator at the base. Varkos, Zane, and Birch take the elevator. Jahsi and his warriors climb. "None of the Kūjē do judge you for taking the easy way up."
  • Nketiah. A young front-elf greets the party at the top — fluent common, no accent, one wooden arm, a great deal of jewelry. She is the village's linguist (a status role, marked by white face-paint). Welcoming, warm, too warm. Both Zane and Varkos read her as having a motive.
  • The tour. A lioness chilling on a high branch. Children playing a kicking-dance game where the goal is to strike each other's ankle bracelets. Hunters preparing for the night's feast. No shops. The Ekujae trade person-to-person; the linguist coordinates between clans.
  • "Frivolous money." Birch observes the Ekujae "don't need any frivolous things such as money." Varkos extends his hand for the share Birch is wasting.
  • Gold is cursed. When Varkos mentions gold/silver/platinum, Nketiah recoils. Only "the purest warriors" wear gold here — everything else is brass. Jahsi's gold marks his rank. Nketiah says her father has gold too. "Maybe you are gonna die soon because of it. A curse can take a lot of time to take place."
Act VI · Mystery · The Cinder Claw Curse

The Jungle Bends Back

  • The barrier. The Cinder Claws have placed a magical curse on the deep jungle. Ekujae who walk into it return after thirty minutes to where they started. Other clans (Gripplis, Anadis) and animals pass through freely. Only the Ekujae are blocked.
  • Druids cannot dissipate it. They've identified that magic is at work but can't read the ritual or the source. Jahsi brought the party to Akrivel in part because they're not Ekujae — they might walk through where his people cannot.
  • Birch and the druids. Birch finds the druid tent — three druids arguing in Wildsong about how much power to draw to break the curse. He introduces himself as a Leaf druid from another land and states his mission: "I came here to investigate a cult known as the Cinder Claw. Their actions have done much harm to nature and other lands, and I came here to stop them." They agree to share advice and tools tomorrow.
  • Zane at the medical tent. With Nketiah translating, Zane spends the afternoon learning Ekujae herbal-paste and bone-setting techniques. New healing knowledge for his kit.
  • Varkos, sweating. Wanders, swats mosquitoes, looks for something to barter for, declines food. He's wary of Nketiah and won't accept her hospitality lightly.
  • Hint dropped. Nketiah lets slip that the Ekujae are "guardians of the gate" and that long ago they locked something called the Grand Obscurity inside it. They've been guarding ever since. She'll tell the full story at tonight's feast. The smoke-dragon in the lava tunnel is almost certainly it.
