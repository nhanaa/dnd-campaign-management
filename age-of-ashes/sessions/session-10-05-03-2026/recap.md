## Session Summary

Zane Alphire arrives in Breachill chasing a vision of fire he can't shake — and finds the town intact, the Citadel in fresh repair, and a "Now Hiring" poster bearing the exact dragon-claw symbol from his nightmares. He climbs the hill to the fortress, gets vetted by Birch (no necromancy, no Wildsong), demonstrates a kineticist's pull on a stray rock, and is hired into the Wildscale Conclave on the spot. The introductions are warm but careful: Birch swears him to secrecy before letting him anywhere near what's downstairs.

What's downstairs is the Alseta Ring — six gates, six elven gods, and a working portal to somewhere very far away. Birch and Meepos magically confirm what Meepos's notes had been heading toward: the Lotus Gate (Alcera's) is broken beyond their skill to repair, but the Hunter's Gate (Ketephys's, elven god of the hunt) accepts the leaf-shaped arrowhead Birch has been carrying. Mist swirls. Monkey calls and humid air spill into the chamber. The party gears up, steps through, and finds themselves in a lava-glassed tunnel where something massive is breathing behind them — a half-formed dragon of fire and smoke. They run, save against the flames, and stumble out into a ruined open-air temple of Ketephys in the Mwangi Expanse, thousands of miles from home.

The temple is surrounded by twenty Ekujae warriors with bows half-drawn. Birch's diplomacy and elven tongue defuses them. Their leader, **Jahsi** — gold-jeweled, calm, a worshipper of Yuelral — tells them the Cinder Claws raided this temple days ago. He invites the party home to **Akrivel**, a city built into the canopy of the jungle. There they meet **Nketiah**, the village's linguist, who welcomes them with an enthusiasm both Zane and Varkos read as off. The Ekujae have been cursed: any of their kin who walks into the deep jungle ends up back where they started after thirty minutes. Animals get through. Other humanoid clans get through. Only the Ekujae are locked out — and the Cinder Claws are somewhere on the other side of that barrier. Birch consults with the Akuje druids. Zane studies herbal medicine at their healing tent. Varkos sweats and looks for something to barter for. The session ends just before the welcome feast, where Nketiah has promised to tell her favorite story — about the day the Ekujae locked something called *the Grand Obscurity* inside a gate.

## Key Events

### Act I — Hiring at the Citadel (00:00–58:00)

- **Four weeks of downtime.** Varkos and Birch have been cleaning the Citadel with the goblin "Bumble Brashers." Meepos has been administering bureaucracy and identifying loot in the basement. The staircase down to the Alseta Ring is now repaired.
- **Zane arrives in Breachill.** Mid-April, sunny. Town buzzing with talk of the Wildscale Conclave's deeds. The vision he chased — fire, baboon laughter, boggard cultists wearing a burning dragon claw — hasn't come to pass. He sees the Conclave's "Now Hiring" notice in the newspaper, recognizes the symbol on it as the *exact* one from his nightmares, and walks up to the Citadel.
- **The interview.** Varkos rappels down from the battlements with a grappling gun. Birch puts down his magical broom and water buckets. Zane introduces himself as a healer with a kit and "good with my hands." Birch tries Wildsong on him to see if he's a druid (no). Birch's first hard question: *"Your abilities have nothing to do with raising the dead, are they?"* Zane answers no. Birch is satisfied.
- **The demonstration.** Zane pulls a rock through the air past the party's faces — a kineticist's first proof. Varkos: "Not bad. Not bad." He's hired.
- **Backstory exchange.** Varkos recaps the Citadel arc — the Bloody Blades, the Dracodile, the high-elf necromancer Vaas (Varkos beheaded with a baseball swing), the Cinder Claw cult underground. Birch names his "biggest regret": the apprentice necromancer who escaped. Varkos's solution: *"If we ever meet her again, we can just cut off her legs."* They tell Zane the cult is well-connected, possibly cross-continental, and invite him to back out before things get serious. He declines.

### Act II — The Hunter's Gate (58:00–130:00)

- **The Alseta Ring.** Six gates, six elven god statues around a central fountain. Meepos is already down there with notes spread across the stones, working on the gate carved with moons, vegetation, and falcons.
- **Identifying the gates.** With a nat 20 Religion/Society, Birch knows these are *ayudharas* — ancient elven teleportation portals, far older than current civilizations, demi-plane-linked, one-to-one only. The Lotus Gate (Alcera, the campaign's titular goddess) sits opposite the broken statue. The Hunter's Gate (Ketephys, elven god of the hunt — Birch keeps calling him "Cataphis," Varkos riffs on it as "Catachan" / "Catface") sits in front of the moon/falcon archway.
- **The ritual.** Birch and Meepos perform a Wildsong-anchored attunement ritual — branches, feathers, rocks, three sentences carved into the floor — to investigate the Lotus Gate. The verdict is final: *physically and magically broken.* Varkos pockets a chunk of the broken stone for later identification. Birch's Crafting check confirms the gate could be repaired, but only by someone with master-level elven knowledge that has been "long gone" for thousands of years.
- **The arrowhead fits.** Birch's nat-20 Perception spots that one of the leaves carved into the Hunter's Gate is *exactly* the shape of the arrowhead in his pack. He inserts it. The mist forms. Forest sounds, monkey calls, exotic flowers, deep humidity. *"Oh, that explains a lot,"* Birch says. Recall Knowledge: the monkey screeches sound similar to but higher-pitched than the cult baboons they fought below the Citadel.
- **Secrecy.** Birch makes Zane swear not to spread word of the gate before they continue.
- **Loot prep.** Meepos's identifies are processed. Varkos claims the +1 breastplate, takes a Cinder Claw dragon-scale gauntlet (scale type unknown), and accepts a fear gem for his Intimidating Strikes. Zane takes an invisibility potion. Antidotes go around (one each). Meepos's downtime activity transferred a striking rune onto Varkos's halberd: now a +1 striking weapon.
- **Through the gate, into fire.** A hardened-lava tunnel about 100 feet long, glowing orange in the cracks, second gate visible at the end. The air smells of smoke. As they advance, a fire-and-smoke dragon-shape forms in the cracks behind them. Reflex saves all around. Varkos rolls a 6, spends a Hero Point to reroll → success. Zane rolls 17 → success (declines the Hero Point reroll). Everyone makes the second gate. Zane decides Armor in Earth is now "always on" — he'll renew it every ten minutes.
- **Ketephys's temple.** The party emerges in an open-air jungle temple — humid, stone arches, branches and roots tangled into the architecture. Recently sacked: vegetation burnt, blood on the stones, a half-finished wooden column with a *white dragon's head* carved on top, snapped off the column and lying broken on the ground. The dragon-head doesn't match the temple's aesthetic — added by someone else, recently.

### Act III — Akrivel and the Cursed Jungle (130:00–215:40)

- **The Ekujae welcome.** Twenty warriors with bows ringed around the temple — dark braided hair, dark skin, warrior face paint. Sense Motive reveals they're *scared*, not aggressive. Birch raises his hand and greets them in Elven. Diplomacy succeeds: the bows lower.
- **Jahsi.** The leader steps forward — gold jewelry on his arms, no drawn weapon, calm. *"I am Jahsi of the Leopold clan. My mother is of the River clan."* (DM coaches: the H is silent.) Jahsi worships **Yuelral**, elven goddess of magic and crystals — her edicts require herbal healing and patience over magic. He politely declines Zane's healing offer for the still-fresh wound on his neck.
- **Where they are.** A high Society/Nature check places the party in the **Mwangi Expanse**, thousands of miles south of Breachill, on another continent entirely. The bow-armed warriors are **Ekujae** — jungle-elf guardians.
- **The temple's recent past.** Cinder Claws raided this temple days ago. They left several Ekujae dead and wounded Jahsi himself with the trinket he wears around his neck. He believes the elders may want to make alliance with the Wildscale Conclave once they understand who killed the cultist back home.
- **The walk to Akrivel.** ~9 miles (15 km, per the DM, prompting a metric-vs-imperial OOC discussion). Around noon they arrive at a city built entirely in the canopy: vine bridges, wood-and-fabric platforms, a pulley elevator at the base. Varkos takes the elevator. Zane takes the elevator. Birch takes the elevator. Jahsi and his warriors climb.
- **Nketiah.** A young front-elf greets the party at the top — fluent common, no accent, one wooden arm, a great deal of jewelry. She is the village's *linguist* (a status role, marked by white face-paint). Welcoming, warm, *too* warm. Both Zane and Varkos read her as having a motive.
- **The tour.** A lioness chilling on a high branch. Children playing a kicking-dance game where the goal is to strike each other's ankle bracelets. Hunters preparing for the night's feast. **No shops.** The Ekujae trade person-to-person; the linguist coordinates between clans. *"They don't need any frivolous things such as money,"* Birch observes (of the Ekujae); Varkos extends his hand for the share of money Birch doesn't want.
- **Gold is cursed.** When Varkos mentions gold/silver/platinum, Nketiah recoils. Only "the purest warriors" wear gold here — everything else is brass. (Jahsi's gold marks his rank. Nketiah says her father has gold too.) She jokes Varkos may die soon for carrying it.
- **The jungle curse.** The Cinder Claws have placed a barrier on the deep jungle. Ekujae who walk into it return after thirty minutes to where they started. Other clans and animals can pass through freely. Only the Ekujae are blocked. The druids cannot identify or dissipate the magic. Jahsi brought the party because they are not Ekujae and might walk through where his people cannot.
- **Birch and the druids.** Birch finds the druid tent — three druids arguing in Wildsong about how much power to draw to break the curse. He introduces himself as a Leaf druid from another land and states his mission: *"I came here to investigate a cult known as the Cinder Claw. Their actions have done much harm to nature and other lands, and I came here to stop them."* They agree to share advice and tools tomorrow.
- **Zane at the medical tent.** With Nketiah translating, Zane spends the afternoon learning Ekujae herbal-paste and bone-setting techniques.
- **Varkos.** Wanders, sweats, swats mosquitoes, looks for something to barter for, declines food (he's wary of Nketiah).
- **Hint dropped.** Nketiah mentions her favorite story — that the Ekujae are "guardians of the gate," and that long ago they locked something called *the Grand Obscurity* inside it. They've been guarding ever since. She'll tell the full story at tonight's feast. The smoke-dragon in the lava tunnel is almost certainly *it*.

**Session ends** right before the feast, with Birch returning from the druid tent.

## Combat Encounters

### The Lava Tunnel — Fire Hazard / Chase

Not a true combat encounter. The party stepped through the Hunter's Gate into a 100-foot lava-glassed tunnel and started toward the second gate. Partway through, the cracks in the wall began blinking with light, and a dragon-shape of smoke and fire — almost certainly *the Grand Obscurity* the Ekujae warned about — formed behind them.

- **DM: "Reflex save."**
- **Varkos**: Rolled 6. Spent a **Hero Point** to reroll. *Success.* Invoked **Quick-Tempered** as a free action (his Dragon Instinct rage-on-trigger feature) and ran for the gate.
- **Birch**: Implicitly succeeded. Through the gate just behind Varkos.
- **Meepos** (mechanical-only, on Varkos's shoulder): assumed success.
- **Zane**: Rolled 17 on Expert Reflex. Declined the Hero Point reroll. Last through; the heat *peaked* as he stepped into the mist.
- **No attack rolls. No rounds. No engagement.** A single saving throw and a chase to the second portal.

After this point: Zane committed to *always-on Armor in Earth* — he'll re-cast every 10 minutes by default. The DM gave him narrative permission to treat it as permanent during exploration to avoid the bookkeeping.
