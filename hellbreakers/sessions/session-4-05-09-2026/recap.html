Session Summary

The Waterspout cliffhanger resolved in one more round. Wing's mast-explosion finished the crow's-nest Marine on his way to the deck; the Chelaxian navy captain dropped Cyrathul with a third-action MAP rapier crit through his Mirror Cloak duplicate and was killed seconds later — Bai Jian crit-Snagging-Struck her open, then Panacea hero-pointed a Needle Darts reroll into the captain's throat. Rhokrag was untied, the ship looted, and the party fled into the dark to lick their wounds. Two days of road travel followed, broken by a vengeance ambush on the second night — Chelaxian survivors who'd tracked them from the inn — that Bai Jian opened by reading the encounter math out loud: "I assume this was balanced for five people because there's no way." The fight ran clean. The post-combat unraveled hard: Panacea's Battle Medicine crit-failed on Moob, killed him on the spot, then Bai Jian sprinted across the camp to pour the goblin's own emergency Elixir of Life down his throat before the Quick Alchemy timer ran out.

Saringallow was a soft landing — Zara crushed Rhokrag in a hug, the goblins set up an alchemy lab, and a boarhound had been delivered to the door for Bai Jian by an unnamed donor. Level-up was bought with Rhokrag's rescue XP: Bai Jian took his Beastmaster companion Ren ("give me my dog"), Cyrathul committed to Draconic Ravager and ditched the longsword, and Panacea took Medic Dedication with Assurance because — her words — "I won't be killing any more moobs, hopefully." A week of downtime followed, then an organization-wide briefing: Alceo and Phoebe were pleased, the Drumish trade lanes were buckling under raid pressure, but Hedvind was about to call in Thrune reinforcements. The new mandate from Eladir: ease off the raids, win hearts and minds in the Chitterwood villages, the Hellbreakers' first reputation point already in the bank from the Night's Prize sailors. The two goblin players left the table; in-fiction, Moob and Fang stayed behind at HQ.

The Wolf Point territory turned out to be a tangle. A Steel Circle bandit camp had taken over a farmstead a mile southeast of the Wolf Point tower; a stout dwarven chieftain greeted the party at twenty leveled crossbows and offered a parley after Cyrathul's Deception failed openly. Her terms: clear whatever was haunting the Wolf Point tower (her boys had spent one night there and come out shaking), and the Steel Circle would help with whatever the party needed. The party deferred the tower for intel and rode for Fernsby, where a villager named Lily showed them a desecrated village green — flowerbeds churned, oak tree chewed, no footprints, only sinuous impressions in the dirt. Cyrathul's Nature check caught it: snake tracks. Lily remembered hissing from a ravine outside town. The party rode out, found the ravine, and walked into a goblin-snake hybrid encounter that the DM rolled four nat 20s on in a row — Bai Jian was crit-bitten unconscious, Ren was crit-bitten down with two dying, Panacea's Battle Medicine pulled Bai Jian back (Treat Wounds got Ren post-combat), Cyrathul mopped the boss with Exploit Vulnerability, and the session ended on the hybrid boss's last words: "Plans ruined!" — and more goblin-ish voices echoing further down the ravine.

Act X · Combat · The Ravine

Plans Ruined

  • Two-hour travel to the ravine. Survival check — Bai Jian's hound nailed the snake-trail cleanly. Stealth on approach failed.
  • 20-foot-high ravine walls. Brackish pool at the back. Skeletons of small wildlife around the pool.
  • First encounter — two goblin-snake hybrids turn at the noise and immediately attack. Combat resolved in one round — Cyrathul max-damage Exploit-Vulnerability strike on the lead hybrid (claw to the face) closed it.
  • Then: "Plans ruined!" A bigger hybrid with two or three heads on its back arguing with each other, flanked by a viper and another helper. New initiative.
  • Boss had a Will-save aura — yammering voices, −1 to perception/Will saves for one round.
  • Combat ran 3-4 rounds. The boss's opening attack on Bai Jian was a NAT 20 (#1) — decent damage but didn't drop him. Panacea's 2-action Heal spell put 12 HP on Bai Jian mid-fight; the boss kept crit-rolling — NAT 20 #2 hit Bai Jian without dropping him ("life of a fighter"), then NAT 20 #3 crit-bit him for 28 damage → DOWN, and on its very next attack the same boss NAT 20 #4 crit-bit Ren → 2 dying. DM, after: "The DM just rolled four nat 20s in a row."
  • Cyrathul mopped: Mirror Cloak flanking + Exploit Vulnerability + a two-strike chain off the Snagging-Strike off-guard finished the boss in Round 3. Demoralize on the viper landed (frightened). Panacea pulled Bai Jian back with Battle Medicine (2d8) and re-cast Trade Life for Death on the boss — the cantrip crit-failed (3 self-damage to Panacea, fast-healing rider applied to Bai Jian anyway). Bai Jian stood up next round and crit-killed the frightened helper for 22 damage. "Your anger has cleaved that one into little snake bits." Panacea closed with a Needle Darts crit (8 + 1 bleed) on the last hybrid.
  • Mid-turn realization from Bai Jian: "Wait, I don't have Intimidating Glare though. Shit." — Demoralize-without-glare wants a shared language; goblin-snake tongue is anyone's guess.
  • Ren revived post-combat via Treat Wounds. He'd been 2 dying (from the crit), not dead. "Alright, my boy. You're back."
  • DM closed the night before sweeping the rest of the ravine. "We'll resolve whatever else is happening here in the ravine next week." Goblin-ish voices still echoing further in.
