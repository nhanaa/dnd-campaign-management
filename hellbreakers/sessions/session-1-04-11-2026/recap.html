Session Summary

The Hellbreakers' newest recruits converge at the Cadence Keg in Breachill, the morning after the rout of the Hellknights of the Rack. Between mugs of free ale and hot sausages, five strangers — a literal medium-sized dragon, a catfolk witch, two goblins raised together in an abandoned weapon bunker, and a one-man hunter from Tian Xia — find an unexpected overlap: four of the five speak Draconic (Bai Jian, Cyrathul, Moob, Fang). Bai Jian floats the idea of using it as party comms shorthand, but Panacea doesn't speak it and the proposal quietly drops. A wounded Eagle Knight named Ethran Howell briefs them on their first mission, and the night dissolves into a tavern storytelling contest that Fang wins by doing wheelies through the bar on his wolf-motorcycle construct.

The next morning is colder. Stuart Hedvind VII's new decree has gone up overnight, offering gold to any citizen willing to turn in a suspected Hellbreaker. Ethran peels the Eagle Knight insignia off his breastplate and drops the planned side trips: straight to Saringallow, incognito. The road carries them through the tense farming village of Draymoor (where Moob talks four drunk Chelaxian sympathizers out of a brawl), past empty autumn fields, and finally to a ferry crossing where two winged shapes appear on the horizon.

They turn out to be undead bats, and they never get a turn. Bai Jian crits on the lead bat. Cyrathul conjures a mirror image behind the second one, reaches behind a wing, draws a sheathed longsword, and attacks through the mirror. Fang overclocks Wing, pops a wheelie, and grinds the second bat into the dirt. The party reaches Saringallow, exchanges the Milani passphrase — "friends of the Rose" — and descends into the Hellbreaker basement. Ethran gifts them his +1 Eagle Knight greatsword, and they're handed a new job: junior Hellbreaker Gertraud Middenhelm went to inventory a warehouse delivery and hasn't come back. The party finds the warehouse locked, circles around, and Bai Jian trips a hairline cord at the side door. Crates fall. Panacea treats his wounds. They round the corner — and find four figures waiting.

Act V · Faction · Saringallow & the Basement

Friends of the Rose

  • Three-quarters of a day later, the party rolls into Saringallow. Ethran leads them to a nondescript mansion on the east side of town — formerly owned by aristocrats who were run out.
  • Passphrase: "Friends of the Rose" — Milani's symbol.
  • A woman opens the door, runs her hand along the fireplace, and a hidden stair descends into a wide basement crammed with maps of Isger and pinned ledgers.
  • Rhokrag the hobgoblin greets them a little too brusquely; Zara the halfling shoos him off and offers reheated pies. She is clearly near tears about everything.
  • Alceo & Phoebe Demetrias are currently in Eladir dealing with the Hedvind decree fallout.
  • Ethran's Gift: He drags down his trunk, opens it, and hands the party his +1 Eagle Knight Greatsword. "I can't swing it with only one arm. It would be better in your hands." He commits to stepping back from the field into strategy and training.
  • Moob and Fang pitch extracting the +1 rune and transferring it to Bai Jian's jian. They also float a hydraulic goblin-tech prosthetic arm for Ethran. He looks surprised and pleased.
  • Bai Jian agrees to leave the jian behind for the transfer — "Just please, take care of my sword. It's very precious to me." Then Moob's crafting check returns a full day, not an hour, and Zara delivers the warehouse mission. Transfer deferred. Bai Jian keeps his jian; the +1 greatsword is leaned against a wine barrel at the new workshop corner to be worked on after the job.
  • Moob and Fang claim a corner between wine barrels as their new alchemy & inventing workshop. Wing parks inside.
Act VI · Mystery · The Everbloom Warehouse

The Missing Junior Hellbreaker

  • New assignment from Zara: Gertraud Middenhelm, a punctual junior Hellbreaker, went to inventory a scheduled delivery at the Everbloom Imports warehouse (a Hellbreaker cover) — and hasn't come back.
  • The warehouse is on Saringallow's dockside, isolated by design. Front door barred from inside. Muffled conversation audible through the walls.
  • Party circles the building. Bai Jian opens a side door — and triggers a hairline tripcord trap. Crates fall. 14 damage, prone, off-guard.
  • Panacea steps up with Battle Medicine (Medic dedication), her first Treat Wounds of the campaign. She fumbles the VTT, rolls a miss, then lands the hero point reroll.
  • Weapons out. The party advances cautiously around the corner.

Character Spotlight

Bai Jian · the Measured Blade

  • First reaction to Cyrathul was genuine wonder, not combat readiness: "A dragon? What such creature is doing here?" For a Tian hunter raised on yaoguai and oni lore, meeting a literal dragon across a tavern table is the session's most on-character Bai Jian beat.
  • Floated Draconic as party comms shorthand: "We can all talk in Draconic. Communicate in front of enemies." Pure tactical instinct, team-first — shelved when Panacea pointed out she doesn't speak it. Four of five (Bai Jian, Cyrathul, Moob, Fang) was not enough to run the channel.
  • Told a solemn oni-hunting story for the tavern contest: "A creature from Tian Xia. A humanoid with skin, colored skin and horns. A very brutal creature." Then dropped the session's most important single hint: "he told it with a bit of a solemn voice when he mentioned he was fighting with another person." First in-character nod at Bai Feng in front of strangers — nobody asked, nobody pulled. The hook is planted.
  • Framed Isger as a second Lingshen in one line to Ethran on the road: "I guess I can sympathize with the cause. My homeland is also ravaged by the greed of the dictatorship." The Hellbreakers war just became personal on-table.
  • Critical opener on the lead dread bat for 24 damage: "I swing it across his wings and it just screeched out."
  • Second combat turn: strode over to help Moob, remembered Snagging Strike this time, Off-Guard rider went through before Fang's crit finisher.
  • Religious-tolerance moment on the road: "If it gives the people the strength to fight again, it is a good thing." Panacea's quiet "I agree. I like that answer" was directly to Bai Jian. First real connection beat between them.
  • Led the party to the Hellbreaker HQ once the passphrase was given. Quiet leadership — the kind the party takes without noticing.
  • Declined the +1 Eagle Knight Greatsword, recognizing what it meant to Ethran: "It seems like it means a lot to him. Maybe someone else can have better use for this." The party's defining generosity beat.
  • Agreed to hand over the jian for the rune transfer — "Just please, take care of my sword. It's very precious to me." Kept the jian when the craft turned out to be a full day and Zara handed them the warehouse mission. Greatsword parked at the workshop corner.
  • Walked point into the warehouse trap. No hesitation. "There's something amiss" and opened the door anyway. 14 damage, prone, off-guard. Paid in full for the party's forward motion.
  • Reminded Panacea she had a hero point when her Treat Wounds came up short — "You have hero point. You have hero point." She spent her own hero point for the reroll.

Cyrathul · the Lunar Observer

  • Opened the session with a deadpan dragon-in-a-tavern bit: "Just for clarification, I am medium-sized."
  • Won the tavern contest crowd with a battle "observed from my lunar observatory" (Esoteric Lore), then bowed with his wings.
  • Delivered the session's mythic backstory beat: "For a while, the majority of my life I was... an observer from the moon. I just got tired of being unable to do things, so I took drastic measures, and now I am here."
  • Combat chain: Exploit Vulnerability → Demoralize → Mirror Cloak → longsword drawn from behind a wing and struck through the mirror image. Moob: "I was imagining a Sith situation."
  • Called the weakness mid-fight via Exploit Vulnerability: "It is weak to slashing damage, by the way." — the mechanical pivot that let Bai Jian and Fang lean into slashing strikes.

Panacea · the Quiet Hex

  • The session's quiet observer. Spoke in nods and affirmations — "I agree. I like that answer."
  • Echoed Cyrathul's Exploit Vulnerability read late in the fight: "I think it's weak to vitality and slashing."
  • Fired Divine Lance + Trade Life for Death hex — failed save but no life to drain, confirming undead. Familiar of Paired Perplexity applied Stupefied 1 regardless.
  • Delivered the campaign's first Battle Medicine (Medic dedication) on Bai Jian after the warehouse trap. Stuck it on the hero point reroll.
  • Affirmed the Draymoor drunks with characteristic brevity: "I agree. They're cat-haters."

Moob · the Action-Economy Machine

  • Action economy MVP of the bat fight: Bottled Lightning via Quick Bomber, Create a Diversion, Ghost Charge via Quick Alchemy (positive + Enfeebled 1), Wallop Stick Parry. Goblin Scuttle moved him behind Bai Jian without provoking.
  • Made Ethran's numbing tonic on the spot and followed up hours later: "How's that numbing tonic, Baron?"
  • Raised Fang from an egg in the abandoned weapon bunker: "it was our place and his garage for Wing. And yeah, war happened."
  • Offered Cyrathul a Tenderizer Grenade seasoned for draconic jaws: "I hold it over your nose as well, like smelling salts."
  • Pitched the hydraulic prosthetic arm for Ethran. Claimed the alchemy corner in the basement between two wine barrels.
  • Caught the eye of Ina, the sun goddess. Inspiration struck him mid-craft and he invented a star grenade — "she saw my craft and gave me inspiration." Won't go near her clergy ("solar radiation") but also won't douse fires with water — "that'll just get her angry."

Fang · the Overclocked Finisher

  • Rolled in on Wing, his wolf-motorcycle construct. Goblin-sized, dragonblooded (distinctly draconic teeth, revealed mid-sausage), raised by Moob.
  • Stole the tavern contest with a wheelie reenactment through the common room — Moob riding backwards on his back — and won two top-shelf whiskeys.
  • Bat-fight finisher: activated Overdrive, crit-hit the second bat, Wing ramming headfirst into its wing, then pop-a-wheelied on its head and "slammed the whole creature down into the ground, leaving a medium-sized creature indent in its entire back."
  • Does not like water. Does not like baths. Smells like "motor oil and dirt."

Things Bai Jian knows that the party does not

  • "The other person I fought beside" is Bai Feng. The party heard a solemn pause and moved on. Do not volunteer. Wait for another character to pull on it. Panacea is the most likely candidate — she's quiet, she'll notice.
  • The Hellbreakers war is a second Lingshen. On-table, Bai Jian called it "the greed of the dictatorship." In private: Lingshen suffered the same pattern, and the infernal creature that took Bai Feng is part of it. Cheliax may not be the whole answer — whatever followed the brothers out of Tian Xia may still be ahead. The tyranny and the hunt are the same job.
  • He is immune to fear effects (Sovereign Dragon ancestry — critical success on fear saves). If the whole party is about to get Frightened, Bai Jian anchors them. The witch's patron and Bai Jian's fear immunity are a natural pairing.
  • Panacea's attention. "I agree. I like that answer" was not throwaway. Remember it every time you think about in-party roleplay from here on.
  • The real motivation is to become the hunter Bai Feng was. Not stated aloud. Not even to himself, most days. But any time Bai Jian is offered a choice between "safer" and "what Bai Feng would have done," play the latter.

What Bai Jian is thinking about

  • Panacea is the first trust candidate. Quiet, catfolk, outsider like him. She agreed with him on religion out of nowhere. If Bai Jian ever tells one party member about Bai Feng, it should be her first. Not yet — play two more sessions of near-silent company before opening that door.
  • The rune transfer is still pending. The greatsword is parked at HQ. Full-day craft. After the warehouse job, prioritize going back to finish the extraction before the next mission leaves town.
  • Snagging Strike is not yet habitual. Forgot it in combat 1, remembered in combat 2. New rule: every sword-hand-free Strike defaults to Snagging Strike unless there's a specific reason not to. Off-Guard rider is free value and stacks with Fang/Cyrathul/Moob follow-ups.
  • Dread Marshal Stance comes online at level 4. That is when the party's damage ceiling jumps — the +1 status damage aura buffs Cyrathul and Moob directly. Plan the level 4 session around getting it right.
  • The declined greatsword is a dividend to collect later. NPCs notice generosity more than strength. Ethran will remember. Next time the party needs political cover, a letter of introduction, or an old Eagle Knight contact pulled — it'll be easier because of this session.
  • Bai Jian is the party's political conscience by default. Cyrathul and Panacea don't care; Moob and Fang are goblins. Bai Jian is the only one paying active attention to the Hedvind decree as a political problem. Lean into it when talking to Alceo or Phoebe eventually.
  • Warehouse cliffhanger plan: Sudden Charge into whoever looks most dangerous. Snagging Strike if sword-hand is free. Don't forget the hero point refill at the start of next session.
