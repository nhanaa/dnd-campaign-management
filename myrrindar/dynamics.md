### Synergy Notes

- **Post-Silas gaps**: Combat-wise the party is fine — Avon was the real frontline and tank all along, Talya adds skirmisher melee. The actual gap is skill coverage: no Rogue means no Reliable Talent on stealth/sleight of hand, and lock/trap detection falls to Mist or Talya. Mist could pivot Bardic Expertise toward Stealth + Sleight of Hand if Pax wants to lean into the scout/infiltrator niche now that the lane is open.
- **Avon as anchor**: With heavy armor + shield + STR build, Avon holds the chokepoint and soaks hits while Talya flanks. Mist's Healing Word keeps him up; Bardic Inspiration on his attack rolls stretches his offensive contribution.
- **Dissonant Whispers + Melee Allies**: Forced movement from Dissonant Whispers provokes opportunity attacks from Avon and Talya — position them adjacent first.
- **Bane + Cleric/Druid Save Spells**: Bane reduces enemy saves by 1d4, boosting the hit rate of Avon and Akasha's save-or-suck spells.
- **Two Healers**: Avon (Cleric) is primary healer with Channel Divinity + heavy slots; Mist's Healing Word is emergency/Bonus Action backup — don't overlap.
- **Eloquence Unsettling Words**: At Lv3, spending a Bardic Inspiration use to subtract from an enemy's save pairs perfectly with the Cleric and Druid throwing save-based spells.
