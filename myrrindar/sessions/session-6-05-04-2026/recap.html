Session Summary

After a long rest in Evrin's pre-paid suite, the party realized something embarrassing: in five sessions of adventuring together, they had never actually told each other their backstories. Breakfast at the Crowned Pheasant became a slow, careful introduction round — Avon's Bahamut vision out of the village of Byron, Akasha's Whispering Canopy and the blight on the mother tree, Mist's fairy settlement and the kidnapping he survived, Talya's shipwreck and the monastery that took her in. Mist, guarded as ever, kept it short: “I grew up in a fairy settlement, and then I got kidnapped, and then...” Avon and Akasha gently offered to help track his captors. The session-wide tonal shift started here.

The day's actual goal was research. Akasha's quest — find “Deep Bloom” in Skybreeze — became the through-line. A street encounter with Carrie-Anne (Avon's bashful Bahamut-temple admirer) revealed the name in full: Thalren Deepbloom, an herbalist in the Craftsman's District. The party first detoured to the Wizard's Tower library, a self-organizing archive run by a magical owl pedestal. Akasha's Deep Bloom query produced The Ins and Outs of Herbs by Thalren Deepbloom. Avon's Wardstone-history query produced a sanitized children's book — and a second owl that turned around mid-flight with a darker, embossed volume the library refused to deliver. A halfling librarian named Tibbles Briarthorne materialized to test them with a “go ahead, take any book” trap; Mist baited him into a Nat-20-on-Insight monologue with Guidance that confirmed the Wardstone story was performative theatre.

The Craftsman's District turned dramatic fast. Thalren Deepbloom answered the door playing a deliberate cover-act — and dropped it the second they were inside. Akasha's blight, on the world tree in Iliora, alarmed him visibly: “If the World Tree is fully infected, it will spread across the land through the roots... it'll reach far and wide underground.” He pressed a small, peach-pit-sized green glowing seed of renewal into Akasha's hand, told her its purpose would reveal itself in time, and bolted east toward Iliora — looking over his shoulder as he went.

Mist flew up and immediately spotted a green-robed Vale Tempest tail sneaking after Thalren. The party gave chase across rooftops and alleyways — Talya backflipping off roofs into hay bales, Akasha tripping over her own feet, Mist as bird's-eye spotter — and Mist landed his Suggestion gambit on the tail with an Unsettling-Words rider, freezing the agent mid-pursuit. After a small back-and-forth (Talya stumbling 40 feet ahead, Akasha climbing a roof to eavesdrop and getting kicked off), Avon ended up alone in the alley with the agent and the agent's amulet — agreeing to wear it in exchange for the truth. He bypassed the 100-gp pearl on Identify via Channel Divinity (Preserve Knowledge), confirmed the amulet was a save-based Charm Person, cast Bless on himself, Mist, and Talya before the others stepped back, and put the amulet on once alone.

What the agent told him was the load-bearing reveal of the campaign: the Wardstones do not keep a storm at bay. “They keep it beneath. They keep it buried.” Massive, not from this world, even the agent's own brain “couldn't process what it is.” The Vale Tempest are keepers of the truth — they suppress it because public panic would empty Skybreeze. The agent also revealed a magic-vs-violence schism inside the Vale Tempest (he's of the magic camp), confirmed his organization did not attack the Wardstone at the festival (so Jensen is someone else), and gave Avon their hierarchy: Initiate (green) → Veilbearer (blue) → Channeler (white). The fancy-robe woman from Session 3 was a Channeler named Lady Avesna.

The amulet's Charm tried to take Avon — he saved on a 14 CHA — and the agent panicked, threw a Nat-20 punch at Avon's face for 7 damage, and tried to flee. Mist's Suggestion held him on a failed save. After a beat of clarity (“They will kill me — they always know”), Avon hard-cast Identify and pinpointed a tracking crystal sewn into the agent's robe. Off came the robe. Off came everything except boxers and shoes. The agent introduced himself: “I know you didn't ask, but my name's Rowan.” Mist offered him asylum-by-utility — disguise him, smuggle him out — and he took the deal. The party bought him a hooded set of civilian clothes for 1 gp and headed to the Temple of Bahamut to hide him. Akasha's closing line: “Yay! Why is it my pet? It's our pet.”

▲ The Truth, Beneath the Wardstones
“What is not true is that the Wardstones keep a storm at bay. They keep it beneath. They keep it buried. They say it's massive. They say it's not even from this world.”
— Rowan (Vale Tempest Initiate), to Avon, alone in the alley, under the amulet's compulsion

Act II: Backstory Swap Over Breakfast
Roleplay
  • Avon proposed introductions: “Should we like introduce each other? Because we haven't done it.” Akasha agreed: breakfast around the table.

A wandering sage introduced him to the wider world of Myrrindar. Halfway to Skybreeze, in Sevra, he intervened in a fight on the road, lost badly, and the local clerics took him in — that's when he became a Cleric of Bahamut. A vision from Bahamut a month ago told him to come to Skybreeze: “there seems to be some sort of danger.”

Her quiet life was broken when “a mysterious creeping blight began to wither the oldest oaks from the inside out.” Tasked by the elders to find the source. Carries a staff carved from a fallen branch of the mother tree.
  • Mist — “I'm Mist. I grew up in a fairy settlement, and then I got kidnapped, and then...” Played guarded and visibly tender. Now in Skybreeze trying to make money — performed a couple of notes on his lyre to lighten the mood, but stayed reserved. Avon and Akasha offered to help track the kidnappers; Mist quietly: “I'd rather not want them to kidnap me again.” — “You've got us now.” (Avon)
  • Talya — “I got swept off my ship, washed up near a monastery, and decided it seemed like a pretty good time to go explore the land for a change.” Plus a beat about “swearing like a sailor the whole time.” Old crew unaccounted for: “I don't know where they wound up. I got washed off. Don't know if they made it, don't know if it sank.”
