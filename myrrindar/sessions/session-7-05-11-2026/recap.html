Session Summary

The session opened with Avon recapping last week for the table's new player, Tobias — playing Jaethal Dionaea, a fairy from the Feywild bound to the Archfey patron Yarnspinner. Mist spotted her in a Skybreeze street, gawking at Avon and looking like nothing the party had ever seen: ladybug-shell wings, cuttlefish eyes with W-shaped pupils, coral pink overalls. Two fairies in one city — Mist's first words to her: “Hey, what are you doing here? I haven't seen one of us in a while.” The party folded her into the trip to the Temple of Bahamut, where they were already heading to hide Rowan.

The temple turned into the load-bearing scene of the session. After Carrie-Anne ushered them in, High Warden Sethrix — a silver dragonborn, militant stance — pulled the party into his private office and dropped two reveals on Avon. First: the skirmish in Sevra wasn't random. The cloaked figure Avon intervened against was an operative of the Karadim, a feared shadowy organization permeating Mirandar. The "noblewoman" was Lady Saraveth. Avon died in that fight. He was resurrected by his mentor, Borenthal, at the local temple — “that was the day Bahamut knew you would be his champion.” Second, Sethrix independently confirmed the Wardstone truth: “The Wardstones do not do what they say they do.” — “Something came here. It brought the storm. It threatened the entirety of Skybreeze.” He agreed to shelter Rowan, and warned the party away from speaking the Vale Tempest's name around Tibbles Briarthorne.

Sethrix also handed Akasha her next step: the teleportation circle in the wizard's tower can put her on the outskirts of Iliora in a single step. Jaethal opened up the Feywild for the High Warden — Yarnspinner, the spider-shaped Archfey who collects stories and “feels like a forest clearing.” Before leaving Rowan, she pressed an orange Witchlight cupcake into his hand — a carnival-prize trinket that regenerates every dawn. “You seem really upset.” Rowan ate it in two bites and visibly settled.

At the wizard's tower, the party hit their first roadblock: Thalren Deepbloom had already warned the mages that travel to Iliora was halted. Mist talked the receiving wizard into letting them through (Deception with advantage via Akasha's help — “you must be that elf”). One at a time, they stepped onto the green teleportation circle. They emerged on the outskirts of a dead forest: no birds, no insects, black puddles, decayed trees, mutated deer head-butting trees until they bled.

Akasha got her bearings — Nature with advantage, with Guidance, dirty Nat 20 — and the corruption tugged her directly east, not toward the mother tree. The party followed, Mist casting Druidcraft to swap the death-stench for flowers. They reached a clearing with a stinking pond and a wooden cabin. A blonde woman offered them cookies, chocolate-mint, in a voice that didn't quite sit right. Jaethal's Insight 16 (with Avon's Guidance): the heebie-jeebies, badly. Avon ranged his channeling Detect Magic — and the illusion shattered. A bog hag in her real shape, and a Shambling Mound erupting from the pond.

The fight that followed went 7-8 rounds of careful tactics and pure attrition. Highlights: Jaethal's first combat action was a sparkly-floral Eldritch Blast and a taunting Misty Step — Archfey patron feature giving the mound disadvantage on any target that wasn't her. Mist's Cutting Words + Shatter caught both enemies in one centered blast (15 to the hag + 7 to the mound). Jaethal cast Phantasmal Force on the hag — a sealed glass orb of honey around her head, 10 psychic on the fail; for a round and a half the hag was blind, gulping honey. Talya went down once, came back, Avon went down twice. Mist landed Dissonant Whispers mid-illusion to chase the hag off — “she just drowned in honey and just ran away.” Then Tasha's Hideous Laughter from Jaethal dropped the hag prone, and Talya — bruised, raised, half-dead from the same hag's eye-claw triple combo — finished her: max-damage acid critical, Walking-Dead boot-stomp on the laughing prone hag. “Until she stops.”

The Shambling Mound proved tougher than the hag in the end. Avon went down again to its tendril-grab — dragged into the mound's body, grappled and restrained while Mist screamed "you are the champion of Bahamut" between death rolls. Akasha's Starry Form (Chalice) transformation was the clutch beat — Cure Wounds plus a bonus heal letting her pull both Jaethal and Avon back from 0 on a single turn. Jaethal and Mist climbed 15 ft into the air to stay out of reach; Akasha eventually got dragged into the mound's body too. Mist's crossbow bolt from altitude found the core of the mound — “a lifeless pile as this thing is down.”

Akasha stabilized at 3 successes, Talya bloodied but standing, Avon stable. The party staggered into the hag's broken shack, set up bedrolls, and took a long rest. Everyone leveled up to 4. Jaethal, slumping back: “I did not expect to fight a hag on my first ever session with you guys.”

Round 1 — Opening Salvo

  • Akasha: club + sickle 1-2 on mound. Club hit. Sickle nat 1 → slipped into the 6-ft-deep pond. “You know, we're on session eight now. I'm going to start being a little mean.”
  • Jaethal (first combat action of the campaign): Eldritch Blast on mound, 15 to hit, 8 force damage. Charged into melee, bonus-action Taunting Misty Step back to safety. Mound DC 14 Wis save → rolled 7 → fail. Mound now has disadvantage on attacks against non-Jaethal targets.
  • Avon: 2nd-level Mind Spike on mound (Knowledge domain expanded spell list), DC Wis 13. Mound rolled 15, save, half damage → 8 psychic. Moved to safer position.
  • Hag: Cast Magic Missile (5 darts auto-hit) via decayed raven corpse focus — Akasha 3, Mist 2, Avon 5, Talya 5, Jaethal 5.
  • Talya: Turned on Elemental Attunement. Punch 1 (Fire), 22 to hit, 6 fire damage. Punch 2 (Acid), 23 to hit, 6 acid damage. “I was Talya doing Talya things.”
  • Mist: Taunt + Cutting Words on hag's upcoming save. 2nd-level Shatter centered between hag and mound. Hag failed Con save with Cutting Words penalty → 15 thunder. Mound saved → 7 thunder. Flew further back.
  • Mound (disadvantage on non-Jaethal): Tendril at Talya — hit, 7 bludgeon + 5 lightning = 12 damage, pulled 5 ft. Tendril at Avon — miss (Dodge advantage). Tendril at Akasha — miss.

Round 2 — Phantasmal Honey

  • Akasha: Moved out of melee (mound OA at disadvantage → miss). Ranged attack at mound — 11 to hit, miss.
  • Jaethal: Phantasmal Force on the hag — sealed glass orb of honey around her head. DC 14 Int save, hag (+0) failed. 10 psychic damage. Hag effectively blinded (gulping honey). Bonus action Refreshing Misty Step to Talya — gave her 9 temp HP.
  • Avon: Bonus action Spiritual Weapon (giant spear) next to mound — miss. Action: Dodge.
  • Hag (blind, gulping honey): Three claws at Talya. Claw 1 (15 to hit): 10 damage — Talya Deflect Attacks reduced to 0. Claw 2 (21 to hit): 9 damage — consumed Talya's 9 temp HP exactly. Claw 3 (19 to hit): 7 damage → Talya 0 HP, down.
  • Talya death save: 18, success (1).

Round 3 — "She Just Drowned in Honey and Ran Away"

  • Mist: Bonus action Healing Word on Talya for 9 HP. Action: 2nd-level Dissonant Whispers on hag (DC Wis 13). Hag (blind) rolled at disadvantage: 19/12 → took 12 → fail. Hag takes 13 psychic damage and is forced to flee at max speed → Talya OA for 9 damage. Total 22 from Mist's turn. Mist: “She just drowned in honey and just ran away.” Talya: “All I can hear is the murloc sounds from World of Warcraft.”
  • Mound: Three tendrils all miss (Avon Dodge active, Akasha untargeted).
  • Akasha: Moved adjacent to Talya. Upcast Cure Wounds at 2nd level for 4d8+3 — rolled high. “Talya basically got full health.” DM: “Wow, really? It doubles every level.”

Round 4 — Honey Wears Off

  • Jaethal: 50 ft from hag, moved closer, fired another Eldritch Blast — 24 to hit, minimum damage 2.
  • Avon: Bonus-action Spiritual Weapon swing on mound — 15 to hit, 6 force damage. Dodge again.
  • Hag: Spent action on Int save vs Phantasmal Force — rolled 19 → save, illusion ends. Moved 30 ft back to Talya. “A lot of her anger is due to the fact that she's embarrassed.”
  • Talya: Acid attunement. Punch 1 — overshot, miss (“You were overcome with excitement and you overshot.”). Punch 2 — hit, 4 acid damage (sad).
  • Mist: Last Bardic Inspiration as Cutting Words + 2nd-level Dissonant Whispers. Hag rolled 16, Cutting Words penalty → 13 → just met the DC, save. Half damage 6 psychic. Moved to safety.
  • Mound: Missed Avon and Talya twice. Slumped back into the pond. “It even kind of starts to slump back into the pond.”

Round 5 — The Hag Tries to Get Even

  • Akasha: Guiding Bolt at hag — 16 radiant damage. “She glows in this bluish light.” (Advantage rider for next attacker.)
  • Jaethal: Taunting Misty Step + Eldritch Blast with advantage. Hag saved the Wis 22 so Jaethal loses the taunt rider, but the blast hit.
  • Avon: Spiritual Weapon swing on mound — miss. Dodge.
  • Hag: Three claws on Talya, then Akasha. Claw 1 (nat 20 hit, 14 damage through Talya's Deflect 4). Claw 2 (26 to hit, 17 damage → Talya 0 HP, down again). Claw 3 on Akasha (22 to hit, 13 damage) — Akasha stays up at low HP.
  • Talya death save: 18, success (2). “Aha, not dead yet. Again.”
  • Mist: Vicious Mockery on hag — hag saved at 18, no damage. Moved further to safety.
  • Mound: Disadvantage, missed all attacks.

Round 6 — Tasha's Hideous Laughter + The Stomp

  • Jaethal: Refreshing Misty Step to Akasha (8 temp HP). Action: 2nd-level Tasha's Hideous Laughter — hag failed Wis 10 → prone, incapacitated, laughing. Mound saved 18. Jaethal: “I don't think he's technically capable of laughter.”
  • Avon: Spiritual Weapon swing — miss. Dodge.
  • Hag: Prone, incapacitated, laughing. Failed save at end of turn — still hollering.
  • Talya: Last focus point. Acid. Punch with advantage from prone — hit for 9 damage. Punch 2 — NAT 20 CRIT. House-rule stunt: max damage on hit + d6 stunt points (rolled 5). Cashed all 5 into Lethal Blow (extra damage dice). Total: 20 damage on a single punch. Killing blow.
  • DM: “Talya, this witch hag has scratched your eyes out several times. You've seen the brink of death several times in the last minute or two. Describe how you kill this hag.”
  • Talya: “I'm just gonna stomp her head in. Like something out of The Walking Dead.”
  • DM: “As she's rolling on the ground, laughing, just an ugly laugh, your boot just squishes and squishes until she stops.”
  • Mist: Faerie Fire (purple) on mound — DEX 13, mound failed → advantage on all attacks against it for the next minute.
  • Mound (lit purple, advantage to attackers): Tendril at Avon — hit for 14 damage total (bludgeon + lightning). Avon 0 HP, down. Mound grappled his unconscious body and pulled him into itself. Crit on Jaethal — Mist Silvery Barbs reaction → reroll, takes lower (19, not a crit). 11 damage. Second tendril (22 to hit) for 9 damage → Jaethal 0 HP, down anyway (Silvery Barbs saved her from the crit, not the down).

Round 7 — Akasha's Star Form Clutch

  • Akasha (low HP, beat up, but conscious): “If I assume my Star Form as a bonus action, I can expend a use of Wild Shape to take on a Star Form rather than shape-shifting.”
  • DM: “Akasha showing up clutch.”
  • Bonus action: Starry Form (Chalice). “My joints become slimmer like stars, and glowing lines connect them as a star chart.” Glowing 10-ft radius. +3 temp HP.
  • Action: 1st-level Cure Wounds on Jaethal for 10 HP — Jaethal gasps back to life. Chalice rider: bonus heal to another creature within 30 ft — Avon for 5 HP, pulled him back to 0+.
  • Jaethal: No Misty Steps left. Disengage, scampered away.
  • Avon: Conscious. Bonus-action Healing Word on Akasha for small heal. Dodge.

Round 8+ — Final Attrition & Mist's Killing Bolt

  • The mound's tendril reached Avon a second time, dragged him back into its body — Avon 0 HP again.
  • Akasha got pulled into the mound's body too (15 damage tendril) — 0 HP, grappled and restrained.
  • Talya took a hit; Deflect + 8 temp HP from Jaethal kept her standing.
  • Jaethal flew 15 ft into the air out of melee range — “at advantage against him.” Eldritch Blast for 7 force.
  • Mist climbed 15 ft up too. Crossbow at advantage from altitude — missed the first shot even with the height advantage.
  • Akasha death saves: three successes — stabilized at 3-and-0.
  • Avon death rolls bracketed by Mist's frantic “you are the champion of Bahamut” cheering. Hit 1 fail, multiple successes → stabilized.
  • Jaethal: “Yarnspinner, I know you don't want a sad story. Let your girl do something here.” Eldritch Blast for 7 — mound at 3 HP.
  • Mist's killing bolt — “Don't guff it, bro.” Dirty 20. “Bolt just sears into the core of this thing, hits whatever is powering it, and it just folds to the ground.”

Post-combat: Long rest granted in the hag's broken-down shack. Everyone leveled to 4.
