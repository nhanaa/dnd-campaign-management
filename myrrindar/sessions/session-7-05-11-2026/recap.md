## Session Summary

The session opened with **Avon recapping last week** for the table's new player, **Tobias** — playing **Jaethal Dionaea**, a fairy from the Feywild bound to the Archfey patron **Yarnspinner**. Mist spotted her in a Skybreeze street, gawking at Avon and looking like nothing the party had ever seen: ladybug-shell wings, cuttlefish eyes with W-shaped pupils, coral pink overalls. Two fairies in one city — Mist's first words to her: *"Hey, what are you doing here? I haven't seen one of us in a while."* The party folded her into the trip to the **Temple of Bahamut**, where they were already heading to hide Rowan.

The temple turned into the load-bearing scene of the session. After Carrie-Anne ushered them in, **High Warden Sethrix** — a silver dragonborn, hands clasped, militant stance — pulled the party into his private office and dropped two reveals on Avon. First: *the skirmish in Sevra wasn't random*. The cloaked figure Avon intervened against was an operative of the **Karadim**, a feared shadowy organization. The "noblewoman" was **Lady Saraveth**. *Avon died in that fight.* He was resurrected by his mentor, **Borenthal**, at the local temple — *"that was the day Bahamut knew you would be his champion."* Second, and quieter: Sethrix knows the **Wardstone truth** and independently confirms what Rowan told the party in S6. *"The Wardstones do not do what they say they do. There was a storm and it was magical, but that wasn't the real issue. Something came here. It brought the storm. It threatened the entirety of Skybreeze — indeed, all of Mirandar, if it hadn't been stopped."* He won't say more in public: *"I don't speak of such things. That's why I'm still alive."* He agreed to shelter Rowan, and warned: don't speak the Vale Tempest's name around **Tibbles Briarthorne**.

Sethrix also handed Akasha her next step: **the teleportation circle in the wizard's tower** can put her on the outskirts of Iliora in a single step. Jaethal, before they left, opened up the Feywild for the High Warden — Yarnspinner, the spider-shaped Archfey who collects stories and *"feels like a forest clearing."* Before leaving Rowan, she pressed an **orange Witchlight cupcake** into his hand — a carnival-prize trinket that regenerates every dawn. *"You seem really upset."* Rowan ate it in two bites and visibly settled.

At the wizard's tower, the party hit their first roadblock: **Thalren Deepbloom had already warned the mages** that travel to Iliora was halted. Mist talked the receiving wizard into letting them through (Deception with advantage via Akasha's help — *"you must be that elf"* — magical liability waivers signed). One at a time, they stepped onto the green teleportation circle. They emerged on the outskirts of a dead forest: no birds, no insects, black puddles, decayed trees, the occasional mutated deer head-butting a tree until it bled.

Akasha got her bearings — and **Nature with advantage, with Guidance, dirty Nat 20** — and the corruption tugged her *directly east*, not toward the mother tree. The party followed, Mist casting **Druidcraft** to swap the death-stench for flowers as they walked. They reached a clearing with a stinking pond and a wooden cabin. A blonde young woman in a craftsman's apron stepped out and offered them cookies, chocolate-mint, in a voice that didn't quite sit right. **Jaethal's Insight 16** (with Avon's Guidance): *the heebie-jeebies, badly.* Avon walked just close enough for his channeling Detect Magic to range her — and the illusion **shattered**. A bog hag in her real shape, and a **Shambling Mound** erupting from the pond.

The fight that followed went five rounds of careful tactics and three of pure attrition. Highlights: **Jaethal's first combat action was a sparkly-floral Eldritch Blast and a taunting Misty Step** — Archfey patron feature giving the mound disadvantage on any target that wasn't her. **Mist's Cutting Words + Shatter** caught both enemies in one centered blast (15 to the hag, 8 to the mound). **Jaethal cast Phantasmal Force** on the hag — a sealed glass orb of honey around her head, 10 psychic on the fail; for a round and a half the hag was blind, *gulping honey*, the table audibly losing it. Talya went down once, came back, Avon went down twice. Mist landed **Dissonant Whispers** mid-illusion to chase the hag off — *"she just drowned in honey and just ran away."* Then **Tasha's Hideous Laughter** from Jaethal dropped the hag prone, and Talya — bruised, raised, half-dead from the same hag's eye-claw triple combo — finished her: max-damage acid critical, Walking-Dead boot-stomp on the laughing prone hag. *"Until she stops."*

The Shambling Mound proved tougher than the hag in the end. Avon went down again to its tendril-grab — **dragged into the mound's body, grappled and restrained and bleeding** while Mist screamed "you are the champion of Bahamut" between death rolls. **Akasha's Starry Form (Chalice) transformation** was the clutch beat — Cure Wounds *plus* a bonus heal letting her pull both Jaethal and Avon back from 0 on a single turn. Jaethal and Mist climbed 15 ft into the air to stay out of reach, and the party plinked it down from above while Avon and Talya tanked the death saves on the ground. Akasha eventually got dragged into the mound's body too. **Mist's crossbow bolt** found the core of the mound — *"a lifeless pile as this thing is down."*

Akasha stabilized at 3 successes, Talya bloodied but standing, Avon stable. The party staggered into the hag's broken shack, set up bedrolls, and took a long rest. Everyone leveled up to **4**. Jaethal, slumping back: *"I did not expect to fight a hag on my first ever session with you guys."*
