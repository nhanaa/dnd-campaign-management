# PF2e Remastered — Best Damage Build Path Tier List

> **System:** Pathfinder 2e Remastered (Player Core 1 & 2)
> **Variant Rule:** Free Archetype
> **Methodology:** Consensus of three expert perspectives — The Optimizer (raw DPR and burst ceilings), The Storyteller (sustained performance across a full adventuring day), and The Tactician (damage contribution including party-enabling effects). Rankings measure personal damage output — how much damage the class itself deals. Party buff contributions (e.g., Bard's Courageous Anthem) are noted but not the primary metric.

---

## S Tier — The Damage Kings

### Fighter
The sustained DPR champion. **Legendary weapon proficiency** gives +2 to hit over every other martial at key breakpoints. In PF2e's bounded accuracy system, this translates to roughly 10% more hits AND 10% more crits. Every buff the party provides (Courageous Anthem, flanking, Demoralize) compounds on the Fighter's superior base accuracy. With a Courageous Anthem active, Fighters crit on a natural 17+. **Combat Flexibility** allows daily feat swapping for situational optimization. Attack of Opportunity punishes enemies who try to avoid them. The most consistent, reliable damage dealer in the game.

### Magus
The burst damage king. **Spellstrike** combining a top-rank spell (e.g., 7th-rank Disintegrate: 12d10) with a weapon Strike on a critical hit with a Fatal weapon can exceed **150+ damage in a single action** — no other class in the game comes close to this ceiling. **True Strike** + Spellstrike eliminates the biggest risk (missing the Strike). **Starlit Span** does this at range with zero risk. The Spellstrike → Recharge cycle creates a 2-round pattern that lowers sustained DPR compared to Fighter, but even averaged over the cycle, the spell damage front-loading keeps Magus competitive. A damage tier list should reward the highest damage ceiling, and that belongs to Magus. *Dissenting opinion: The Storyteller argued A-tier, noting the recharge cycle drops sustained DPR below Fighter.*

## A Tier — Excellent Damage Output

### Kineticist
The highest **total adventuring day damage** of any class. Unlimited Elemental Blasts and impulses with zero resource cost means the Kineticist hits just as hard in fight #6 as fight #1. Strong AoE options for multi-target encounters. While per-round damage doesn't match Fighter or Magus burst, the complete absence of resource depletion means Kineticist's cumulative damage over a full day often exceeds everyone else's.

### Barbarian
**Rage** provides a flat damage bonus on every hit that's massive at all levels — combined with a d12 weapon on Giant Instinct, the raw numbers are enormous. 12 HP per level means Barbarians can stay in melee absorbing hits while dealing them back. Dragon Instinct adds elemental damage and eventually a breath weapon. Dominates early-to-mid levels for raw damage output. Falls off slightly at very high levels as the flat Rage bonus becomes a smaller percentage of total damage.

### Ranger
**Precision Edge** adds flat bonus damage to the first hit each round, scaling with level. **Hunted Shot** (ranged) and **Twin Takedown** (melee) provide extra attacks for excellent action economy. **Gravity Weapon** adds bonus dice. Animal companion builds effectively add another attacker to the party, pushing total damage output higher. **Impossible Volley** at high levels is devastating AoE. Consistent and reliable across all levels.

### Thaumaturge
**Exploit Vulnerability** (Personal Antithesis) adds weakness damage equal to 2 + half level to EVERY Strike against the target — even if the creature has no natural weakness. This flat bonus scales well and applies to every hit, not just the first. Combined with decent martial proficiency and implement-enhanced Strikes, the Thaumaturge's sustained damage is consistently underrated. The bonus requires a Recall Knowledge action to set up, but once active, it applies all combat.

### Rogue
**Sneak Attack** dice are massive when Off-Guard is reliable — and with flanking, party debuffs (Frightened, Clumsy), or class features like Gang Up, Off-Guard is very reliable. At high levels, Sneak Attack adds 4d6+ to every qualifying Strike. **Debilitations** add tactical debuffs on top. The Rogue's damage ceiling with a cooperative party rivals the Fighter's, though it's more party-dependent.

## B Tier — Decent Personal Damage

### Monk
**Flurry of Blows** (2 Strikes for 1 action) is outstanding damage action economy — effectively getting a free extra attack each turn. Ki Strike and other ki spells add bonus damage. But lower weapon dice (d6-d8 for most stances) and no big damage riders (no Rage, no Sneak Attack, no Spellstrike) limit the absolute damage ceiling compared to A-tier strikers.

### Sorcerer
More spell slots than Wizard means more blasting per day. Primal and Arcane bloodlines access the best damage spells (Fireball, Lightning Bolt, Chain Lightning, Meteor Swarm). **Dangerous Sorcery** adds flat damage to offensive spells. Signature Spells allow flexible heightening for maximum AoE damage. Solid blaster, but casters in PF2e Remastered are balanced to support rather than dominate damage.

### Gunslinger
**Fatal** trait on firearms produces massive critical hit damage — Fatal d12 on a crit adds enormous bonus dice. **Way of the Sniper** with Called Shot and Headshot delivers excellent alpha strikes. Running Reload helps action economy. But the reload tax (many firearms require an action to reload after each shot) devastates sustained DPR, and without Fighter's +2 accuracy, crits are less frequent.

### Swashbuckler
**Finisher** damage with Panache active delivers strong burst — Confident Finisher, Bleeding Finisher, and Perfect Finisher all add significant dice. The Panache system encourages aggressive action (Tumble Through, Demoralize, Feint) before unleashing a big hit. But Panache upkeep is inconsistent — you lose it on Finisher and must earn it back. When the rhythm works, damage spikes hard. When it doesn't, it drops significantly. High ceiling, variable floor.

### Commander
Not a personal damage dealer — the Commander's damage contribution comes through enabling ally attacks. **Strike Hard!** gives an ally an immediate Strike. **Pop, Drop, and Lock** gives up to 3 ally Strikes as reactions. **Executioner's Volley** gives all squadmates a ranged Strike. These attacks happen at the ALLY's attack bonus, so pairing Commander with a Fighter effectively gives the Fighter 4+ actions per round. Ranked B because the damage is real but indirect.

## C Tier — Damage is Secondary

### Bard
Personal damage output is low — occult spell list isn't great for blasting, and weapon proficiency tops out at Expert. **BUT**: Courageous Anthem adds +1 damage to every ally's attack roll for the entire combat. In a 4-person party where allies make 2+ attacks per round, the Bard's effective damage contribution through buffs may actually exceed any individual's personal output. Ranked C for personal damage — the Bard's true damage contribution is creating the conditions for everyone else to deal more.

### Cleric
**Warpriest** is an adequate melee combatant. **Harm Font** with 3-action Harm is surprisingly good burst (30-foot emanation). Spiritual Weapon provides sustained damage as a spell. But damage is not the Cleric's role — they heal, buff, and remove conditions. Ranked here because they CAN deal damage, just not efficiently.

### Wizard
The arcane list has the best AoE damage spells in the game (Fireball, Chain Lightning, Meteor Swarm). But fewer spell slots than Sorcerer, fragile positioning that limits aggressive play, and the general PF2e design philosophy that casters support rather than dominate damage output. A Sorcerer does the blaster role better with more slots.

### Champion
Decent melee damage with Strikes and **Smite** features. Retributive Strike deals damage to attackers as a reaction. Not a damage-focused class, but the combination of regular Strikes plus reactive damage adds up. Champions are always in melee swinging, but damage output is secondary to protection.

### Druid
Primal list access to Lightning Bolt, Fireball, and Tempest Surge provides solid blasting. **Wild Shape** builds can function as competent melee combatants with form-specific damage. Animal companion provides additional attacks. Not a dedicated damage class — versatile casters rarely match martial DPR in PF2e's design philosophy.

### Guardian
Tank chassis built for protection, not offense. Strikes are functional but unremarkable. Taunt and Intercept don't deal damage. Ranked here because Guardians are in melee and do swing weapons, but damage output is an afterthought.

## D Tier — Damage is Not the Point

### Oracle
**Flames Mystery** has damage focus with offensive revelation spells, but the curse tax limits sustained output — pushing further into curse for more power comes with increasingly harsh penalties. Other mysteries (Life, Cosmos) are explicitly support/utility focused. Damage is not Oracle's strength.

### Investigator
**Strategic Strike** adds damage to Strikes after a successful Devise a Stratagem, but Devise a Stratagem costs an action every turn. Net damage output is below most martials because you're spending 1 of 3 actions on setup rather than attacking. The math rarely works out in Investigator's favor for pure DPR.

### Witch
Hex cantrips are debuffs, not damage dealers. Spell list depends on patron, but the Witch's terrible proficiencies and weak chassis don't support aggressive spellcasting. The class has no damage-boosting features.

### Psychic
**Amped cantrips** do decent single-target damage (Amped Imaginary Weapon hits hard). But the squishy chassis (6 HP/level) limits aggressive positioning, and Amps are a limited resource. Unleash Psyche provides a burst window but leaves you stunned afterward.

### Summoner
Eidolon does acceptable damage, but shared MAP (Multiple Attack Penalty applies across both summoner and eidolon) and the 3-action split mean neither character is fully optimized for output. You're getting two characters dealing 60% damage rather than one dealing 100%.

### Inventor
**Unstable** abilities can spike damage impressively (Megaton Strike, Explode), but on a failure the innovation breaks and needs repair. Too unreliable for consistent DPR. When it works, it's impressive. When it doesn't, you've wasted your turn.
